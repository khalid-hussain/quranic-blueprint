
# Translator's Preface {.unnumbered}

In the Name of Allāh, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful. All types of
thanks and praise belong to Allāh, the most worthy of thanks and praise.
A praise superior to all other types of praise, like the superiority of
Allāh over all of His creation. All praise belongs to Allāh, who bestows
understanding of the Dīn upon whom He wills from His servants and grants
success to those obedient to Him. I bear witness that nothing deserves
to be worshipped save Allāh alone, and I bear witness that Muḥammad is
his slave and Messenger. May Allāh bestow blessings upon him, his
family, and his companions as long as the clouds bring about rain and as
long as the thunder comes after the lightning.

This book in your hands is the work of my great-grandfather, Shaykh
Owais al-Nagrāmī al-Nadwī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}. It is the second book
of the Shaykh to be presented in the English language. The Urdu version
was written to teach the foundations of Islam to children, young adults,
and new Muslims. Many of the Shaykh's books were lost in manuscript
form, and barely a handful survived in print. Despite the negligence in
preserving his works, Shaykh's works continued to be read, and students
from across the world continued to benefit from them. This reminds me of
the words of Shaykh Muḥammad ʿAlī Ādam al-Ityūbī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"},
who said,

[يخلد ذكره بلا نهاية - مفخرة بين ذوي العناية]{dir="rtl"}

*Forever does Allāh keep alive their remembrance,*

*A glorious feat among the people of diligence.*

[تفاخر الناس بنسلهم غدا - وفخر أهل العلم بالكتب بدا]{dir="rtl"}

*For tomorrow, people shall boast about their offspring,*

*While within the books of the knowledgeable does their pride sing.*

[مؤلفاتهم بها الذكر ارتفع - وذكر ذي النسل بموته انقطع]{dir="rtl"}

*Through their works, their recognition takes rise,*

*While the mention of descendants ends with their demise.*

This book was translated at the direction of my grandmother, i.e., the
Shaykh's daughter, Sultana Fāṭima [رحمها الله]{dir="rtl"}. This was done
with the intention of increasing the flow of continuous rewards in the
Hereafter, preserving the work of the Shaykh.

What can you expect from this book? This book offers a structured and
comprehensive presentation of the core religious and moral teachings of
Islam for Muslim children. It is carefully developed from the Qurʾān and
authentic Sunnah, ensuring that every lesson is rooted in sound and
verified sources. Rather than presenting abstract concepts, the book
organizes essential Islamic values into clear, practical teachings
supported by complete aḥādīth and Qurʾānic guidance. Each topic is
explained with purpose, showing not only what Islam teaches but why it
matters in daily life.

The style is intentionally simple, accessible, and engaging, while
maintaining accuracy, depth, and scholarly reliability. Complex ethical
concepts are broken down into language suitable for children, without
compromising the integrity of the original sources. Through this
approach, the book aims to:

- Build a strong moral foundation rooted in revelation.

- Develop character alongside knowledge.

- Instill a sense of accountability before Allah.

- Encourage practical application of Islamic teachings in everyday life.

In essence, this is not merely a collection of lessons but a blueprint
for shaping faith, character, and conduct, preparing young Muslims to
live with clarity, purpose, and integrity.

The original book in Urdu spanned 150 pages, organized under 119
subheadings, and was authored during the Shaykh's tenure as Professor of
Tafsīr at Nadwat al-ʿUlamāʾ. It was written at the request of Thanāʾ
Allāh Raʾīs of Gorakhpur and published by Dār al-Muṣannifīn at his
expense.

In preparing this edition, we have undertaken a careful and methodical
refinement of the translation to enhance both clarity and scholarly
integrity. Relevant āthār have been incorporated to strengthen the
discussions and present a more comprehensive treatment of the issues
addressed. In recognition of the author's deep admiration for Shaykh
al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} and his student Imam Ibn
al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, we have included selected explanatory
statements from these two Imams where appropriate, thereby enriching the
reader's understanding and engagement with the text.

Particular attention has also been given to the history of previous
editions. This work has seen multiple publications in Urdu, including a
relatively recent edition prepared under the auspices of the Shaykh's
daughter, Sultana Fāṭima [رحمها الله]{dir="rtl"}, and my grandfather,
Dr. ʿAbdul ʿAleem Siddīqī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}. A close comparison of
these editions revealed inconsistencies in structure and flow.
Consequently, we have adopted the edition published by Dār
al-Muṣannifīn, Aʿẓamgarh as our base text, as it demonstrated the most
coherent arrangement and organization. All sources and references have
been rigorously verified, omissions have been rectified, and earlier
citation errors have been corrected.

In addition, key terms and definitions have been clarified and, where
necessary, expanded to ensure precision. Certain sections that were
previously incomplete have been supplemented to present a more complete
and cohesive work. Through these efforts, we aim to offer a translation
that is both faithful to the original and beneficial to the contemporary
reader, while maintaining due respect for the scholarly legacy upon
which it stands.
