
# About the Author

Shaykh Muḥammad Owais ibn Muḥammad Anīs ibn Muḥammad Idrīs ibn Ḥāfiẓ Sayyid ʿAbd
al-ʿAlī al-Nagrāmī al-Nadwī was born on 4 January 1914 in Nagrām, Lucknow, into
an illustrious family of scholars whose educational, religious, political, and
reformative services left a deep imprint upon Awadh (modern-day Uttar Pradesh in
India) and beyond.

His birth coincided with a moment of symbolic transition in Indian Islamic
scholarship. He was born in the very year that ʿAllāmah Shiblī Nuʿmānī (d.
1914), a radiant sun of knowledge, reform, and intellectual revival, passed
away. This coincidence recalls a familiar pattern in Islamic scholarly history:
just as Imam al-Shāfiʿī was born in the year Imam Abū Ḥanīfah passed away, so
too, with the setting of one great sun of learning, another quietly rose upon
the land of Nagrām.

Shaykh Abū al-ḤasanʿAlī Nadwī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} wrote, "In the thirteenth
century Hijrī, through Allah's special Mercy, a new wave of guidance and reform
appeared in this region. Among the many scholarly families of Awadh, there were
two noble lineages that remained firm upon Tawḥīd, adherence to the Sunnah, and
the revival of pure Islamic teachings. One was the family of Ḥaḍrat Sayyid Aḥmad
Shahīd [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, whose spiritual center was Takiyah Kalān, Rae
Bareilly (the family lineage of ʿAlī Miyan himself). The other was the family of
Ḥaḍrat Mawlānā Ḥāfiẓ ʿAbd al-ʿAlī Nagrāmī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, based in
Nagrām, Lucknow (known as the ʿUlamāʾ of Nagrām). Together, these two families
played a foundational role in shaping the religious and spiritual legacy of the
region." \[Purāne Charāgh\]

The Shaykh's great-grandfather, Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAlī ibn Pīr ʿAlī ibn Ghulām Imam
al-Hindī al-Nagrāmī, was born in the year 1131 AH (1719 CE). He authored and
compiled works, and studied the religious sciences under several scholars such
as his maternal uncle Sayyid Anwar ʿAlī al-Murādābādī, Shaykh Aḥmad al-Bakrī
al-Nagrāmī, Shaykh ʿAbd al-Ḥakīm ibn ʿAbd al-Rabb al-Kahawnawī, and many other
scholars. He studied the spiritual sciences with al-Qāḍī ʿAbd al-Karīm
al-Nagrāmī. He also received authorization (ijāzah) from Shaykh Bināʾ ʿAṭāʾ
al-Salūnī and Khwāja Aḥmad ibn Yāsīn al-Naṣīrābādī.

Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAlī was a man of piety, righteousness, chastity, and trust in
Allah. The people around him benefited from his good character even before they
benefited from his knowledge. Allah guided many people to accept Islam at his
hands. He authored numerous works, among the most famous of which is Tafsīr Āyāt
al-Aḥkām.

From an academic perspective, his Tafsīr Āyāt al-Aḥkām holds a distinguished
status. Its most notable feature is how the Qurʾānic āyahs are arranged
according to jurisprudential order. Previously, works on Aḥkām al-Qurʾān
followed the sūrah-based sequence, but in this book, legal chapters were first
established, followed by sections under which relevant āyahs were collected.
Then came the translation, followed by explanatory marginal notes. The legal
positions of the four Imams are also mentioned. Among the books written in India
on Aḥkām al-Qurʾān, this work holds significant importance. Accordingly, the
renowned Indian historian and scholar Mawlana Sayyid ʿAbd al-Ḥayy Ḥasanī [رحمة
الله]{dir="rtl"} mentioned it in al-Thaqāfah al-Islāmīyah fī'l-Hind. When Nawab
Ṣiddīq Ḥasan Khān [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} received this book, he wrote a letter
in Arabic to Ḥāfiẓ ʿAbd al-ʿAlī [رحمة الله]{dir="rtl"}, stating, after praising
his knowledge and scholarship, that although he himself had written a book on
Aḥkām al-Qurʾān, Ḥāfiẓ Ṣāḥib's work surpassed his own in scholarly merit. This
entire letter was published in the Maʿārif magazine.

Shaykh ʿAbd al-ʿAlī passed away on the night of Wednesday, the 6th of Shawwāl,
in the year 1296 AH (1782 CE). His son, Idrees ibn ʿAbd al-ʿAlī, performed his
funeral rites. May Allah have mercy on him.

The Shaykh's grandfather, Shaykh Muḥammad Idrīs Nagrāmī, was born on Monday,
11th of Shawwal 1275 AH (1859 CE), in Nagram, Lucknow. He was highly
distinguished in knowledge, virtue, righteousness, and piety. Besides studying
under his father, he acquired knowledge from the esteemed Shaykh ʿAbd al-Ḥayy
Farangī Maḥallī, Maulana ʿAbd al-Raḥmān Pānīpatī, and Shaykh ʿAbd al-Ḥaqq, the
author of Tafsīr Ḥaqqānī. In the spiritual sciences, he benefited from the
spiritual guide of the age, Maulana Faḍl al-Raḥmān of Ganj Muradabad and his
maternal uncle, the late 'Abd al-Salām ibn Abī al-Qāsim al-Husaynī al-Wāsiṭ.
Thus he attained a lofty rank in both the outward and inward sciences.

The Shaykh authored many works, and he's most renowned for his seminal
biographical work Ulamah-e-Haal, which resurrects the legacies of 423 pioneering
Islamic scholars from the Indian subcontinent of the 14th century till his time.
This work was first published in 1897 (1315 AH). He established and ran a Darul
Ifta in Nagram (Lucknow) decades before the very establishment of Nadwatul
Ulama. The Sheikh authored 60+ other works, including Ḥawashī on many books,
nearly all of which are lost today.

There were thousands of books in the family library, many of which were copied
meticulously by hand by Shaykh Idrīs. He spent 30 years doing this and many a
time had nothing to eat. His nights were like his days, where he was in the
constant company of books. Shaykh Idrīsestablished Matba-e-Nafeesi Publication
in Nagram to spread authentic teachings of Islam and rectify the wrong practices
that had crept into the lives of the people.

Shaykh Muḥammad ʿAlī Mungeri (the founder of Nadwatul ʿUlamāʾ ) personally
requested Shaykh Idrīs Nagrāmī to have his family become a part of the newly
born Dārul ʿUloom Nadwatul ʿUlamāʾ in Lucknow. Heeding this call, Shaykh Idrīs
enrolled his sons, Anīs and Nafīs Nagrāmī, at the institution. Shaykh Idrīs
passed away on the 10th of Ramaḍān in the year 1330 AH (23 August 1912 CE).

The late Maulana's father, Maulana Muḥammad Anīs Nagrāmī, was likewise a
well-known scholar who regularly undertook preaching and reformative tours
throughout the towns and villages of Awadh.

Shaykh Owais's father, Shaykh Muḥammad Anīs Nagrāmī, alongside figures such as
Shaykh Muḥammad Nafīs Nagrāmī, Shaykh Muḥammad Idrīs Nagrāmī, Shaykh ʿAbd
al-Raḥmān Nagrāmī, and Shaykh Ḥusayn Aḥmad Madanī, actively participated in
India's freedom struggle while simultaneously leading sustained efforts against
shirk, heretical beliefs, and corrupt social practices throughout Awadh. They
visited many remote areas in harsh conditions on foot and using bullock carts.
This kind of outreach is not something many of us can even imagine doing today.

In this way, the late Maulana inherited both the outward and inward sciences,
and the proclamations of "Qāla Allāh wa Qāla al-Rasūl" reached his ears even
before he came of age. As he advanced in life, he greatly enriched this
priceless inheritance and spread the name and work of his forefathers far and
wide.

Shaykh Owais began his early education in Nagrām. A praiseworthy trait observed
within the family of Shaykh Owais was that a son would first study the
foundational sciences and manners (ādāb) at the feet of his father before
proceeding to other teachers. Thus, we see Shaykh Owais learning from Shaykh
Anīs, Shaykh Anīs learning from Shaykh Idrīs, and likewise Shaykh Idrīs learning
from Shaykh Ḥāfiẓ Sayyid ʿAbd al-ʿAlī. May Allah have Mercy upon them all.

He later enrolled at Dār al-ʿUlūm Nadwat al-ʿUlamāʾ in 1927, where he studied
until 1932 under some of the most distinguished scholars of the age. After
completing his formal studies, he pursued advanced training in the Six Books of
Ḥadīth, with special emphasis on Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī and Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī, under
Shaykh Ḥusayn Aḥmad Madanī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, then principal of Dār
al-ʿUlūm Deoband.

The intellectual refinement and cultivation he acquired were also deeply shaped
by the environment of Nadwah itself. Furthermore, he had the opportunity to
benefit from the incomparable Sayyid al-Ṭāʾifah ʿAllāmah Sayyid Sulaymān Nadwī
[رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, whose discerning gaze could transform raw metal into
pure gold. Shaykh Sayyid Sulaiman immediately recognized his intelligence and
potential, took him under his special supervision, and brought him to Dār
al-Muṣannifīn.

In 1939, Shaykh Owais proceeded to Dār al-Muṣannifīn. During this period, he was
profoundly influenced by the works of Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه
الله]{dir="rtl"}, Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, and Shāh Walīullāh
al-Dehlawī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, an influence that would later become evident
in both his teaching and his writings. He assisted ʿAllāmah Sayyid Sulaymān
Nadwī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} in completing Sīrah al-Nabī, the monumental work
initiated by ʿAllāmah Shiblī Nuʿmānī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, and contributed to
the research and preparation of several other scholarly projects. He remained at
Dār al-Muṣannifīn for approximately seven years, teaching and writing. His
articles published in Maʿārif became a focal point of attention for scholars.
From the outset, his inclination was toward Qur'anic sciences, and most of his
writings revolved around the Qur'an and its teachings.

Upon returning to Lucknow, Shaykh Owais was appointed Shaykh al-Tafsīr at Dār
al-ʿUlūm Nadwat al-ʿUlamāʾ on the recommendation of ʿAllāmah Sayyid Sulaymān
Nadwī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, who was one of the leading scholars of his time,
the Qāḍī of Bhopal, and one of the founding members of Nadwat al-ʿUlamāʾ. He
held this position for nearly four decades, quietly shaping generations of
students through deep Qurʾānic insight, intellectual discipline, and spiritual
sobriety. He was married to the daughter of Shaykh Muḥammad Aṣghar Pratāpgarhī
[رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} and was blessed to perform Ḥajj in 1948, 1953, and 1964.

Despite being offered prestigious academic posts, including a teaching position
at the newly established Islamic University of Madinah, as well as appointments
in institutions in Peshawar and Calcutta, he declined them all, believing that
India was in greater need of his services. Instead, he sent his son, Prof. Dr.
Yūnus Nagrāmī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}, to Madinah with the first batch of Indian
students. Dr. Yūnus would later gain international recognition and become the
first Indian adviser to the Muslim World League, receiving the Presidential
Award of India in 1992 for his services to the Arabic language and literature.

Shaykh Owais toured and preached about the importance of Tawḥīd, warned against
innovations, and advocated for social reforms throughout his life in places like
Lucknow, Sultanpur, Rai Bareilly, Barabanki, and far-off villages, and made
arrangements for spreading the teachings of Islam in hundreds of homes.

Shaykh Owais was a colleague and close friend of Shaykh Abū al-Ḥasan ʿAlī Nadwī
[رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} for nearly twenty-five years. Shaykh ʿAlī writes about
him that, apart from Shaykh Owais's services to the Qurʾān, among his most
impactful contributions to daʿwah was a public tafsīr class held every Saturday
after Maghrib in Lucknow. This gathering attracted scholars, social elites,
government officials, and even British officers. Through it, Shaykh Owais
conveyed the Qurʾān's authentic message, warned against innovations, and
addressed intellectual doubts, sparking the emergence of multiple Qurʾānic study
circles across the city.

Shaykh ʿAbd al-Mājid Daryābādī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} wrote in his preface to
*How to Study the Qurʾān*, "Shaykh Owais is not merely a teacher of Tafsīr at
Dār al-ʿUlūm Nadwat al-ʿUlamāʾ with many years of experience in teaching the
Qurʾān; rather, in terms of taste and intuition, he is truly--- in the real
sense--- a scholar and student of Qurʾānic studies. His reading in this field is
extensive, and his insight, by the grace of Allah, is remarkably deep. In every
respect, he has devoted himself to the service of the Qurʾān. That is the bounty
of Allah; He grants it to whom He wills."

His writings, spanning Arabic, Urdu, and Persian, can be found in some of the
most prestigious libraries and universities around the world. Some of his
published works include:

- Tafsīr al-Qayyim (Arabic)

- Taʿlīm al-Qurʾān (Urdu)

- Al-ʿAqīdah al-Sunnīyah fī Sharḥ al-ʿAqīdah al-Ḥasanah (Arabic)

- Al-Khayr al-Kathīr fī Sharḥ al-Fawz al-Kabīr (Arabic)

- Rehnumāʾī ʿIlm al-Tafsīr (Persian)

- Fiqh al-Qurʾān (Arabic)

- Uṣūl al-Ḥadīth (Urdu)

- Qurʾān kā Muṭālaʿah Kaise? (Urdu)

- Taṣawwuf Kyā Hai? (Urdu)

Shaykh Owais compiled the scattered commentary on Quranic *āyāt* of Imam Ibn
al-Qayyim into one volume that was first published in 1949 in Egypt. This book
was widely welcomed and lauded by peers and scholars alike, such that it became
part of the curriculum for many institutions of learning across the world. He
embarked on doing the same for the works of Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyyah, but
could not complete it. Likewise, his other works on grammar (*naḥw*) and
rhetoric (*balāghah*) were also left incomplete because of his bad health.

In 1961, Shaykh Owais became a member of both the Editorial Board of the journal
Maʿārif and the Executive Committee of Dār al-Muṣannifīn. During his stay at Dār
al-Muṣannifīn, he authored numerous articles on Qurʾānic studies, including:

- Translations of the Qurʾān

- The Bible, the Qurʾān, and Ḥadīth

- The Reality of the Zindīq

- Ḥāfiẓ Jalāl al-Dīn al-Suyūṭī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}

- Kalimatullāh

- Imam Ibn Jarīr al-Ṭabarī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"}

- The Orientalist Nöldeke and the Qurʾān

- Some Observations on Tafsīr al-Rāzī

Sheikh Owais, as a teacher, had an effect on the lives of his students not only
by virtue of his knowledge but also because of his immaculate manners and
immense care towards nurturing them. Many of his students recall how his classes
used to be an arena of enlightenment and learning that everyone looked forward
to, as it was filled with Quranic gems and beautified with poetry.

A student recalls, "A distinctive habit of Shaykh Owais was that he would always
bring his own Qurʾān to class, unlike other teachers who would ask students for
the text. The margins of his Qurʾān were filled with carefully written notes and
insights. He believed that a successful teacher must prepare thoroughly,
reviewing the lesson, identifying key points, and highlighting important
passages, and he practiced this consistently."

Shaykh Owais was known for always motivating, pushing, supporting, and
empowering the youth to study and learn about Islam in any manner possible.
Despite the morning classes at the institution, Shaykh Owais also taught
students at his home after ʿAṣr and Maghrib prayers. He was also known to take
special care of orphans. All of this was done so that the students could excel
and carry the Prophetic legacy forward.

Some of his prominent students include Shaykh Rabiʿ Ḥasanī Nadwī (India), Shaykh
Saʿīd al-Raḥmān Aʿẓamī (India), Shaykh al-Sayyid Muḥammad al-Ḥasanī al-Nadwī
(India), Shaykh ʿAbdullāh al-Ḥasanī al-Nadwī (India), Shaykh Muḥammad Isrāʾīl
Nadwī Salafī (India), Dr. Ziyā Nadwī (Saudi Arabia), Shaykh Yūsuf Ṣāliḥ Karājeh
(Turkey), Shaykh Dr. Taqī al-Dīn al-Nadwī (UAE), Mufti Zafīruddīn Miftāḥī
(India), Sayyid Wāẓiḥ Rashīd Nadwī (India), Shaykh Zillur Raḥmān Nadwī (India),
Shaykh Muḥammad Shakīl ʿAzmī (Bahrain), Shaykh Shafīq Aḥmad Khān (India), Shaykh
Muṣṭafā Rifāʿī (India), Shaykh Sayyid Maḥmūd Madanī (India), Shaykh Muḥammad
Walī ar-Rahmānī, Shaykh Naẓr al-Ḥafīẓ al-Nadwī al-Azhari (India), Shaykh
Muḥammad Ḥasan Khān (India), Shaykh Ṭāhir Nadwī (Nepal), and Shaykh Professor
Salīm al-Raḥmān Khān (Japan), among many others across the world.

Shaykh Owais passed away on the first of Ramaḍān in the year 1396 AH (27 August
1976).. His body was washed by the esteemed Shaykh Manẓūr Nuʿmānī [رحمه
الله]{dir="rtl"}, and he was laid to rest in Lucknow. His janāzah was led by
Shaykh ʿAbd al-Ḥasan ʿAlī Nadwī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} on 28 August 1976. After
the burial, Shaykh Manẓūr Nuʿmānī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "It was because
of the Shaykh's stronghold on adhering and propagating the creed (*aqīdah*) of
Tawḥīd that his face was so relaxed and radiant when laid to rest. It did not
appear at all that he had endured such a long illness." (Kārvān-e-Zindagī)

May Allāh have Mercy on the Shaykh and his family, and elevate his ranks in
Jannah.
