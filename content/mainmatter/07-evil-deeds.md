
# Evil Deeds

## Immorality, Evil, and Transgression

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَيَنۡهَىٰ عَنِ ٱلۡفَحۡشَآءِ وَٱلۡمُنكَرِ وَٱلۡبَغۡيِۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And He forbids immorality, evil, and transgression. (Sūrat al-Naḥl, Q16:90)
\AYAH_END

In this _āyah_, three things are prohibited:

1. _Faḥshāʾ_ (Immorality)
2. _Munkar_ (Evil)
3. _Baghiyy_ (Transgression)

_Faḥshāʾ_ means exceeding the limits. Whatever boundaries Allāh has set,
crossing them is immorality, and this is disobedience to Allāh.

<!-- TODO ref Madarij Salikin Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Faḥshāʾ_ includes every act whose
ugliness is extreme, whether in speech or action." He further explained, "It is
called _faḥshāʾ_ because it exceeds the limits in its ugliness and corruption."
\[Madarij al-Salikin\]

_Munkar_ refers to actions that are generally disliked among people, such that the
one who commits them falls in the eyes of others. Human nature itself finds them
repugnant, and they must be avoided.

<!-- TODO ref Majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Munkar_ is everything
that Allāh has prohibited, and which the sound natural disposition (_fiṭrah_)
and intellect recognize as evil." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->
<!-- TODO check translit of baghiyy -->
<!-- TODO add the word baghiyy somewhere in the translation -->

_Baghiyy_ means excess and wrongdoing, which is also called oppression. The
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that Allāh says, "O My servants, I
have forbidden oppression for Myself and have made it forbidden among you, so do
not oppress one another." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref madarij salikin -->

Exceeding limits and attacking the rights of others is precisely what oppression
is. Imam Ibn al-Qayyim said, "Oppression is placing a thing in other than its
proper place, whether by excess, deficiency, or transgression beyond limits."
\[Madarij al-Salikin\]


## Open and Hidden Sins

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تَقۡرَبُواْ ٱلۡفَوَٰحِشَ مَا ظَهَرَ مِنۡهَا وَمَا بَطَنَۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And do not go near immoralities, whether open or hidden. (Sūrat al-Anʿām,
Q6:151)
\AYAH_END


Allāh has commanded us to avoid every sin, whether open or concealed. Open sins
include acts such as robbery, murder, and lying. Hidden sins include envy,
hatred, malice, and backbiting. A person must protect himself from all such
sins.

In this _āyah_, Allāh has also explained the method of avoiding sins: one should
stay away from the places and means through which sins arise. One should not
even go near them.

Just as one who avoids water does not drown, and one who avoids fire does not
burn, one who avoids the paths to sin avoids sin itself.

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "When Allāh prohibits something, He
prohibits all the ways and means that lead to it. This is from His mercy toward
His servants, for if He were to permit the means, it would inevitably lead them
to falling into the prohibited act itself." \[Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]


## Lying

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱجۡتَنِبُواْ قَوۡلَ ٱلزُّورِ ٣٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And avoid false speech. (Sūrat al-Ḥajj, Q22:30)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Lying is an extremely evil trait and must be avoided. The Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Truthfulness leads to righteousness, and righteousness
leads to Paradise. A man continues to speak the truth until he is recorded with
Allāh as truthful. And lying leads to wickedness, and wickedness leads to
Hellfire. A man continues to lie until he is recorded with Allāh as a liar."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} identified lying as a sign of hypocrisy. He
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The signs of a hypocrite are three: when he speaks, he
lies; when he promises, he breaks it; and when he is entrusted, he betrays."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Companions did not lie. If they committed a mistake, they would admit it
immediately. During the expedition of Tabūk, some people did not participate.
When the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} asked for the reason, the hypocrites
presented false excuses. However, Kaʿb ibn Mālik \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} openly
confessed his mistake and said that if he gave a false excuse, Allāh might cause
the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to be displeased with him, but if he spoke the
truth, he hoped for Allāh's forgiveness.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Kaʿb ibn Mālik \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "I said, 'O Messenger of Allāh, by
Allāh, if I were sitting before anyone else of the people of the world, I would
have avoided his anger with an excuse... but by Allāh, I know that if I were to
tell you a lie to please you, Allāh would soon make you angry with me. But if I
tell you the truth, I hope for Allāh's forgiveness...'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

Lying remains lying whether it is done in jest or to amuse children. People
commonly fall into this and do not consider it wrong, even though it has also
been prohibited. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Woe to the one
who lies in order to make people laugh. Woe to him! Woe to him!" \[Sunan Abī
Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿĀmir \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, a young Companion, said that once his
mother called him while the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was present in
their house. She said, "Come here, I will give you something." The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} asked, "Do you intend to give him something or not?" She
said, "I will give him dates." The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said,
"If you had not given him anything, a lie would have been recorded against you."
\[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref missing -->

Once, a female Companion asked whether it would be considered lying if someone
desired something but then said that they did not want it. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied that even the smallest lie is recorded as a lie.

<!-- TODO ref Madarij Salikin -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Truthfulness and lying are the
origin of righteousness and corruption. From truthfulness arise all forms of
good, and from lying arise all forms of evil." \[Madarij al-Salikin\]


## Oaths

Allāh Says,

<!-- TODO clarify translation -->

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تَجۡعَلُواْ ٱللَّهَ عُرۡضَةٗ لِّأَيۡمَٰنِكُمۡ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And do not make Allāh a target for your oaths. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:224)
\AYAH_END


Taking an oath is, in reality, a form of testimony. Swearing by Allāh over a
matter means making Allāh a witness to it. Making Allāh a witness in matters is
not a trivial issue. Therefore, one should swear only with careful
consideration. Allāh is displeased with those who swear unnecessarily and
repeatedly.

The Quran instructs that one should not obey a person who swears excessively and
is contemptible.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->
<!-- TODO Check arak tree translation -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever swears an oath to take
the right of a Muslim unjustly, Allāh will make the Fire obligatory for him and
forbid Paradise for him." A man said, "Even if it is something small, O
Messenger of Allāh?" He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Even if it is a twig of the
arāk tree." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Muwaṭṭāʾ -->

The Companions were extremely cautious about taking oaths. Once, ʿAbdullāh ibn
ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} sold a slave. Later it was discovered that the slave
had an illness. The case was presented before ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, who
wished to take an oath from Ibn ʿUmar \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY}, but he refused to
swear and instead took the slave back. \[al-Muwaṭṭaʾ\]

Allāh has commanded that oaths should not be used as a means of deceiving
people---by swearing falsely to make others believe what is untrue. The
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that whoever takes a false oath will
prepare his place in Hell.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The major sins are: associating partners with
Allāh, disobedience to parents, killing a soul, and false oaths (_al-yamīn
al-ghamūs_)." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Oaths are of the nature
of testimony, and swearing by Allāh is making Him a witness over what one says."
He also said, "False oaths (_al-yamīn al-ghamūs_) are among the gravest of major
sins because it combines lying with dishonoring the Name of Allāh." \[Majmūʿ
al-Fatāwā\]

Just as excessive oaths and false oaths are sinful, breaking an oath after
taking it and failing to fulfill it is also sinful. Allāh has commanded that
once oaths are firmly taken, they should not be broken.


## Betrayal (Khiyānah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ لَا تَخُونُواْ ٱللَّهَ وَٱلرَّسُولَ وَتَخُونُوٓاْ أَمَٰنَٰتِكُمۡ وَأَنتُمۡ تَعۡلَمُونَ ٢٧
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Do not betray Allāh and the Messenger, nor betray your trusts knowingly.
(Sūrat al-Anfāl, Q8:27)
\AYAH_END

This _āyah_ contains three commands:

1.  Do not betray Allāh;
2.  Do not betray the Messenger; and
3.  Do not betray one another.

Not betraying Allāh means that once we accept Him as our Lord and Master, we
must not act against His commands. We should do what pleases Him and avoid what
displeases Him. Acting otherwise constitutes betrayal of Allāh.

Not betraying the Messenger \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} means fulfilling the commands of
Allāh that have reached us through him and not turning away from the path he has
shown. Failing to do so constitutes betrayal of the Messenger.

<!-- TODO translation of "we return wealth incompletely" -->

Not betraying one another means that when a matter is entrusted to us and we
accept responsibility for it, we must not fall short in fulfilling it. Betrayal
includes situations where wealth is entrusted to us and we do not return it,
secrets are shared with us and we disclose them, advice is sought and we give
dishonest counsel, or when we return wealth incompletely. All such actions are
forms of betrayal and must be avoided.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The signs of a hypocrite are
three: when he speaks, he lies; when he makes a promise, he breaks it; and when
he is entrusted, he betrays." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} sought refuge from betrayal and would
say, "O Allāh, I seek refuge in You from hunger, for it is an evil companion,
and I seek refuge in You from betrayal, for it is an evil inner trait." \[Sunan
Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref missing -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} described betrayal as a sign of hypocrisy and said that a
Muslim is the brother of another Muslim and must not betray him.

<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Trust (_amānah_) is the
foundation of all obligations, and betrayal (_khiyānah_) is the foundation of
all corruption." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

## Shortfall in Weights and Measures

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَأَوۡفُواْ ٱلۡكَيۡلَ وَٱلۡمِيزَانَ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And give full measure and weight. (Sūrat al-Anʿām, Q6:152)
\AYAH_END

Reducing weights and measures is, in reality, usurping the rights of others.
Whoever increases the measure when taking, and reduces it when giving,
unlawfully takes possession of another's property. This, too, is a form of
theft.

The Quran has strongly emphasized avoiding such dishonesty. Cheating in weights
and measures removes blessing and prosperity. We observe that those who cheat in
this manner lose credibility in the marketplace, and their businesses are
ruined.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever cheats us is not from
us." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]


<!-- TODO ref Ibn Mājah -->
<!-- TODO translation: shortage in weights -->
<!-- TODO translation: cheat in measure and weight -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that shortage in weights and
measures leads to famine, just as the widespread practice of adultery leads to
the outbreak of plagues. The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "O
assembly of the Muhājirūn, there are five things with which you may be tested...
When people cheat in measure and weight, they are afflicted with years of
famine, severe hardship, and oppression from their rulers..." \[Sunan Ibn
Mājah\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn Mājah -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} warned that those who cheat in weighing and measuring are
committing actions due to which previous nations were destroyed. In the same
narration, he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "...and immorality (_zinā_) does not
become widespread among a people except that plagues and diseases spread among
them which were not present among those before them..." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Injustice in
transactions, such as cheating in weights and measures, is among the greatest
forms of oppression." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The foundation of transactions is
justice, and anything that departs from justice is oppression." \[Iʿlām
al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]


## Breaking Covenants (Naqḍ al-ʿAhd)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
ٱلَّذِينَ عَٰهَدتَّ مِنۡهُمۡ ثُمَّ يَنقُضُونَ عَهۡدَهُمۡ فِي كُلِّ مَرَّةٖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Those with whom you made a covenant, then they break their covenant every time.
(Sūrat al-Anfāl, Q8:56)
\AYAH_END


Treachery and deception mean reassuring someone by giving one's word and then
acting against them when an opportunity arises. The Quran has classified this
behavior as betrayal.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "For every traitor there will be
a banner on the Day of Resurrection, and it will be said, 'This is the betrayal
of so-and-so.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would advise his military commanders
not to commit treachery---that is, not to betray agreements made with enemies.
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

Once, Amīr Muʿāwiyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} made a treaty with the Romans for a
fixed period. As the end of the treaty approached, he moved his army near their
border so that the moment the treaty expired, an attack could be launched.
Seeing this, a Companion named ʿAmr ibn ʿAbasah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} rode out
calling loudly, "Allāhu Akbar! Allāhu Akbar! No treachery!"

Amīr Muʿāwiyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} called him and asked what the matter was. He
replied that he had heard the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} say, "When
a treaty is made with a people, it should not be tightened nor loosened until
its term ends, or it should be openly declared that the treaty has been
annulled."

Upon hearing this, Amīr Muʿāwiyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} withdrew his forces.
\[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Fulfilling covenants is
among the greatest obligations, and treachery (_ghadr_) is among the gravest
prohibitions." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

<!-- TODO ref Zaad Ma3aad -->
<!-- TODO ref Madarij Salikin -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
would never betray a covenant, even with his enemies, and he commanded
fulfillment of agreements in all circumstances." \[Zād al-Maʿād\] He further
said, "Treachery is a sign of corruption in the heart, just as fulfilling
covenants is a sign of righteousness." \[Madarij al-Salikin\]


## False Accusations (Buhtān)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا يَأۡتِينَ بِبُهۡتَٰنٖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And that they do not bring forth false accusations. (Sūrat al-Mumtaḥanah,
Q60:12)
\AYAH_END

<!-- TODO review translation of ff para -->

_Buhtān_ means deliberately declaring an innocent person to be guilty, or
attributing to someone a sin or wrongdoing that he does not possess. This is
also a form of lying. The Quran has classified it as betrayal.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->
<!-- TODO review translation of ff para -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever says about a believer
what is not in him, Allāh will make him dwell in the discharge of the people of
Hell until he retracts what he said." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Every Muslim is sacred to another Muslim: his
blood, his wealth, and his honor." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also said, "Whoever accuses his slave of _zinā_ while he is
innocent will be lashed on the Day of Resurrection, unless he is as he said."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The one who fabricates false accusations is sinful in the sight of Allāh, and
his testimony is not considered valid.

## Tale-Bearing (Namīmah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تُطِعۡ كُلَّ حَلَّافٖ مَّهِينٍ ١٠هَمَّازٖ مَّشَّآءِۭ بِنَمِيمٖ ١١
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And do not obey every habitual swearer, disgraced, a slanderer, one who goes
about spreading tales. (Sūrat al-Qalam, Q68:10--11)
\AYAH_END


The work of a tale-bearer is to convey true or false statements between two
people in order to incite them against one another. Such a person is extremely
disgraceful in the sight of Allāh. The Quran has prescribed an excellent remedy
to protect oneself from the mischief of tale-bearers, namely, not accepting
their words.

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

Once, the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Shall I not inform you of
the worst of people?" They said, "Yes, O Messenger of Allāh." He
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Those who go about spreading tales, causing separation
between loved ones, and seeking corruption for the innocent." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Once, the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} passed by two graves and said,

that is difficult to avoid. As for one of them, he used not to protect himself
from urine. As for the other, he used to go about spreading tale-bearing

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} instructed, "Let none of you convey to me
anything about my Companions, for I love to come out to you while my heart is

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A tale-bearer (_qattāt_) will not enter

<!-- TODO review translation -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Namīmah_ is conveying
the speech of people to one another with the intent of causing corruption

## Backbiting (Ghībah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ ٱجۡتَنِبُواْ كَثِيرٗا مِّنَ ٱلظَّنِّ إِنَّ بَعۡضَ ٱلظَّنِّ إِثۡمٞۖ وَلَا تَجَسَّسُواْ وَلَا يَغۡتَب بَّعۡضُكُم بَعۡضًاۚ أَيُحِبُّ أَحَدُكُمۡ أَن يَأۡكُلَ لَحۡمَ أَخِيهِ مَيۡتٗا فَكَرِهۡتُمُوهُۚ وَٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابٞ رَّحِيمٞ ١٢
\AYAH_AR_END

<!-- TODO Accepter or Acceptor? -->

\AYAH_START
O believers! Avoid many suspicions, (for) indeed, some suspicions are sinful.
And do not spy, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like to eat the flesh
of their dead brother? You would despise that! And fear Allāh. Surely Allāh is
(the) Accepter of Repentance, Most Merciful. (Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt, Q49:12)
\AYAH_END

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Do you know what backbiting is?"
They said, "Allāh and His Messenger know best."

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "It is to mention about your brother that which he
dislikes." It was said, "What if what I say about my brother is true?" He
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "If what you say about him is true, then you have

Backbiting is not limited to the tongue alone; it can also be committed through
gestures, actions, and expressions. For example, if a person is lame and someone
mimics his walk, that is also backbiting.

Allāh has described backbiting by saying, "Would any of you like to eat the
flesh of his dead brother?" From this, it is understood that backbiting is like

\[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

There are certain situations where mentioning another's faults becomes
permissible and is no longer sinful. These situations generally include:

- Preventing religious or moral harm;
- Seeking a legal verdict (_fatwá_);
- Warning others against someone's harm;
- When a person is widely known by a particular nickname, even if it contains a
  flaw, and he is no longer offended by it;
- Complaining about oppression to an authority; and
- Mentioning the wrongdoing of someone who commits sins openly.

Hereafter." \[al-Dāʾ wa al-Dawāʾ\]

## Arrogance (Kibr)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَٱهۡبِطۡ مِنۡهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا فَٱخۡرُجۡ إِنَّكَ مِنَ ٱلصَّٰغِرِينَ ١٣

When a person possesses some excellence and, because of it, considers others
inferior, disgraceful, or beneath himself, this is called arrogance and pride.
It is a reprehensible trait.

Shayṭān was expelled from Paradise because of arrogance. He considered himself
superior to Ādam and said, "I am better than him. You created him from clay, and
You created me from fire." Allāh disliked this arrogance and commanded, "Descend
from here; it is not for you to be arrogant here. So depart---indeed, you are
among the humiliated." (Sūrat al-Aʿrāf, Q7:13)

man likes his clothes to be good and his sandals to be good." He
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allāh is Beautiful and loves beauty. Arrogance is
rejecting the truth and looking down upon people." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also said, "Whoever loves that people stand up for him,
let him take his seat in the Fire." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

ground. A Bedouin present said, "What kind of sitting is this?" The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, "Allāh has made me a noble servant, not an arrogant
or tyrannical one." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

On another occasion, he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Shall I inform you who the

<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Arrogance is rejecting

al-Fatāwā\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim Fawa2id -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The origins of wrongdoing are

1. Arrogance: It is that which drove Iblīs to that which it drove him (i.e. to

2. Desire: It is that which caused Ādam to be expelled from the Paradise; and

## Envy (Ḥasad)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تَتَمَنَّوۡاْ مَا فَضَّلَ ٱللَّهُ بِهِۦ بَعۡضَكُمۡ عَلَىٰ بَعۡضٖۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And do not covet that by which Allāh has given some of you advantage over
others. (Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, Q4:32)
\AYAH_END

<!-- TODO review translation "emulation" -->
<!-- TODO footnote for difference? ghibtah and envy? -->

If Allāh grants someone honor or excellence in worldly or religious matters, and
upon seeing this, a desire arises in your heart to attain the same honor, this
is called emulation (_ghibṭah_). This is not a bad trait; rather, emulation in
religious matters is commendable.

However, if upon seeing these blessings, jealousy develops and one wishes that
the other person should lose that blessing, this is envy (_ḥasad_).

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Beware of envy, for envy
consumes good deeds just as fire consumes wood." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO add ghibtah? emulation? Why envy? -->
<!-- TODO review trans: spends it in truth? -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "There is no envy except in two cases: a
man whom Allāh has given wealth and he spends it in truth, and a man whom Allāh
has given wisdom and he judges by it and teaches it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO: ref ibn al-qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Envy is to wish for the removal of
Allāh's blessing from its possessor, and this is its first level. The second
level is to wish that harm and misfortune accompany him constantly, like a
shadow that follows its object. At this level, the envier wishes that the one he
envies remains ignorant, poor, weak, and cut off from Allāh. He wishes
deficiency in his religion. In reality, such a person hates what Allāh has
decreed and granted to His servants." \[Ḥasad: Dhamm al-Ḥasad wa Ahlīhi\]

<!-- TODO: ref ibn al-qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} also said,

> A protection from envy is giving charity and doing good as much as one is
> able. This has an amazing effect in repelling calamities, the evil eye, and
> the evil of envy. If there were nothing other than the experiences of past
> nations to prove this, it would suffice.

> The evil eye and envy do not come near the one who gives charity and does good
> to creation; and if they do reach him, they are light, and his end is
> praiseworthy. This is because gratitude protects blessings.

> The envier's heart does not find rest until the envied loses his blessing, and
> nothing protects blessings like gratitude. Just as nothing causes blessings to
> vanish like ingratitude." \[Miʾat Naṣīḥah\]


## Quarreling (Tanāzuʿ)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تَنَٰزَعُواْ فَتَفۡشَلُواْ وَتَذۡهَبَ رِيحُكُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And do not dispute, lest you lose courage and your strength depart. (Sūrat
al-Anfāl, Q8:46)
\AYAH_END


In this _āyah_, Allāh commands that you must not quarrel among yourselves.
Quarreling produces two consequences.

First: You become weak. Internal conflict weakens you, drains your strength, and
leaves you unable to stand firm against enemies. Your energy is consumed in
internal disputes, and your resolve collapses when facing others.

Second: When this happens, your influence disappears, your reputation
deteriorates, and your honor vanishes in the eyes of others.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A believer to another believer
is like a building, each part strengthening the other," and he interlaced his
fingers. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] Just as bricks of a wall join together to form a
strong fortress, the Muslim community is like a fortress whose strength lies in
each brick supporting the other. As long as the bricks remain connected, the
wall stands; once a brick slips from its place, the entire wall collapses.

<!-- TODO: ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO review trans: if even one suffers -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The example of the believers in their
mutual love, mercy, and compassion is like a body: when one limb suffers, the
whole body responds to it with sleeplessness and fever." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]
This is how Muslims should be---if even one suffers, all should feel the pain.


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO review translation -->
<!-- TODO trans: conceals a Muslim -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A Muslim is the brother of
another Muslim: he does not wrong him, nor abandon him. Whoever fulfills the
need of his brother, Allāh will fulfill his need. Whoever relieves a Muslim of a
hardship, Allāh will relieve him of a hardship on the Day of Resurrection.
Whoever conceals a Muslim, Allāh will conceal him on the Day of Resurrection."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->
<!-- TODO review translation: mirror of another believer -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A believer is the mirror of another believer, and a
believer is the brother of another believer. He protects him from loss and
guards him in his absence." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]


## Killing (Qatl)

Some people, overwhelmed by grief, hardship, or illness, take their own lives.

the lives of people due to enmity, greed for wealth, or other motives. All of

## Self-Destruction (Halākah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تُلۡقُواْ بِأَيۡدِيكُمۡ إِلَى ٱلتَّهۡلُكَةِ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And do not throw yourselves into destruction. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:195)
\AYAH_END


This _āyah_ shows that one must not engage in actions whose outcome is
destruction and ruin.

Some people misunderstand this _āyah_ to mean that one should never face danger or
hardship. This is incorrect because, at times, avoiding hardship and danger
itself leads to destruction and ruin.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

Abū Ayyūb al-Anṣārī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said,

> This _āyah_ was revealed about us, the Anṣār. When Allāh gave strength to Islam
> and its people became numerous, we said among ourselves secretly, without
> informing the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, "Our wealth has been lost,
> and Allāh has strengthened Islam and increased its supporters. If we were to
> stay behind and tend to our wealth, we could restore what we have lost." So
> Allāh revealed to His Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY},

<!-- TODO: The ff needs to be part of the block quote -->

\AYAH_AR_START
وَأَنفِقُواْ فِي سَبِيلِ ٱللَّهِ وَلَا تُلۡقُواْ بِأَيۡدِيكُمۡ إِلَى ٱلتَّهۡلُكَةِ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
'And spend in the way of Allāh and do not throw yourselves into destruction.'
(Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:195)
\AYAH_END

> So destruction was in staying behind from Jihād and occupying ourselves with
> our wealth and abandoning striving. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Tirmidhī -->
<!-- TODO review if the phrase in the brackets at the end; should there be brackets or not -->

It is reported that during a battle near Constantinople, a man charged alone
into the ranks of the enemy. People said, "Subḥān Allāh! He is throwing himself
into destruction!" Abū Ayyūb al-Anṣārī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "O people! You
interpret this _āyah_ incorrectly. It was revealed about us, the Anṣār..." (then
he mentioned the above explanation) \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Destruction (_tahlukah_)
is abandoning what Allāh has obligated, and this is more severe than facing
danger in obedience to Allāh." He also said, "Entering danger for the sake of
Allāh is not destruction; rather, destruction lies in abandoning His commands."
\[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]


## Laziness and Grief

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تَهِنُواْ وَلَا تَحۡزَنُواْ وَأَنتُمُ ٱلۡأَعۡلَوۡنَ إِن كُنتُم مُّؤۡمِنِينَ ١٣٩
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So do not weaken and do not grieve; and you will be superior if you are
believers. (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, Q3:139)
\AYAH_END


From this _āyah_, two lessons are learned.

First: A person should not be lazy. A lazy person can never succeed. One should
be active and hardworking and must not show laziness in any task. Allāh grants
success to the one who strives and works hard.

Second: If at any time hardship occurs or failure is faced in a matter, one
should not waste time in grief. Instead, one should consider that time valuable
and use it to make up for the loss.

The result of these two qualities is that if faith is sincere, success and
elevation will surely be attained.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} used to say, "O Allāh, I seek refuge in
You from anxiety and grief, from incapacity and laziness, from cowardice and
miserliness, and from the burden of debt and the oppression of men." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->
<!-- TODO review translation: do not be helpless -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The strong believer is better
and more beloved to Allāh than the weak believer, though there is good in both.
Be keen on what benefits you, seek help from Allāh, and do not be helpless. If
something befalls you, do not say, 'If only I had done such-and-such,' but say,
'_Qaddar Allāh wa mā shāʾa faʿal_ (Allāh has decreed, and whatever He wills, He
does),' for 'if' opens the door to Shayṭān." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->
<!-- TODO review translation: something pleasing? -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "How amazing is the affair of the
believer! All of his affairs are good, and this is not for anyone except the
believer. If something pleasing happens to him, he is grateful, and that is good
for him. If something harmful happens to him, he is patient, and that is good
for him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Knowledge and action are twins,
and their mother is high aspiration. Ignorance and inactivity are twins, and
their mother is laziness." \[al-Fawāʾid\]


## Mockery

\AYAH_AR_START

Muslims should not ridicule or mock one another. Times do not remain the same

## Spreading Corruption and Discord (Fitnah wa Fasād)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START

\AYAH_START
And do not roam about on the earth spreading corruption. (Sūrat al-Baqarah,

in the world and commands that mischief and corruption not be spread on the
earth.

discord, and spread immoral conduct. Instead, we should participate in righteous
deeds that establish true peace in the world and grant people honesty and
genuine tranquility.

nor reciprocating harm." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

## Reminding of One's Favors (Mann)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ لَا تُبۡطِلُواْ صَدَقَٰتِكُم بِٱلۡمَنِّ وَٱلۡأَذَىٰ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O you who believe! Do not invalidate your charities by reminders of generosity
and by causing harm. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:264)
\AYAH_END


Charity given in the path of Allāh must be done with a sincere intention. Its
purpose should not be to remind people of one's favor or to seek repayment for
the good deed.

When we do good to someone, it is done in one of two ways: either for the sake
of Allāh or for some personal motive.

If it is done for Allāh, then we have no favor over the servant, nor should we
expect anything in return. And if it is done for personal motives, then it no
longer remains true goodness.

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START

\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START

\AYAH_END

speak to on the Day of Resurrection, nor will He look at them, nor purify them,
and they will have a painful punishment: the one who lets his garment hang below
his ankles out of pride, the one who reminds others of what he has given, and
the one who sells his goods by false oaths." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO review translation: meeting and parting upon that -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Seven will be shaded by Allāh on

grew up in the worship of Allāh; a man whose heart is attached to the mosques;
two men who love each other for the sake of Allāh, meeting and parting upon
that; a man who is called by a woman of status and beauty and says, 'I fear
Allāh'; a man who gives charity and conceals it so that his left hand does not
know what his right hand has spent; and a man who remembers Allāh in solitude
and his eyes overflow with tears." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]


<!-- TODO ref majmu3 fatawa -->

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymīyah \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Reminding others of

harms the recipient and corrupts intention." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

<!-- TODO madaarij salikin -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The perfection of giving is that
the giver does not see himself as having any favor over the one he gives."
\[Madarij al-Salikin\]


## Miserliness (Bukhl)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ مَن كَانَ مُخۡتَالٗا فَخُورًا ٣٦ٱلَّذِينَ يَبۡخَلُونَ وَيَأۡمُرُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ بِٱلۡبُخۡلِ

miserly and command others to be miserly. (Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, Q4:36--37)
\AYAH_END


When a person possesses wealth but does not spend it in its proper places, this
is miserliness. It is such a corrupt trait that many other evils---such as
betrayal, dishonesty, lack of compassion, greed, and similar moral
faults---arise from it.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} used to supplicate, "O Allāh, I seek refuge in
You from miserliness, from cowardice, from being returned to the most decrepit
old age, from the trial of this world, and from the punishment of the grave."

## Intoxicants, Gambling, Divining Arrows, and Sacrificial Stones

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Intoxicants, gambling, idols, and drawing lots for decisions are
all evil of Shayṭān's handiwork. So shun them so you may be successful. (Sūrat
al-Māʾidah, Q5:90)
\AYAH_END

<!-- O you who believe! Indeed, intoxicants, gambling, sacrificial stones, and

<!-- TODO translation: unbefitting for a Muslim → unbefitting a Muslim -->

In this _āyah_, four things are prohibited as satanic practices, unbefitting for
a Muslim.

<!-- TODO trans: In intoxication, people commit acts they would never do while sober. -->
<!-- Intoxicated, people commit acts... -->
<!-- When intoxicated, people commit acts... -->

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->
<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

1.  Intoxicants (Alcohol): Alcohol affects the intellect and ruins health and
    wealth. In intoxication, people commit acts they would never do while sober.
    Many murders and fights occur because of it, and it causes negligence toward
    religious and worldly duties. In a _ḥadīth_, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
    said, "The adulterer does not commit adultery while he is a believer, the
    thief does not steal while he is a believer, and the one who drinks alcohol
    does not drink it while he is a believer." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] . He
    \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also said, "From among the signs of the Hour is that
    knowledge will be taken away, ignorance will prevail, alcohol will be drunk,
    and fornication will become widespread." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

    The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allāh has cursed alcohol, the
    one who drinks it, the one who pours it, the one who sells it, the one who
    buys it, the one who squeezes it, the one for whom it is squeezed, the one
    who carries it, and the one to whom it is carried." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

2.  Gambling: This too is a cause of wasted wealth, destruction of religion and
    worldly life, and moral corruption. Just as alcohol makes a person heedless
    of religion and life, gambling also makes one oblivious to profit and loss.
    A gambler's life becomes one of failure and loss. Countless respectable
    families have been ruined because of it; hence, Islam has prohibited it.

    The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever says to his
    companion, 'Come, let me gamble with you,' must give charity." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
    al-Bukhārī\]

3.  Divining Arrows: This is also a form of gambling. Among the Arabs, camels
    were the greatest wealth, so they gambled with them. They would slaughter
    camels and divide the meat into ten portions, then cast lots using arrows.
    Ten arrows were designated with different shares; they were placed in a bag
    and drawn by an impartial person. Those whose arrows had shares succeeded,
    while those whose arrows had no shares failed. The collected meat was then
    distributed among the poor, the needy, and friends. Because this was
    ostentatious and deceptive generosity, Islam commanded Muslims to avoid it.

4.  Sacrificial Stones (_Anṣāb_): Before Islam, among the objects worshipped
    besides Allāh were sacrificial stones---unshaped stones erected for
    offerings and animal sacrifices. This was a polytheistic practice;
    therefore, Muslims were commanded to keep away from it.

    The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allāh has cursed the one who
    sacrifices for other than Allāh." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

may live lives of honor and purity. The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
said, "No man is alone with a woman except that Shayṭān is the third of them."
\[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also said that adultery leads to poverty and destitution.

## Begging Persistently

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
لَا يَسۡـَٔلُونَ ٱلنَّاسَ إِلۡحَافٗاۗ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
They do not beg people persistently. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:273)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO review translation: not a single piece of flesh on his face. -->

Begging is considered extremely reprehensible in Islam. The Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever persists in begging will come on the Day of
Judgment with not a single piece of flesh on his face." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever seeks to be self-sufficient, Allāh
will make him self-sufficient; and whoever seeks independence, Allāh will grant
him independence; and whoever is patient, Allāh will grant him patience. No one
is given a gift better and more expansive than patience." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Such was the state of the Companions that even if a whip fell from their hands
to the ground, they would not ask anyone to pick it up for them.

<!-- TODO ref missing -->
<!-- TODO ref missing -->

If a person is compelled to ask, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that he
should ask righteous people, for there is hope that they will fulfill the
request with gentleness, honor, and good intention. He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said
that begging is not permissible for a strong, capable, healthy person.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} further said, "By the One in whose hand is my soul, it is
better for one of you to take his rope, gather firewood, carry it on his back,
and sell it, than to ask a man who may give him or withhold from him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhāri\]

<!-- TODO def Abū Dāwūd -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said to a man who came asking, "Do you
have anything in your house?" He said, "Yes, a piece of cloth, part of it we
wear and part we spread, and a bowl from which we drink water." He said, "Bring
them to me." He brought them, and the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} took
them in his hand and said, "Who will buy these?"

A man said, "I will buy them for one dirham." He said, "Who will offer more?"
twice or thrice. A man said, "I will take them for two dirhams." He gave them to
him and took the two dirhams, then gave them to the man and said, "Buy food with
one dirham for your family, and buy an axe with the other." Then he said, "Go
and gather firewood and sell it, and do not let me see you for fifteen days." He
did so, then came back having earned ten dirhams.

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} then said, "This is better for you
than begging, which will come as a mark on your face on the Day of
Resurrection." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]
