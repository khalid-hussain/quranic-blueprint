
# Evil Deeds

## Immorality, Evil, and Transgression

Allah Says,

[وَيَنْهَىٰ عَنِ الْفَحْشَاءِ وَالْمُنكَرِ وَالْبَغْيِ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And He forbids immorality, evil, and transgression."* \[Sūrat al-Naḥl,
90\]

In this āyah, three things are prohibited:

1.  Faḥshā' (Immorality)

2.  Munkar (Evil)

3.  Baghy (Transgression)

Faḥshā' means exceeding the limits. Whatever boundaries Allah has set,
crossing them is immorality, and this is disobedience to Allah.

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Faḥshā' includes every
act whose ugliness is extreme, whether in speech or action." He further
explained, "It is called faḥshā' because it exceeds the limits in its
ugliness and corruption." \[Madarij al-Salikin\]

Munkar refers to actions that are generally disliked among people, such
that the one who commits them falls in the eyes of others. Human nature
itself finds them repugnant, and they must be avoided.

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Al-Munkar is
everything that Allah has prohibited, and which the sound natural
disposition (fiṭrah) and intellect recognize as evil." \[Majmūʿ
al-Fatāwā\]

Baghy means excess and wrongdoing, which is also called oppression. The
Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that Allah says, "O My servants,
I have forbidden oppression for Myself and have made it forbidden among
you, so do not oppress one another." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Exceeding limits and attacking the rights of others is precisely what
oppression is. Imam Ibn al-Qayyim said, "Oppression is placing a thing
in other than its proper place, whether by excess, deficiency, or
transgression beyond limits." \[Madarij al-Salikin\]

## Open and Hidden Sins

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَقْرَبُوا الْفَوَاحِشَ مَا ظَهَرَ مِنْهَا وَمَا بَطَنَ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not go near immoralities, whether open or hidden."* \[Sūrat
al-Anʿām, 151\]

Allah has commanded us to avoid every sin, whether open or concealed.
Open sins include acts such as robbery, murder, and lying. Hidden sins
include envy, hatred, malice, and backbiting. A person must protect
himself from all such sins.

In this āyah, Allah has also explained the method of avoiding sins: one
should stay away from the places and means through which sins arise. One
should not even go near them.

Just as one who avoids water does not drown, and one who avoids fire
does not burn, one who avoids the paths to sin avoids sin itself.

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "When Allah prohibits
something, He prohibits all the ways and means that lead to it. This is
from His mercy toward His servants, for if He were to permit the means,
it would inevitably lead them to falling into the prohibited act
itself." \[Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]

## Lying

Allah Says,

[وَاجْتَنِبُوا قَوْلَ الزُّورِ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And avoid false speech."* \[Sūrat al-Ḥajj, 30\]

Lying is an extremely evil trait and must be avoided. The Messenger of
Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Truthfulness leads to righteousness, and
righteousness leads to Paradise. A man continues to speak the truth
until he is recorded with Allah as truthful. And lying leads to
wickedness, and wickedness leads to Hellfire. A man continues to lie
until he is recorded with Allah as a liar." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} identified lying as a sign of hypocrisy. He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The signs of a hypocrite are three: when he
speaks, he lies; when he promises, he breaks it; and when he is
entrusted, he betrays." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Companions did not lie. If they committed a mistake, they would
admit it immediately. During the expedition of Tabūk, some people did
not participate. When the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} asked for the reason,
the hypocrites presented false excuses. However, Kaʿb ibn Mālik [رضي
الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} openly confessed his mistake and said that if he
gave a false excuse, Allah might cause the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} to be
displeased with him, but if he spoke the truth, he hoped for Allah's
forgiveness.

Ka\'b ibn Malik [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported: "I said, 'O
Messenger of Allah, by Allah, if I were sitting before anyone else of
the people of the world, I would have avoided his anger with an
excuse... but by Allah, I know that if I were to tell you a lie to
please you, Allah would soon make you angry with me. But if I tell you
the truth, I hope for Allah's forgiveness...'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Lying remains lying whether it is done in jest or to amuse children.
People commonly fall into this and do not consider it wrong, even though
it has also been prohibited. The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
"Woe to the one who lies in order to make people laugh. Woe to him! Woe
to him!" \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿĀmir [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} , a young Companion, said
that once his mother called him while the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was present in their house. She said, "Come here, I will
give you something." The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} asked, "Do you intend to
give him something or not?" She said, "I will give him dates." The
Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "If you had not given him
anything, a lie would have been recorded against you." \[Sunan Abī
Dāwūd\]

Once, a female Companion asked whether it would be considered lying if
someone desired something but then said that they did not want it. The
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} replied that even the smallest lie is recorded as
a lie.

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Truthfulness and lying
are the origin of righteousness and corruption. From truthfulness arise
all forms of good, and from lying arise all forms of evil." \[Madarij
al-Salikin\]

## Oaths

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَجْعَلُوا اللَّهَ عُرْضَةً لِأَيْمَانِكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not make Allah a target for your oaths."* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah,
224\]

Taking an oath is, in reality, a form of testimony. Swearing by Allah
over a matter means making Allah a witness to it. Making Allah a witness
in matters is not a trivial issue. Therefore, one should swear only with
careful consideration. Allah is displeased with those who swear
unnecessarily and repeatedly.

The Qur'an instructs that one should not obey a person who swears
excessively and is contemptible.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever swears an oath to
take the right of a Muslim unjustly, Allah will make the Fire obligatory
for him and forbid Paradise for him." A man said, "Even if it is
something small, O Messenger of Allah?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Even if
it is a twig of the arāk tree." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Companions were extremely cautious about taking oaths. Once,
ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} sold a slave. Later it was
discovered that the slave had an illness. The case was presented before
ʿUthmān [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}, who wished to take an oath from Ibn
ʿUmar [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}, but he refused to swear and instead
took the slave back. \[al-Muwaṭṭaʾ\]

Allah has commanded that oaths should not be used as a means of
deceiving people---by swearing falsely to make others believe what is
untrue. The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that whoever takes a
false oath will prepare his place in Hell.

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The major sins are: associating partners with
Allah, disobedience to parents, killing a soul, and the false oath
(al-yamīn al-ghamūs)." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Oaths are of
the nature of testimony, and swearing by Allah is making Him a witness
over what one says." He also said, "The false oath (al-yamīn al-ghamūs)
is among the gravest of major sins because it combines lying with
dishonoring the Name of Allah." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Just as excessive oaths and false oaths are sinful, breaking an oath
after taking it and failing to fulfill it is also sinful. Allah has
commanded that once oaths are firmly taken, they should not be broken.

## Betrayal (Khiyānah)

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَخُونُوا اللَّهَ وَالرَّسُولَ وَتَخُونُوا أَمَانَاتِكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"O believers! Do not betray Allah and the Messenger, nor betray your
trusts."* \[Sūrat al-Anfāl, 8:27\]

This āyah contains three commands:

1.  Do not betray Allah.

2.  Do not betray the Messenger.

3.  Do not betray one another.

Not betraying Allah means that once we accept Him as our Lord and
Master, we must not act against His commands. We should do what pleases
Him and avoid what displeases Him. Acting otherwise constitutes betrayal
of Allah.

Not betraying the Messenger [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} means fulfilling the commands
of Allah that have reached us through him and not turning away from the
path he has shown. Failing to do so constitutes betrayal of the
Messenger.

Not betraying one another means that when a matter is entrusted to us
and we accept responsibility for it, we must not fall short in
fulfilling it. Betrayal includes situations where wealth is entrusted to
us and we do not return it, secrets are shared with us and we disclose
them, advice is sought and we give dishonest counsel, or when we return
wealth incompletely. All such actions are forms of betrayal and must be
avoided.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The signs of a hypocrite are
three: when he speaks, he lies; when he makes a promise, he breaks it;
and when he is entrusted, he betrays." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} sought refuge from betrayal and
would say, "O Allah, I seek refuge in You from hunger, for it is an evil
companion, and I seek refuge in You from betrayal, for it is an evil
inner trait." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} described betrayal as a sign of hypocrisy and said
that a Muslim is the brother of another Muslim and must not betray him.

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Trust
(amānah) is the foundation of all obligations, and betrayal (khiyānah)
is the foundation of all corruption." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

## Shortfall in Weights and Measures

Allah Says,

[وَأَوْفُوا الْكَيْلَ وَالْمِيزَانَ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And give full measure and weight."* \[Sūrat al-Anʿām, 152\]

Reducing weights and measures is, in reality, usurping the rights of
others. Whoever increases the measure when taking and reduces it when
giving unlawfully takes possession of another's property. This, too, is
a form of theft.

The Qur'an has strongly emphasized avoiding such dishonesty. Cheating in
weights and measures removes blessing and prosperity. We observe that
those who cheat in this manner lose credibility in the marketplace, and
their businesses are ruined.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever cheats us is not from
us." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that shortage in weights and
measures leads to famine, just as the widespread practice of adultery
leads to the outbreak of plagues. The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
said, "O assembly of the Muhājirīn, there are five things with which you
may be tested... When people cheat in measure and weight, they are
afflicted with years of famine, severe hardship, and oppression from
their rulers..." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} warned those who cheat in weighing and measuring that
they are committing actions due to which previous nations were
destroyed. In the same narration, he [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "...and
immorality (zinā) does not become widespread among a people except that
plagues and diseases spread among them which were not present among
those before them..." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Injustice in
transactions, such as cheating in weights and measures, is among the
greatest forms of oppression." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The foundation of
transactions is justice, and anything that departs from justice is
oppression." \[Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]

## Breaking Covenants (Naqḍ al-ʿAhd)

Allah Says,

[الَّذِينَ عَاهَدتَّ مِنْهُمْ ثُمَّ يَنقُضُونَ عَهْدَهُمْ فِي كُلِّ مَرَّةٍ]{dir="rtl"}

*"Those with whom you made a covenant, then they break their covenant
every time."* \[Sūrat al-Anfāl, 56\]

Treachery and deception mean reassuring someone by giving one's word and
then acting against them when an opportunity arises. The Qur'an has
classified this behavior as betrayal.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "For every traitor there will
be a banner on the Day of Resurrection, and it will be said: 'This is
the betrayal of so-and-so.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} would advise his military
commanders not to commit treachery---that is, not to betray agreements
made with enemies. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Once, Amīr Muʿāwiyah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} made a treaty with the
Romans for a fixed period. As the end of the treaty approached, he moved
his army near their border so that the moment the treaty expired, an
attack could be launched. Seeing this, a Companion named ʿAmr ibn
ʿAbasah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} rode out calling loudly, "Allāhu
Akbar! Allāhu Akbar! No treachery!"

Amīr Muʿāwiyah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} called him and asked what the
matter was. He replied that he had heard the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} say, "When a treaty is made with a people, it should not
be tightened nor loosened until its term ends, or it should be openly
declared that the treaty has been annulled."

Upon hearing this, Amīr Muʿāwiyah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} withdrew his
forces. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Fulfilling
covenants is among the greatest obligations, and treachery (ghadr) is
among the gravest prohibitions." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} would never betray a covenant, even with his enemies, and
he commanded fulfillment of agreements in all circumstances." \[Zād
al-Maʿād\] He further said, "Treachery is a sign of corruption in the
heart, just as fulfilling covenants is a sign of righteousness."
\[Madarij al-Salikin\]

## False Accusation (Buhtān)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا يَأْتِينَ بِبُهْتَانٍ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And that they do not bring forth false accusations."* \[Sūrat
al-Mumtaḥanah, 12\]

Buhtān means deliberately declaring an innocent person to be guilty, or
attributing to someone a sin or wrongdoing that he does not possess.
This is also a form of lying. The Qur'an has classified it as betrayal.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever says about a believer
what is not in him, Allah will make him dwell in the discharge of the
people of Hell until he retracts what he said." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Every Muslim is sacred to another Muslim: his
blood, his wealth, and his honor." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "Whoever accuses his slave of zina while he
is innocent will be lashed on the Day of Resurrection, unless he is as
he said." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The one who fabricates false accusations is sinful in the sight of
Allah, and his testimony is not considered valid.

Accusing chaste women is an assault on their honor. Therefore, its
punishment is that whoever does so and fails to produce the legally
required witnesses shall be flogged. Allah Says,

[وَالَّذِينَ يَرْمُونَ الْمُحْصَنَاتِ ثُمَّ لَمْ يَأْتُوا بِأَرْبَعَةِ شُهَدَاءَ فَاجْلِدُوهُمْ ثَمَانِينَ جَلْدَةً وَلَا
تَقْبَلُوا لَهُمْ شَهَادَةً أَبَدًا]{dir="rtl"}

"*Those who accuse chaste women and do not produce four witnesses---flog
them with eighty lashes and never accept their testimony."* \[Sūrat
al-Nūr, 4\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Avoid the seven destructive
sins." They said, "O Messenger of Allah, what are they?" He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Associating partners with Allah, sorcery, killing
a soul which Allah has forbidden except by right, consuming usury,
consuming the wealth of an orphan, fleeing from the battlefield, and
accusing chaste, believing, unsuspecting women." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Slandering
the innocent is among the gravest forms of injustice and falsehood."
\[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Buhtān is to attribute
to a person what he is free from, and it is more severe than lying."
\[Madarij al-Salikin\]

## Tale-Bearing (Namīmah)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تُطِعْ كُلَّ حَلَّافٍ مَّهِينٍ ۝ هَمَّازٍ مَّشَّاءٍ بِنَمِيمٍ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not obey every habitual swearer, disgraced, a slanderer, one
who goes about spreading tales."* \[Sūrat al-Qalam, 10-11\]

The work of a tale-bearer is to convey true or false statements between
two people in order to incite them against one another. Such a person is
extremely disgraceful in the sight of Allah. The Qur'an has prescribed
an excellent remedy to protect oneself from the mischief of
tale-bearers, namely, not accepting their words.

Once, the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Shall I not inform
you of the worst of people?" They said, "Yes, O Messenger of Allah." He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Those who go about spreading tales, causing
separation between loved ones, and seeking corruption for the innocent."
\[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Once, the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} passed by two graves and
said, "Indeed, they are being punished, and they are not being punished
for something that is difficult to avoid. As for one of them, he used
not to protect himself from urine. As for the other, he used to go about
spreading tale-bearing (namīmah)." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} instructed, "Let none of you convey to me
anything about my Companions, for I love to come out to you while my
heart is free from ill feeling." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A tale-bearer (qattāt) will not enter
Paradise." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Namīmah is
conveying the speech of people to one another with the intent of causing
corruption between them." He also said, "This is among the gravest
causes of corruption between people and the severing of relationships."
\[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Namīmah is among the
most destructive diseases of the heart and the greatest causes of enmity
between people." He further said, "The tale-bearer corrupts hearts,
destroys relationships, and spreads hatred among people." \[al-Dāʾ wa
al-Dawāʾ\]

## Backbiting (Ghībah)

Allah Says,

[يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ ٱجْتَنِبُوا۟ كَثِيرًۭا مِّنَ ٱلظَّنِّ إِنَّ بَعْضَ ٱلظَّنِّ إِثْمٌۭ ۖ وَلَا تَجَسَّسُوا۟
وَلَا يَغْتَب بَّعْضُكُم بَعْضًا ۚ أَيُحِبُّ أَحَدُكُمْ أَن يَأْكُلَ لَحْمَ أَخِيهِ مَيْتًۭا فَكَرِهْتُمُوهُ ۚ وَٱتَّقُوا۟
ٱللَّهَ ۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ تَوَّابٌۭ رَّحِيمٌۭ]{dir="rtl"}

*"O believers! Avoid many suspicions, (for) indeed, some suspicions are
sinful. And do not spy, nor backbite one another. Would any of you like
to eat the flesh of their dead brother? You would despise that!1 And
fear Allah. Surely Allah is (the) Accepter of Repentance, Most
Merciful."* \[Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt, 12\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Do you know what backbiting
is?" They said, "Allah and His Messenger know best."

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "It is to mention about your brother that which
he dislikes." It was said, "What if what I say about my brother is
true?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "If what you say about him is true, then
you have backbitten him; and if it is not true, then you have slandered
him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Backbiting is not limited to the tongue alone; it can also be committed
through gestures, actions, and expressions. For example, if a person is
lame and someone mimics his walk, that is also backbiting.

Aisha bint Abu Bakr [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} said, "I said to the
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, 'It is enough for you about Ṣafiyyah that she is
such-and-such'---meaning that she was short. He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
'You have said a word which, if it were mixed with the water of the sea,
it would have polluted it.' She said, 'I also imitated a person in front
of him.' He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, 'I do not like that I should imitate
someone even if I were given such-and-such.'" \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Allah has described backbiting by saying, "Would any of you like to eat
the flesh of his dead brother?" From this, it is understood that
backbiting is like eating the flesh of one's dead brother.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "When I was taken up (on the
Night Journey), I passed by people who had copper nails with which they
were scratching their faces and chests. I said: 'Who are these, O
Jibrīl?' He said: 'These are the ones who used to eat the flesh of
people and dishonor them.'" \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

There are certain situations where mentioning another's faults becomes
permissible and is no longer sinful. These situations generally include:

- Preventing religious or moral harm.

- Seeking a legal verdict (fatwā).

- Warning others against someone's harm.

- When a person is widely known by a particular nickname, even if it
  contains a flaw, and he is no longer offended by it.

- Complaining about oppression to an authority.

- Mentioning the wrongdoing of someone who commits sins openly.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "What do you say about a
person who does not refrain from people?" They said, "Allah and His
Messenger know best." He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Mention what is in him so
that people may beware of him." \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Help your brother whether he is an oppressor or
oppressed." They said, "O Messenger of Allah, we help him if he is
oppressed, but how do we help him if he is an oppressor?" He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "By preventing him from oppression." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Backbiting
is mentioning a person in a way he dislikes, even if it is true." He
also said, "If mentioning a person contains a legitimate benefit, such
as warning or seeking justice, then it is permitted." \[Majmūʿ
al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Backbiting harms honor,
and harm to honor is more severe than harm to wealth." He further said,
"The tongue is the greatest cause of destruction for a person in both
this world and the Hereafter." \[al-Dāʾ wa al-Dawāʾ\]

## Spying (Tajassus)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَجَسَّسُوا]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not spy."* \[Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt, 12\]

Investigating and prying into people's private matters is a serious
moral fault and must be avoided.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "O you who have believed
with your tongues while faith has not yet entered your hearts! Do not
harm the Muslims, nor shame them, nor seek out their faults. For whoever
seeks out the faults of his Muslim brother, Allah will seek out his
faults; and whoever Allah seeks out his faults, He will disgrace him,
even if he is inside his home." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "Whoever listens to the
conversation of a people while they dislike that or flee from him,
molten lead will be poured into his ears on the Day of Resurrection."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

It is worth reflecting why one should unnecessarily pry into the
conversations and private matters of others. Are one's own affairs not
sufficient that one interferes in the affairs of others? After the
commands of Allah and His Messenger [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, no Muslim should
commit such an error.

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The believer
conceals and advises, whereas the hypocrite searches for faults and
exposes." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever busies himself
with the faults of others will be tested with his own faults."
\[al-Fawāʾid\]

## Suspicion (Sūʾ al-Ẓann)

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا اجْتَنِبُوا كَثِيرًا مِّنَ الظَّنِّ إِنَّ بَعْضَ الظَّنِّ إِثْمٌ]{dir="rtl"}

*"O believers! Avoid much suspicion; indeed some suspicion is sin."*
\[Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt, 12\]

Entertaining baseless negative assumptions about someone's intentions is
called suspicion. Because of it, mutual affection decreases, people
begin to view one another with doubt, and gradually suspicion takes root
in hearts, creating fertile ground for conflict.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Beware of suspicion, for
suspicion is the most false of speech. Do not spy, do not compete with
one another, do not envy one another, do not hate one another, and do
not turn away from one another. Be, O servants of Allah, brothers."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

It is also appropriate that if a person does something that may give
others an opportunity for suspicion, he should remove that suspicion.

Safiyyah bint Huyayy [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} reported, "The Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was in iʿtikāf, and I came to visit him at night. I spoke
with him, then I stood to return, and he stood with me to accompany me.
When we reached the door of the mosque, two men from the Anṣār passed
by. When they saw the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, they hastened away. The
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, 'Take it easy, she is Ṣafiyyah bint
Ḥuyayy.'

They said, 'Subḥān Allāh, O Messenger of Allah!' He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
'Indeed, Shayṭān flows through the son of Ādam like blood, and I feared
that he might cast something into your hearts.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The conclusion is that the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} removed even the
possibility of suspicion.

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The believer
interprets the words and actions of people in the best possible way,
while the hypocrite follows suspicion and bad assumptions." He also
said, "Suspicion without evidence is forbidden, for it is speaking about
others without knowledge." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The foundation of
brotherhood is a sound heart, and it is destroyed by suspicion and ill
assumptions." \[Madarij al-Salikin\]

## Greed (Ḥirṣ)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَمُدَّنَّ عَيْنَيْكَ إِلَىٰ مَا مَتَّعْنَا بِهِ أَزْوَاجًا مِّنْهُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not extend your eyes toward what We have given some of them to
enjoy."* \[Sūrat Ṭā Hā, 131\]

From this āyah, it is understood that it is not befitting for a Muslim
to give his life for the outward adornments of this world and, in
pursuit of them, ruin his religion and faith. Whatever Allah has given
should be accepted with patience and contentment. Desiring excess is
futile. This state is called self-sufficiency (qanāʿah).

True self-sufficiency means being independent of all except Allah, not
extending one's hand before others, and being satisfied with whatever
little one has received.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Richness is not having many
possessions. Rather, true richness is the richness of the soul." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever seeks
self-sufficiency, Allah will make him self-sufficient; whoever seeks
chastity, Allah will grant him chastity; and whoever seeks patience,
Allah will grant him patience. No one is given a gift better and more
expansive than patience." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Hakim ibn Hizam [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported, "I asked the
Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} and he gave me. Then I asked him
again, and he gave me. Then I asked him again, and he gave me. Then he
said, 'O Ḥakīm, this wealth is sweet and green. Whoever takes it with
generosity of soul will be blessed in it, and whoever takes it with
greed will not be blessed in it, and he will be like one who eats but is
never satisfied. The upper hand is better than the lower hand.'

So I said, 'O Messenger of Allah, by the One Who sent you with the
truth, I will never ask anyone for anything after you.'

Then Abū Bakr used to call him to give him something, but he refused to
accept anything from him. Then ʿUmar called him to give him something,
but he refused. Then ʿUmar said, 'O Muslims! I bear witness over you
concerning Ḥakīm that I offer him his share of this wealth, but he
refuses to take it.'

So Ḥakīm did not take anything from anyone after the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} until he died." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

This affected Ḥakīm so deeply that thereafter he never accepted a gift
from anyone.

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The true
richness of the servant is that his heart is independent of creation and
attached only to Allah." He also said, "Greed (ḥirṣ) is a form of
poverty of the heart, even if a person possesses the world." \[Majmūʿ
al-Fatāwā\]

## Anger (Ghaḍab)

Allah Says,

[وَإِذَا مَا غَضِبُوا هُمْ يَغْفِرُونَ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And when they become angry, they forgive."* \[Sūrat al-Shūrā, 37\]

A Muslim should restrain his anger and should not display it without
reason. Allah has praised righteous believers by saying that they
suppress their anger.

Forgiving while calm is easy, but forgiving while angry, when a person
is out of control, is difficult. Yet it is this act that is
praiseworthy.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said: "A strong man is not the one
who overpowers others, but the one who controls himself when angry."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

A man said to the Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, "Advise me." He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Do not become angry." The man repeated his request
several times, and he [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said each time, "Do not become
angry." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī \]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} taught three remedies for anger:
He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that anger comes from Shayṭān, and Shayṭān was
created from fire. Fire is extinguished by water, so when one becomes
angry, he should perform wuḍū'. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "If one of you becomes angry while
standing, let him sit down. If the anger leaves him, well and good;
otherwise, let him lie down." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Sulayman ibn Surad [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported, "Two men insulted
each other in the presence of the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, and one of
them became angry, and his face became red. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
said, 'I know a statement which, if he were to say it, what he feels
would go away. If he were to say:

[أَعُوذُ بِاللَّهِ مِنَ الشَّيْطَانِ الرَّجِيم]{dir="rtl"}ِ

"I seek refuge with Allah from Shayṭān, the accursed." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "If anger is not
restrained, it leads to oppression, injustice, and regret." \[Madarij
al-Salikin\]

## Resentment and Malice (Ḥiqd)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَجْعَلْ فِي قُلُوبِنَا غِلًّا لِّلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not place in our hearts resentment toward the believers."*
\[Sūrat al-Ḥashr, 59:10\]

Keeping enmity in the heart for a long time is called malice and
resentment. It is such an evil trait that Allah has praised those who
pray to be free from it. In Paradise, people will be brothers to one
another; there will be no resentment there.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Do not envy one another, do
not inflate prices against one another, do not hate one another, do not
turn away from one another, and do not undercut one another in trade.
Be, O servants of Allah, brothers. A Muslim is the brother of a Muslim:
he does not oppress him, nor abandon him, nor belittle him. Taqwā is
here," and he pointed to his chest three times. "It is enough evil for a
person to look down upon his Muslim brother. Every Muslim is sacred to
another Muslim: his blood, his wealth, and his honor." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "It is not permissible for a Muslim to
abandon his brother for more than three nights. They meet, and this one
turns away, and that one turns away. The better of the two is the one
who initiates the greeting of peace." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The gates of Paradise are opened on Mondays and
Thursdays, and every servant who does not associate anything with Allah
is forgiven, except a man between whom and his brother there is
resentment. It will be said: 'Delay these two until they reconcile.
Delay these two until they reconcile.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Removing
malice from the heart is among the greatest acts of purification of the
soul." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The heart cannot be
sound while it contains malice, envy, and hatred." \[Madarij
al-Salikin\]

## Arrogance (Kibr)

Allah Says,

[فَاهْبِطْ مِنْهَا فَمَا يَكُونُ لَكَ أَن تَتَكَبَّرَ فِيهَا ۖ فَاخْرُجْ إِنَّكَ مِنَ
الصَّاغِرِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*"Descend from here; it is not for you to be arrogant here. So
depart---indeed, you are among the humiliated."* \[Sūrat al-Aʿrāf, 13\]

When a person possesses some excellence and, because of it, considers
others inferior, disgraceful, or beneath himself, this is called
arrogance and pride. It is a reprehensible trait.

Shayṭān was expelled from Paradise because of arrogance. He considered
himself superior to Ādam and said, "I am better than him. You created
him from clay, and You created me from fire." Allah disliked this
arrogance and commanded, "Descend from here; it is not for you to be
arrogant here. So depart---indeed, you are among the humiliated."
\[Sūrat al-Aʿrāf, 7:13\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "No one who has in his heart
the weight of a mustard seed of arrogance will enter Paradise." A man
said, "A man likes his clothes to be good and his sandals to be good."
He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah is Beautiful and loves beauty. Arrogance
is rejecting the truth and looking down upon people." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "Whoever loves that people stand up for
him, let him take his seat in the Fire." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Once, the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was eating while seated on
the ground. A Bedouin present said, "What kind of sitting is this?" The
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} replied: "Allah has made me a noble servant, not
an arrogant or tyrannical one." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

On another occasion, he [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Shall I inform you who the
people of Hell are? They said, "Yes." He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Every
harsh, violent, and arrogant person." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Arrogance is
rejecting the truth and despising people, and this is the essence of
kibr." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, The origins of
wrongdoing are three:

1.  Arrogance: It is that which drove Iblees to that which it drove him
    (i.e., to commit that which Iblees committed).

2.  Desire: It is that which caused Aadam to be expelled from the
    Paradise.

3.  Jealousy: It is that which caused one of the sons of Adam to
    transgress against his brother.

Therefore, whoever is protected from the evil of these three (traits)
then indeed he has been protected from evil. For disbelief is from
arrogance; disobedience from desires; and transgression and oppression
from jealousy. \[al-Fawā'id\]

## Showing Off (Riyāʾ)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَكُونُوا كَالَّذِينَ خَرَجُوا مِن دِيَارِهِم بَطَرًا وَرِئَاءَ النَّاسِ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not be like those who came out boastfully and to show off
before people."* \[Sūrat al-Anfāl, 47\]

This āyah shows that Allah dislikes actions done for show and arrogance.
Showing off means performing acts such as prayer, fasting, ḥajj, zakāh,
jihād, generosity, and other good deeds not for fulfilling obligation or
seeking Allah's Pleasure, but so that people may call one pious,
righteous, or noble. All such deeds have no value with Allah.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that Allah said, "I am the most
independent of partners. Whoever does a deed in which he associates
others with Me, I will leave him and his shirk." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "When Allah gathers the first
and the last on the Day of Resurrection for a Day about which there is
no doubt, a caller will announce, 'Whoever associated partners with
Allah in any deed, let him seek his reward from other than Allah, for
Allah is the most independent of partners.'" \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The first of people to be
judged on the Day of Resurrection will be a man who died as a martyr. He
will be brought, and Allah will make known to him His favors, and he
will recognize them. Allah will say, 'What did you do with them?' He
will say, 'I fought for Your sake until I was martyred.'

Allah will say, 'You have lied. Rather, you fought so that it would be
said: "He is brave," and it was said.' Then it will be commanded that he
be dragged on his face until he is thrown into the Fire.

Then a man who acquired knowledge and taught it and recited the Qur'an
will be brought. Allah will make known to him His favors, and he will
recognize them. Allah will say, 'What did you do with them?' He will
say, 'I acquired knowledge and taught it, and I recited the Qur'an for
Your sake.'

Allah will say, 'You have lied. Rather, you learned so that it would be
said: "He is a scholar," and you recited so that it would be said: "He
is a reciter," and it was said.' Then it will be commanded that he be
dragged on his face until he is thrown into the Fire.

Then a man whom Allah had enriched and given all kinds of wealth will be
brought. Allah will make known to him His favors, and he will recognize
them. Allah will say, 'What did you do with them?' He will say, 'I did
not leave any path in which You love wealth to be spent except that I
spent in it for Your sake.'

Allah will say, 'You have lied. Rather, you did it so that it would be
said: "He is generous," and it was said.' Then it will be commanded that
he be dragged on his face until he is thrown into the Fire." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Riyāʾ is a
form of minor shirk, and it invalidates the deed for which it is
intended." He also said, "Actions are only accepted when they are purely
for Allah and in accordance with the Sunnah." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Ikhlāṣ is that the
servant's inward and outward actions are the same. Riyāʾ is when the
outward actions are better than the inward. And truthfulness in
sincerity is when the inward is better than the outward." \[Madarij
al-Sālikīn\]

## Envy (Ḥasad)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَتَمَنَّوْا مَا فَضَّلَ اللَّهُ بِهِ بَعْضَكُمْ عَلَىٰ بَعْضٍ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not covet that by which Allah has given some of you advantage
over others."* \[Sūrat al-Nisā', 32\]

If Allah grants someone honor or excellence in worldly or religious
matters, and upon seeing this, a desire arises in your heart to attain
the same honor, this is called emulation (ghibṭah). This is not a bad
trait; rather, emulation in religious matters is commendable.

However, if upon seeing these blessings jealousy develops and one wishes
that the other person should lose that blessing, this is envy (ḥasad).

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Beware of envy, for envy
consumes good deeds just as fire consumes wood." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "There is no envy except in two cases:
a man whom Allah has given wealth and he spends it in truth, and a man
whom Allah has given wisdom and he judges by it and teaches it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Envy is to wish for the
removal of Allah's blessing from its possessor, and this is its first
level. The second level is to wish that harm and misfortune accompany
him constantly, like a shadow follows its object. At this level, the
envier wishes that the one he envies remains ignorant, poor, weak, and
cut off from Allah. He wishes deficiency in his religion. In reality,
such a person hates what Allah has decreed and granted to His servants."
\[Ḥasad: Dhamm al-Ḥasad wa Ahlīhi\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} also said, "A protection from
envy is giving charity and doing good as much as one is able. This has
an amazing effect in repelling calamities, the evil eye, and the evil of
envy. If there were nothing other than the experiences of past nations
to prove this, it would suffice.

The evil eye and envy do not come near the one who gives charity and
does good to creation; and if they do reach him, they are light, and his
end is praiseworthy. This is because gratitude protects blessings.

The envier's heart does not find rest until the envied loses his
blessing, and nothing protects blessings like gratitude. Just as nothing
causes blessings to vanish like ingratitude." \[Miʾat Naṣīḥah\]

## Quarreling (Tanāzuʿ)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَنَازَعُوا فَتَفْشَلُوا وَتَذْهَبَ رِيحُكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not dispute, lest you lose courage and your strength depart."*
\[Sūrat al-Anfāl, 46\]

In this āyah, Allah commands that you must not quarrel among yourselves.
Quarreling produces two consequences.

First: You become weak. Internal conflict weakens you, drains your
strength, and leaves you unable to stand firm against enemies. Your
energy is consumed in internal disputes, and your resolve collapses when
facing others.

Second: When this happens, your influence disappears, your reputation
deteriorates, and your honor vanishes in the eyes of others.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A believer to another
believer is like a building, each part strengthening the other," and he
interlaced his fingers. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] Just as bricks of a wall
join together to form a strong fortress, the Muslim community is like a
fortress whose strength lies in each brick supporting the other. As long
as the bricks remain connected, the wall stands; once a brick slips from
its place, the entire wall collapses.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The example of the believers in their
mutual love, mercy, and compassion is like a body: when one limb
suffers, the whole body responds to it with sleeplessness and fever."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] This is how Muslims should be---if even one
suffers, all should feel the pain.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A Muslim is the brother of
another Muslim: he does not wrong him, nor abandon him. Whoever fulfills
the need of his brother, Allah will fulfill his need. Whoever relieves a
Muslim of a hardship, Allah will relieve him of a hardship on the Day of
Resurrection. Whoever conceals a Muslim, Allah will conceal him on the
Day of Resurrection." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A believer is the mirror of another believer,
and a believer is the brother of another believer. He protects him from
loss and guards him in his absence." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

## Killing (Qatl)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَقْتُلُوا أَنفُسَكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not kill yourselves."* \[Sūrat al-Nisā', 29\]

Whether it is one's own life or another's, life is a blessing from Allah
and must be respected. It must not be taken unjustly.

Some people, overwhelmed by grief, hardship, or illness, take their own
lives. Some kill their children due to financial stress or other
reasons. Others take the lives of people due to enmity, greed for
wealth, or other motives. All of these are grave sins.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "There was a man among those
before you who was wounded. He became impatient and took a knife and cut
his hand, and the blood did not stop until he died. Allah said: 'My
servant hastened his death; I have forbidden Paradise for him.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever kills himself with
something will be punished with it on the Day of Resurrection." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever throws himself from a
mountain and kills himself will be throwing himself in the Fire of Hell
forever. Whoever drinks poison and kills himself will be sipping it in
the Fire of Hell forever. Whoever kills himself with an iron weapon will
have that weapon in his hand, stabbing himself in his abdomen in the
Fire of Hell forever." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "If the people of the
heavens and the earth were to participate in the shedding of the blood
of a believer, Allah would throw them all into the Fire." \[Sunan
al-Tirmidhī\]

Killing unjustly is sinful not only with respect to Muslims, but also to
others. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever kills a person under
covenant will not smell the fragrance of Paradise, though its fragrance
can be perceived from a distance of forty years." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The sanctity
of blood is among the greatest sanctities in Islam, and violating it is
among the gravest sins." He also said, "Killing without right is among
the greatest causes of punishment in this world and the Hereafter."
\[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "After shirk, there is
no sin greater than unlawful killing." \[al-Jawāb al-Kāfī\] He further
said, "The preservation of life is one of the greatest objectives of the
Sharīʿah." \[Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]

## Self-Destruction (Halākah)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تُلْقُوا بِأَيْدِيكُمْ إِلَى التَّهْلُكَةِ]{dir="rtl"}

"And do not throw yourselves into destruction." \[Sūrat al-Baqarah,
2:195\]

This āyah shows that one must not engage in actions whose outcome is
destruction and ruin.

Some people misunderstand this āyah to mean that one should never face
danger or hardship. This is incorrect because, at times, avoiding
hardship and danger itself leads to destruction and ruin.

Abu Ayyub al-Ansari [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} said, "This āyah was
revealed about us, the Anṣār. When Allah gave strength to Islam and its
people became numerous, we said among ourselves secretly, without
informing the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}: 'Our wealth has been
lost, and Allah has strengthened Islam and increased its supporters. If
we were to stay behind and tend to our wealth, we could restore what we
have lost.' So Allah revealed to His Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}:

[وَأَنفِقُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ وَلَا تُلْقُوا بِأَيْدِيكُمْ إِلَى التَّهْلُكَة]{dir="rtl"}ِ

'And spend in the way of Allah and do not throw yourselves into
destruction.' So destruction was in staying behind from jihād and
occupying ourselves with our wealth and abandoning striving." \[Sunan
Abī Dāwūd\]

It is reported that during a battle near Constantinople, a man charged
alone into the ranks of the enemy. People said, "Subḥān Allāh! He is
throwing himself into destruction!" Abu Ayyub al-Ansari [رضي الله
عنه]{dir="rtl"} said, "O people! You interpret this āyah incorrectly. It
was revealed about us, the Anṣār..." (then he mentioned the above
explanation) \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Destruction
(tahlukah) is abandoning what Allah has obligated, and this is more
severe than facing danger in obedience to Allah." He also said,
"Entering danger for the sake of Allah is not destruction; rather,
destruction lies in abandoning His commands." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

## Laziness and Grief

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَهِنُوا وَلَا تَحْزَنُوا وَأَنتُمُ الْأَعْلَوْنَ إِن كُنتُم
مُّؤْمِنِينَ]{dir="rtl"}[]{dir="ltr"}

*"So do not weaken and do not grieve; and you will be superior if you
are believers."* \[Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, 139\]

From this āyah, two lessons are learned.

First: A person should not be lazy. A lazy person can never succeed. One
should be active and hardworking and must not show laziness in any task.
Allah grants success to the one who strives and works hard.

Second: If at any time hardship occurs or failure is faced in a matter,
one should not waste time in grief. Instead, one should consider that
time valuable and use it to make up for the loss.

The result of these two qualities is that if faith is sincere, success
and elevation will surely be attained.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} used to say, "O Allah, I seek refuge
in You from anxiety and grief, from incapacity and laziness, from
cowardice and miserliness, and from the burden of debt and the
oppression of men." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The strong believer is better
and more beloved to Allah than the weak believer, though there is good
in both. Be keen on what benefits you, seek help from Allah, and do not
be helpless. If something befalls you, do not say: 'If only I had done
such-and-such,' but say: 'Qaddarallāh wa mā shāʾa faʿal (Allah has
decreed, and whatever He wills, He does),' for 'if' opens the door to
Shayṭān." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "How amazing is the affair of
the believer! All of his affairs are good, and this is not for anyone
except the believer. If something pleasing happens to him, he is
grateful, and that is good for him. If something harmful happens to him,
he is patient, and that is good for him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Knowledge and action
are twins, and their mother is high aspiration. Ignorance and inactivity
are twins, and their mother is laziness." \[al-Fawāʾid\]

## Mockery

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا يَسْخَرْ قَوْمٌ مِّن قَوْمٍ عَسَىٰ أَن يَكُونُوا خَيْرًا مِّنْهُمْ وَلَا نِسَاءٌ
مِّن نِّسَاءٍ عَسَىٰ أَن يَكُنَّ خَيْرًا مِّنْهُنَّ]{dir="rtl"}

*"O you who believe! Let not a people mock another people; perhaps they
may be better than them. Nor let women mock other women; perhaps they
may be better than them."* \[Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt, 11\]

Muslims should not ridicule or mock one another. Times do not remain the
same forever. The one who laughs at others may himself become an object
of ridicule.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} described the condition of those
who mock others by saying, "The gates of Paradise will be opened for one
of you, and he will be called to enter, but when he comes, it will be
closed before him. Then another gate will be opened, and he will be
called again, and when he comes, it will be closed before him, until he
becomes distressed. This is for the one who used to mock people."
\[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

Note: This narration is reported, but its chain is not among the
strongest. It should be used cautiously.

The Companions were aware of people's major faults, yet they did not
expose or ridicule them. Instead, they tried to conceal them. The
Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever conceals a Muslim, Allah
will conceal him in this world and the Hereafter." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Abu Bakr al-Siddiq [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} said, "If I were to find a
man committing a sin, I would love that Allah conceal him."

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has not permitted
joking as an excuse to mock others." \[Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]

## Spreading Corruption and Discord (Fitnah wa Fasād)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَعْثَوْا فِي الْأَرْضِ مُفْسِدِينَ]{dir="rtl"}[]{dir="ltr"}

*"And do not roam about on the earth spreading corruption."* \[Sūrat
al-Baqarah, 60\]

Islam is a religion of peace and security. It desires calmness and
tranquility in the world and commands that mischief and corruption not
be spread on the earth.

We should avoid actions that cause distress to Allah's creation, lead to
discord, and spread immoral conduct. Instead, we should participate in
righteous deeds that establish true peace in the world and grant people
honesty and genuine tranquility.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "There should be neither
harming nor reciprocating harm." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A Muslim is the one from
whose tongue and hand the Muslims are safe, and a muhājir is the one who
abandons what Allah has forbidden." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "There will be tribulations
(fitan) during which the one sitting is better than the one standing,
the one standing is better than the one walking, and the one walking is
better than the one running. Whoever exposes himself to them will be
drawn into them, so whoever finds a refuge or shelter should seek it."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Corruption
(fasād) in the earth is every action that leads to harm in religion or
worldly affairs." He also said, "The objective of the Sharīʿah is to
bring about benefit and prevent corruption." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The Sharīʿah is built
upon achieving benefits and preventing harms, and all of it revolves
around justice and wisdom." \[Iʿlām al-Muwaqqiʿīn\] He further said,
"Wherever corruption prevails, it is due to abandoning the commands of
Allah and His Messenger." \[al-Dāʾ wa al-Dawāʾ\]

## Claiming Self-Purity

Allah Says,

[فَلَا تُزَكُّوا أَنفُسَكُمْ هُوَ أَعْلَمُ بِمَنِ اتَّقَىٰ]{dir="rtl"}

*"So do not claim purity for yourselves; He knows best who is truly
righteous."* \[Sūrat al-Najm, 32\]

We should not boast about our own virtue and purity. Those who are truly
pure, sincere, and accomplished always consider themselves lesser than
others.

When a person begins to view piety, excellence, and righteousness as
self-created qualities, the diseases of self-admiration and arrogance
arise. Therefore, the Qur'an says:

Allah Says,

[لِكَيْلَا تَأْسَوْا عَلَىٰ مَا فَاتَكُمْ وَلَا تَفْرَحُوا بِمَا آتَاكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"Do not exult over what He has given you."* \[Sūrat al-Ḥadīd, 23\]

If a person believes that all skill and excellence are gifts from Allah,
he will remain protected from this disease.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} mentioned among the signs of the
Last Hour that every person will begin to admire his own opinion, take
pride in it, and boast. The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "When
you see miserliness being obeyed, desires being followed, worldly life
being preferred, and every person being impressed with his own opinion,
then focus on yourself and leave the common people." \[Sunan
al-Tirmidhī\]

Once, someone was mentioned in the presence of the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, and a man praised him. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
"Woe to you! You have cut the neck of your companion!" He repeated this
several times. Then he said, "If one of you must praise his brother, let
him say: 'I think so-and-so is such-and-such, and Allah knows best about
him, and I do not purify anyone before Allah,' if he truly knows that of
him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} discouraged excessive praise because it
causes self-admiration to develop in the heart of the one being praised.
The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Three things are destructive:
miserliness that is obeyed, desire that is followed, and a person being
impressed with himself." \[al-Ṭabarānī\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The more a servant
recognizes the favors of Allah upon him, the less he sees himself."
\[al-Fawāʾid\]

## Reminding of One's Favors (Mann)

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تُبْطِلُوا صَدَقَاتِكُم بِالْمَنِّ وَالْأَذَىٰ]{dir="rtl"}

*"O you who believe! Do not invalidate your charities by reminders of
generosity and by causing harm."* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah, 264\]

Charity given in the path of Allah must be done with a sincere
intention. Its purpose should not be to remind people of one's favor or
to seek repayment for the good deed.

When we do good to someone, it is done in one of two ways: either for
the sake of Allah or for some personal motive.

If it is done for Allah, then we have no favor over the servant, nor
should we expect anything in return. And if it is done for personal
motives, then it no longer remains true goodness.

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَمْنُن تَسْتَكْثِر]{dir="rtl"}ُ

*"Do not give, seeking greater return."* \[Sūrat al-Muddaththir, 6\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Three people Allah will not
speak to on the Day of Resurrection, nor will He look at them, nor
purify them, and they will have a painful punishment: the one who lets
his garment hang below his ankles out of pride, the one who reminds
others of what he has given, and the one who sells his goods by false
oaths." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Seven will be shaded by Allah
on the Day when there is no shade except His shade: a just ruler; a
young man who grew up in the worship of Allah; a man whose heart is
attached to the mosques; two men who love each other for the sake of
Allah, meeting and parting upon that; a man who is called by a woman of
status and beauty and says: 'I fear Allah'; a man who gives charity and
conceals it so that his left hand does not know what his right hand has
spent; and a man who remembers Allah in solitude and his eyes overflow
with tears." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Reminding
others of one's favor (mann) invalidates charity just as Allah has
stated, because it harms the recipient and corrupts intention." \[Majmūʿ
al-Fatāwā\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The perfection of
giving is that the giver does not see himself as having any favor over
the one he gives." \[Madarij al-Salikin\]

## Miserliness (Bukhl)

Allah Says,

[وَاللَّهُ لَا يُحِبُّ كُلَّ مُخْتَالٍ فَخُورٍ - الَّذِينَ يَبْخَلُونَ وَيَأْمُرُونَ النَّاسَ
بِالْبُخْلِ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And Allah does not love every self-conceited, boastful person---those
who are miserly and command others to be miserly."* \[Sūrat al-Nisā',
36--37\]

When a person possesses wealth but does not spend it in its proper
places, this is miserliness. It is such a corrupt trait that many other
evils---such as betrayal, dishonesty, lack of compassion, greed, and
similar moral faults---arise from it.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Faith and miserliness are
not combined in the heart of a servant, nor are faith and bad character
combined in his heart." \[Sunan al-Nasāʾī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} used to supplicate, "O Allah, I seek refuge
in You from miserliness, from cowardice, from being returned to the most
decrepit old age, from the trial of this world, and from the punishment
of the grave." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Beware of oppression, for
oppression will be darkness on the Day of Resurrection, and beware of
miserliness, for miserliness destroyed those before you. It led them to
shed their blood and make lawful what was unlawful." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

A person who hoards wealth and remains attached to it---about such a
person, the Qur'an has drawn a striking picture. Allah says:

Allah Says,

[الَّذِي جَمَعَ مَالًا وَعَدَّدَهُ ۝ يَحْسَبُ أَنَّ مَالَهُ أَخْلَدَهُ ۝ كَلَّا لَيُنبَذَنَّ فِي
الْحُطَمَة]{dir="rtl"}ِ

*"The one who amasses wealth and counts it, thinking that his wealth
will make him live forever---never! He will surely be thrown into the
Crusher."* \[Sūrat al-Humazah, 2--4\]

A person should understand that wealth is not our ultimate objective.
The real objective is to spend the wealth given by Allah in righteous
causes and thereby earn His pleasure. Gratitude for wealth lies in using
it as a means for success in both religion and the world.

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Miserliness is among
the greatest diseases of the heart, and it opposes trust in Allah and
reliance upon Him." \[Madarij al-Salikin\] He further said, "The cure
for miserliness is generosity and certainty that what is with Allah is
better and more lasting." \[al-Fawāʾid\]

## Extravagance (Isrāf / Tabdhīr)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تُبَذِّرْ تَبْذِيرًا - إِنَّ الْمُبَذِّرِينَ كَانُوا إِخْوَانَ الشَّيَاطِينِ ۖ وَكَانَ الشَّيْطَانُ لِرَبِّهِ
كَفُورًا]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not squander wastefully. Indeed, the wasteful are brothers of
the devils, and Shayṭān is ever ungrateful to his Lord."* \[Sūrat Banī
Isrā'īl, 26--27\]

Spending beyond one's means or at improper times is extravagance. The
result of extravagance is that a person becomes destitute and
impoverished, and poverty leads to many sins. Therefore, it must be
avoided.

In this āyah, Allah has called the extravagant person a brother of
Shayṭān, and Shayṭān an extreme ingrate---meaning that wasting wealth,
which is a blessing from Allah, is severe ingratitude.

Today, people may praise you for your extravagance; tomorrow, when all
wealth is gone, the same people will reproach you. The Qur'an says:

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَجْعَلْ يَدَكَ مَغْلُولَةً إِلَىٰ عُنُقِكَ وَلَا تَبْسُطْهَا كُلَّ الْبَسْطِ فَتَقْعُدَ مَلُومًا
مَّحْسُورًا]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not make your hand chained to your neck, nor extend it
completely, lest you sit blamed and destitute."* \[Sūrat Banī Isrā'īl,
29\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has disliked for you
three things: idle talk, excessive questioning, and wasting wealth."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Eat, give charity, and clothe
yourselves without extravagance and without arrogance." \[Sunan Ibn
Mājah\]

Abdullah ibn Abbas [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} said, "Eat what you wish
and wear what you wish, so long as you avoid two things: extravagance
and arrogance." \[Muṣannaf Ibn Abī Shaybah\]

Imam Ibn Kathir [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} mentioned, "Some of the Salaf
said: 'Allah has gathered all of medicine in half an āyah: "Eat and
drink, but do not be extravagant."'" \[Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "If wealth is in your
hand and not in your heart, it will not harm you even if it is abundant;
but if it is in your heart, it will harm you even if you possess
nothing." \[Madārij al-Sālikīn\]

## Secretly Taking from Shared Property (Ghulūl)

Allah Says,

[وَمَن يَغْلُلْ يَأْتِ بِمَا غَلَّ يَوْمَ الْقِيَامَةِ]{dir="rtl"}

*"And whoever betrays by secretly taking will come with what he took on
the Day of Resurrection."* \[Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, 161\]

When property jointly belongs to several people and has not yet been
divided, secretly taking something from it without the others' knowledge
is called ghulūl. This is also a form of theft.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said: "O people! Whoever is
appointed over any of our affairs and takes even a needle secretly has
committed ghulūl and will bring it on the Day of Judgment." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim\]

Abu Hurayra [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} narrated, "When we conquered
Khaybar, we did not gain gold or silver as booty, but we gained
livestock, goods, and gardens. Then we departed with the Messenger of
Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} to the valley of al-Qurā. The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} had a slave named Midʿam who had been given to him by one
of Banū al-Ḍibbāb.

While he was unloading the saddle of the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, an arrow whose source was unknown struck him. The people
said, 'Congratulations to him for martyrdom.' The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, 'No, by the One in whose hand is my soul, the cloak
which he took unlawfully from the spoils of Khaybar before they were
distributed is burning him with fire.' When the people heard this, a man
came with one or two straps of sandals and said, 'O Messenger of Allah,
I took these unlawfully.' The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, 'A
strap of fire, or two straps of fire.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Ghulūl is
among the gravest forms of betrayal because it combines theft and breach
of trust." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

## Consuming Wealth Unjustly

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَأْكُلُوا أَمْوَالَكُم بَيْنَكُم بِالْبَاطِلِ]{dir="rtl"}[]{dir="ltr"}

*"And do not consume one another's wealth unjustly."* \[Sūrat
al-Baqarah, 188\]

Any wealth acquired in a manner contrary to what Allah has prescribed is
unlawful. It is forbidden for a Muslim to use such wealth.

Theft, robbery, bribery, usury, immorality, and dishonesty are all
unlawful means of earning and must be avoided.

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "O people, Allah is Pure and
accepts only what is pure. Allah has commanded the believers with what
He commanded the Messengers, and He said: 'O Messengers, eat from the
good things and act righteously,' and He said: 'O you who believe, eat
from the good things We have provided for you.'

Then he mentioned a man who travels for a long time, disheveled and
dusty, stretching his hands to the sky, saying: 'O Lord, O Lord,' while
his food is unlawful, his drink is unlawful, his clothing is unlawful,
and he is nourished with unlawful wealth, so how can he be answered?"
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "No flesh nourished by
unlawful earnings will enter Paradise; the Fire is more deserving of
it." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Once, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that if a man buys a garment for
ten dirhams and even one dirham among them is from unlawful earnings,
then as long as he prays wearing that garment, his prayer will not be
accepted. \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

Abu Bakr al-Siddiq [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} had a servant who brought
him some earnings. Abū Bakr ate from it, then the servant informed him
that it was earned in pre-Islamic times through unlawful means. Upon
hearing this, Abū Bakr inserted his fingers into his mouth and vomited
everything he had consumed. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Duʿāʾ is among the
strongest means of repelling harm and attaining what is sought. However,
its effect may not appear due to a weakness in the duʿāʾ itself, such as
when it contains transgression or is not beloved to Allah, or due to
weakness in the heart of the one making duʿāʾ, such that it does not
fully turn to Allah or gather its resolve in supplication, like a weak
bow from which the arrow is released feebly.

Another reason for the duʿāʾ not being answered is the presence of a
barrier, such as consuming unlawful sustenance, committing oppression,
or the accumulation of sins upon the heart, or a heart overtaken by
heedlessness and distraction." \[al-Dāʾ wa al-Dawāʾ\]

## Bribery (Rishwah)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تُدْلُوا بِهَا إِلَى الْحُكَّامِ لِتَأْكُلُوا فَرِيقًا مِّنْ أَمْوَالِ النَّاسِ بِالْإِثْمِ وَأَنتُمْ
تَعْلَمُونَ]{dir="rtl"}

"*And do not offer it to the authorities in order that you may consume a
portion of people's wealth sinfully, while you know."* \[Sūrat
al-Baqarah, 188\]

This āyah prohibits bribery. Bribery means giving something to an
official in order to incline him in one's favor, whether the objective
is rightful or wrongful.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has cursed the one
who gives a bribe and the one who takes a bribe." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

When the Jews of Khaybar attempted to offer a bribe to ʿAbdullāh ibn
Rawāḥah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} in a matter, he said: "O Jews! What
you have offered as a bribe is unlawful. We Muslims do not consume it."

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} prohibited rulers from accepting
gifts due to this very concern. Once, an official came and said, "This
is charity, and this was given to me as a gift." Hearing this, the
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} stood on the pulpit and said,

"What is the matter with an official whom we appoint, and he comes and
says: 'This is for you and this was given to me as a gift'? Why does he
not sit in the house of his father or his mother and see whether gifts
would be given to him or not?

By the One in whose hand is my soul, whoever takes anything from it
unlawfully will come carrying it on his neck on the Day of Resurrection.
If it is a camel, it will be groaning; if a cow, it will be lowing; and
if a sheep, it will be bleating." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Bribery
given to obtain falsehood or to prevent a right is unlawful by
consensus." He also clarified, "If a person gives money to repel
injustice from himself when he has no other means, the sin is upon the
one who takes it, not the one compelled to give it." \[Majmūʿ
al-Fatāwā\]

## Theft

Allah Says,

[وَالسَّارِقُ وَالسَّارِقَةُ فَاقْطَعُوا أَيْدِيَهُمَا]{dir="rtl"}

*"As for the thief, male or female, cut off their hands."* \[Sūrat
al-Mā'idah, 38\]

Taking someone's property secretly without their permission is called
theft. Its punishment has been set as severe, cutting off the hand.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} used to take pledges from Muslims
that they would not commit theft or immorality. He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
"May Allah curse the thief who steals an egg and his hand is cut off,
and who steals a rope and his hand is cut off." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Give me your pledge that you
will not associate anything with Allah, that you will not steal, that
you will not commit adultery, that you will not kill your children, that
you will not bring a slander you fabricate between your hands and your
feet, and that you will not disobey me in what is right. Whoever
fulfills this among you, his reward is with Allah; and whoever commits
any of that and is punished for it in this world, it will be an
expiation for him; and whoever commits any of that and Allah conceals
him, then his affair is with Allah---if He wills, He will punish him,
and if He wills, He will forgive him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "I was shown Paradise and Hell
just now during my prayer. I have never seen good and evil like today. I
saw in the Fire a man who used to steal from the pilgrims with a hooked
stick. If he was noticed, he would say: 'It only got caught on my
stick,' and if he was not noticed, he would take it. I saw him dragging
his intestines in the Fire." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The punishment for
theft is among the most effective deterrents, ensuring the protection of
people's wealth." He further said, "Sharīʿah punishments are built upon
justice, mercy, and wisdom, even if they appear severe." \[Iʿlām
al-Muwaqqiʿīn\]

## Usury (Ribā)

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا لَا تَأْكُلُوا الرِّبَا]{dir="rtl"}

*"O you who believe! Do not consume usury."* \[Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, 3:130\]

Wealth is a blessing from Allah and should be used to serve His
creation. Those whose wealth is used contrary to Allah's commands will
suffer loss in the Hereafter. The business of usury is against Allah's
will and spreads poverty among people. Although a few individuals may
become wealthy, the general public becomes impoverished.

Taking usury is like sucking people's blood. The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Last night two men came to me and took me to a
sacred land. Then we set out until we reached a river of blood, in which
a man was standing, and on the bank of the river, there was another man
with stones in front of him.

The man in the river tried to come out, but whenever he came near the
bank, the man threw a stone into his mouth, forcing him to return to
where he was. He kept coming, and the man kept throwing stones into his
mouth, returning him to his place.

I asked, 'What is this?' They said, 'The one you saw in the river is the
one who consumed ribā.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has cursed the one who
consumes ribā, the one who pays it, the one who records it, and the two
who witness it," and he said, "They are all equal." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Imam Ibn Qudamah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Every loan in which it is
stipulated that an additional payment be made is ḥarām, and there is no
difference of opinion among the scholars regarding that." He also said,
"It was narrated from Ubayy ibn Ka\'b [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"},
Abdullah ibn Abbas [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}, and Abdullah ibn Mas\'ud
[رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} that they forbade loans that bring about any
benefit." \[al-Mughnī\]

Imam Ibn al-Mundhir [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "They (the scholars)
are unanimously agreed that if the lender stipulates that the borrower
must pay extra or give a gift, and he gives the loan on that basis, then
it is ribā." \[al-Ijmāʿ\]

## Intoxicants, Gambling, Divining Arrows, and Sacrificial Stones

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِنَّمَا الْخَمْرُ وَالْمَيْسِرُ وَالْأَنْصَابُ وَالْأَزْلَامُ رِجْسٌ مِّنْ عَمَلِ
الشَّيْطَانِ فَاجْتَنِبُوهُ]{dir="rtl"}

*"O you who believe! Indeed, intoxicants, gambling, sacrificial stones,
and divining arrows are filth from the work of Shayṭān, so avoid them."*
\[Sūrat al-Mā'idah, 90\]

In this āyah, four things are prohibited as satanic practices,
unbefitting for a Muslim.

1.  Intoxicants (Alcohol): Alcohol affects the intellect and ruins
    health and wealth. In intoxication, people commit acts they would
    never do while sober. Many murders and fights occur because of it,
    and it causes negligence toward religious and worldly duties. In a
    hadith, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The adulterer does not
    commit adultery while he is a believer, the thief does not steal
    while he is a believer, and the one who drinks alcohol does not
    drink it while he is a believer." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] . He
    [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "From among the signs of the Hour is that
    knowledge will be taken away, ignorance will prevail, alcohol will
    be drunk, and fornication will become widespread." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
    al-Bukhārī\
    \
    The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has cursed alcohol,
    the one who drinks it, the one who pours it, the one who sells it,
    the one who buys it, the one who squeezes it, the one for whom it is
    squeezed, the one who carries it, and the one to whom it is
    carried." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

2.  Gambling; This too is a cause of wasted wealth, destruction of
    religion and worldly life, and moral corruption. Just as alcohol
    makes a person heedless of religion and life, gambling also makes
    one oblivious to profit and loss. A gambler's life becomes one of
    failure and loss. Countless respectable families have been ruined
    because of it; hence, Islam has prohibited it.\
    \
    The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever says to his
    companion, 'Come, let me gamble with you,' must give charity."
    \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

3.  Divining Arrows: This is also a form of gambling. Among the Arabs,
    camels were the greatest wealth, so they gambled with them. They
    would slaughter camels and divide the meat into ten portions, then
    cast lots using arrows. Ten arrows were designated with different
    shares; they were placed in a bag and drawn by an impartial person.
    Those whose arrows had shares succeeded, while those whose arrows
    had no shares failed. The collected meat was then distributed among
    the poor, the needy, and friends. Because this was ostentatious and
    deceptive generosity, Islam commanded Muslims to avoid it.

4.  Sacrificial Stones (Anṣāb): Before Islam, among the objects
    worshipped besides Allah were sacrificial stones---unshaped stones
    erected for offerings and animal sacrifices. This was a polytheistic
    practice; therefore, Muslims were commanded to keep away from it.\
    \
    The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has cursed the one
    who sacrifices for other than Allah." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Adultery (Zinā)

Allah Says,

[وَلَا تَقْرَبُوا الزِّنَىٰ ۖ إِنَّهُ كَانَ فَاحِشَةً وَسَاءَ سَبِيلًا]{dir="rtl"}

*"And do not go near adultery. Indeed, it is an obscenity and an evil
path."* \[Sūrat al-Isrāʾ, 32\]

Illicit relations between men and women are among the gravest acts of
disobedience. Allah commands not merely to avoid adultery, but not even
to go near it. This means that all actions that lead to immodesty and
immorality must be avoided.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The adulterer does not
commit adultery while he is a believer, the thief does not steal while
he is a believer, and the one who drinks alcohol does not drink it while
he is a believer." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The gaze greatly influences the unlawful relationship between men and
women. Therefore, Allah clearly Says:

[قُل لِّلْمُؤْمِنِينَ يَغُضُّوا مِنْ أَبْصَارِهِمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*"Tell the believing men to lower their gaze."* \[Sūrat al-Nūr, 30)

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that the first glance, which occurs
unintentionally, is forgiven, but looking again deliberately is not
permitted. \[\[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Women were especially instructed not to display their adornment to
strangers, not to let the sound of their jewelry be heard, not to wear
jingling ornaments, to cover their chests, and, when going outside, to
cover their entire bodies with a cloak. They should not apply strong
perfume when going out, and should walk modestly along the side of the
road.

Men and women should not sit together freely, and no man should meet a
non-maḥram woman in seclusion. All these commands are meant so that
Muslims may live lives of honor and purity. The Prophet Muhammad
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "No man is alone with a woman except that Shayṭān
is the third of them." \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} narrated about the Night of
Mi'rāj, "We proceeded until we came to something like a tannūr (oven),
narrow at the top and wide at the bottom, beneath which a fire was
kindled. When the fire flared up, they rose until they were about to
come out of it, and when it subsided, they returned inside it. In it
were naked men and women. I said, 'Who are these?' They said, 'These are
the adulterers and adulteresses.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whenever adultery becomes
widespread among a people, plagues and diseases that were not known
among their predecessors will spread among them." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said that adultery leads to poverty and
destitution.

And he [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said: "Whoever commits adultery, drinks wine, or
steals has removed the rope of Islam from his neck."

Shaykh al-Islam Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Zinā
corrupts lineage, honor, and society, and thus its prohibition is among
the greatest objectives of the Sharīʿah." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

## Begging Persistently

Allah Says,

[لَا يَسْأَلُونَ النَّاسَ إِلْحَافًا]{dir="rtl"}

*"They do not beg people persistently."* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah, 273\]

Begging is considered extremely reprehensible in Islam. The Messenger of
Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said: "Whoever persists in begging will come on the
Day of Judgment with not a single piece of flesh on his face." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever seeks to be self-sufficient,
Allah will make him self-sufficient; and whoever seeks independence,
Allah will grant him independence; and whoever is patient, Allah will
grant him patience. No one is given a gift better and more expansive
than patience." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Such was the state of the Companions that even if a whip fell from their
hands to the ground, they would not ask anyone to pick it up for them.

If a person is compelled to ask, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that he
should ask righteous people, for there is hope that they will fulfill
the request with gentleness, honor, and good intention. He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that begging is not permissible for a strong,
capable, healthy person.

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} further said, "By the One in whose hand is my soul, it
is better for one of you to take his rope, gather firewood, carry it on
his back, and sell it, than to ask a man who may give him or withhold
from him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhāri\]

The Prophet Muhammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said to a man who came asking, "Do
you have anything in your house?" He said, "Yes, a piece of cloth, part
of it we wear and part we spread, and a bowl from which we drink water."
He said, "Bring them to me." He brought them, and the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} took them in his hand and said, "Who will buy these?"

A man said, "I will buy them for one dirham." He said, "Who will offer
more?" twice or thrice. A man said, "I will take them for two dirhams."
He gave them to him and took the two dirhams, then gave them to the man
and said, "Buy food with one dirham for your family, and buy an axe with
the other." Then he said, "Go and gather firewood and sell it, and do
not let me see you for fifteen days." He did so, then came back having
earned ten dirhams.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "This is better for you than
begging, which will come as a mark on your face on the Day of
Resurrection." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]
