
# Good Deeds

## Truthfulness

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَكُونُواْ مَعَ ٱلصَّٰدِقِينَ ١١٩
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And be with those who are truthful. (Sūrat al-Tawbah, Q9:119)
\AYAH_END


Truthfulness brings peace to the heart. A believer should speak the truth in all
circumstances, whether in hardship or ease, and should act truthfully.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO add footnote for Siddiq? -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was asked about the distinguishing
quality of the people of Paradise. He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Truthfulness
leads to righteousness, and righteousness leads to Paradise. A man continues to
speak the truth until he is recorded with Allāh as a _ṣiddīq_." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

Speaking the truth before a tyrannical ruler is the greatest form of Jihād. The
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The best _jihād_ is a word of truth spoken
before a tyrannical ruler." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

In speaking the truth, one should not favor relatives, close ones, friends, or
even fear enemies.

<!-- TODO ref Al-Muwaṭṭāʾ -->
<!-- TODO committed an offence? -->

Once, the Companion Qudāmah ibn Maẓʿūn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} committed an offense
of consuming alcohol. When ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} intended to carry out the
legal punishment, Qudāmah's wife testified truthfully against him. She did not
favor her husband over the truth. \[Muwaṭṭaʾ Mālik\] She did not give preference
to her husband over the truth, but upheld honesty even when it was difficult.

Abandoning truth for a small worldly benefit and earning the displeasure of
Allāh is not the conduct of people of intellect. Truthfulness is:

- A mark of _īmān_;
- A path to righteousness; and
- A means to eternal success.


## Generosity and Charity

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ أَنفِقُواْ مِمَّا رَزَقۡنَٰكُم
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O you who believe! Spend from what We have provided for you. (Sūrat al-Baqarah,
Q2:254)
\AYAH_END


Whatever Allāh has granted a servant from His blessings, he should give from it
to those who have not received it or have received less. This is what is called
_sakhāwah_ (generosity) and _fayyāḍīyah_ (magnanimity).

Generosity manifests in many forms, including:

- Forgiving one's rightful dues for others;
- Giving surplus wealth to others;
- Giving despite one's own need;
- Preferring others over oneself (_īthār_);
- Spending one's physical strength in helping others;
- Using one's intellect for the benefit of others;
- Enduring personal risk to protect others or uphold truth; and
- Even sacrificing one's life in the path of justice.

Thus, generosity is not limited to wealth; it encompasses every form of selfless
giving.

<!-- TODO ref missing -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Do not tie up (your wealth),
otherwise it will be tied up from you." Meaning: if you withhold from others,
Allāh will withhold from you.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} once asked the Companions, "Which of you loves
his own wealth more than the wealth of his heirs?" They replied, "None of us
considers the wealth of his heirs more beloved than his own."

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Your wealth is what you send ahead, and what you
leave behind belongs to your heirs." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Envy (_ghibṭah_) is not permissible except
in two cases:

1.  A person whom Allāh has given wealth, and he spends it rightly.
2.  A person whom Allāh has given knowledge, and he acts upon it and teaches
    it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

A man asked which charity is the greatest. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
replied, "That you give charity while you are healthy, desirous of wealth, and
hopeful of living, and not delay it until the soul reaches the throat, then you
say, 'Give such-and-such to so-and-so,' while it has already become the property
of your heirs." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]


## A Goodly Loan (Qard Ḥasan)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱلۡمُصَّدِّقِينَ وَٱلۡمُصَّدِّقَٰتِ وَأَقۡرَضُواْ ٱللَّهَ قَرۡضًا حَسَنٗا يُضَٰعَفُ لَهُمۡ وَلَهُمۡ أَجۡرٞ كَرِيمٞ ١٨
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, those men and women who give in charity and lend to Allāh a good loan
will have it multiplied for them, and they will have an honorable reward. (Sūrat
al-Ḥadīd, Q57:18)
\AYAH_END


When someone shows kindness to us in this world, we return that kindness.
However, the favors of Allāh upon us can never be repaid. Therefore, the way to
"lend to Allāh" is to serve His servants---to assist those in need and support
beneficial causes.

Clearly, Allāh is not in need of any loan. Rather, giving a "loan to Allāh"
means helping His needy servants and spending in paths that are pleasing to Him.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that on the Day of Judgment,
Allāh will say, "O son of Ādam! I was ill, and you did not visit Me." The
servant will reply that Allāh is the Lord of the worlds. Allāh will say that His
servant was ill and had he visited him, he would have found Allāh with him.

Allāh will then say, "O son of Ādam! I asked you for food, and you did not feed
Me." The servant will reply that Allāh is the Lord of the worlds. Allāh will say
that His servant asked for food and had he fed him, he would have found his
reward with Allāh.

Allāh will then say, "O son of Ādam! I asked you for water, and you did not give
Me water." The servant will reply that Allāh is the Lord of the worlds. Allāh
will say that His servant asked for water and had he given it, he would have
found it with Allāh. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]


## Loans and Debt

Allāh Says,

<!-- TODO The complete āyah is not quoted. -->

\AYAH_AR_START
وَإِن كَانَ ذُو عُسۡرَةٖ فَنَظِرَةٌ إِلَىٰ مَيۡسَرَةٖۚ وَأَن تَصَدَّقُواْ خَيۡرٞ لَّكُمۡ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And if the debtor is in difficulty, then grant him time until ease; and if you
remit it as charity, that is better for you. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:280)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

People take loans from one another in times of need, but a person should strive
to repay their debt as soon as possible. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said,
"The best among you are those who are best in repaying debts." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

When al-Zubayr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} went out for the Battle of the Camel, he
called his son and said, "I believe I may be wronged and killed today. What
worries me most is my debt. Sell our property and repay my debts first; and if
anything remains, seek Allāh's help." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

Because of this Quranic command, the Companions would grant respite to debtors.
A man owed Qatādah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} money. Whenever Qatādah came to collect,
the man would hide. One day, he was found and admitted to his hardship. Qatādah
wept and narrated that the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever grants
respite to a debtor in difficulty or forgives him, Allāh will shade him under
His Throne on the Day when there is no shade but His." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Companions not only granted respite, but they would also forgive debts. A
man owed money to Abū al-Yasar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and was extremely poor. Abū
al-Yasar said to him, "Go, your debt is forgiven." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Once, a funeral was brought in, for which the deceased owed three dinars. The
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} refused to lead the funeral prayer. Abū
Qatādah al-Anṣārī said, "O Messenger of Allāh, I will take responsibility for
his debt." Then the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} led the funeral prayer. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]


## Trustworthiness (Amānah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأۡمُرُكُمۡ أَن تُؤَدُّواْ ٱلۡأَمَٰنَٰتِ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهَا
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, Allāh commands you to deliver trusts to their rightful owners. (Sūrat
al-Nisāʾ, Q4:58)
\AYAH_END


_Amānah_ (trust) means that a person conducts his dealings with complete honesty
and integrity, giving everyone their due fully and precisely. This includes:

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

[Returning Entrusted Property]{.smallcaps}: If someone entrusts you with wealth
or property, it must be returned exactly as it was given. The Prophet Muḥammad
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Render the trust to the one who entrusted you, and do
not betray the one who betrays you." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

[Safeguarding Secrets]{.smallcaps}: If someone shares a secret, it is also an
_amānah_ and must not be disclosed. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "When a
man speaks to another and then turns away, it is a trust." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]
To expose such a trust is a sign of shamelessness and betrayal.

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

[Give Sincere Advice]{.smallcaps}: If someone seeks advice, sincere advice
should be given. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allow people
to barter amongst themselves, but if one of your brothers seeks advice, then let
him offer advice." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

[Leadership Is A Trust]{.smallcaps}: If a person considers himself unfit for a
responsibility, he should withdraw from it. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said
to Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī, "O Abū Dharr, you are weak, and it (leadership) is a
trust. On the Day of Resurrection, it will be a cause of humiliation and regret,
except for one who takes it with its right and fulfills what is due upon him."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

Such people are called trustworthy. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
was extremely trustworthy. People generally referred to him as “al-Amīn,” and
even his enemies trusted him. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that betraying
a trust is a sign of deficiency in faith and hypocrisy. He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
said, "There is no faith for the one who has no trustworthiness, and there is no
religion for the one who does not fulfill his covenant." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would repay debts with better than what he
borrowed and emphasized justice in all dealings. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]


## Modesty (Ḥayāʾ)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَيَسۡتَحۡيِۦ مِنكُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
He felt shy before you. (Sūrat al-Aḥzāb, Q33:53)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO goodness → good? -->

Due to modesty, a person is saved from many evils. The Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that modesty brings nothing but goodness. A person who
feels no hesitation in committing evil is shameless and immodest. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "If you have no modesty, then do whatever you wish."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that faith has over sixty branches, and modesty is
one branch of faith. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was extremely modest. During his time,
there was a man who was excessively shy, to the extent that it caused him loss.
His brother was becoming upset with him. When the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} saw
this, he said not to be angry with him, because modesty is part of faith.
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

However, one should not be shy in understanding religion, speaking the truth,
enjoining good, or forbidding evil. The Quran states that Allāh does not shy
away from stating the truth.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

Once, a female Companion came to ask the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} a question
and prefaced it by saying, "O Messenger of Allāh, Allāh does not shy away from
the truth." ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} said that how excellent were the women
of the Anṣār, for modesty did not prevent them from seeking religious knowledge.
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]


## Mercy (Raḥmah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
رُحَمَآءُ بَيۡنَهُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Merciful among themselves. (Sūrat al-Fatḥ, Q48:29)
\AYAH_END


Showing kindness and good conduct toward one another in relationships is called
mercy.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The one who does not show mercy
will not be shown mercy." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

This means that whoever does not show mercy to others, Allāh will not show mercy
to him. It may also mean that those who do not show mercy to others will not
receive mercy from others.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The Most Merciful shows mercy to those who are
merciful. Show mercy to those on the earth, and the One above the heavens will
show mercy to you." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

A Bedouin once said to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, "You people kiss your
children, but we do not." The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, "What can I do
if Allāh has removed mercy from your heart?" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

Special mercy should be shown to the young. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said,
"Whoever does not show mercy to our young is not from us." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Mercy is not limited to Muslims alone, but applies to all of humanity. The
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that whoever does not show mercy to
people, Allāh will not show mercy to him. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref missing -->

Mercy toward animals is also a teaching of Islam. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
said that if a person shows mercy even to a sacrificial animal, Allāh will show
mercy to him on the Day of Judgment.


## Justice and Excellence (ʿAdl & Iḥsān)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأۡمُرُ بِٱلۡعَدۡلِ وَٱلۡإِحۡسَٰنِ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, Allāh commands justice and excellence. (Sūrat al-Naḥl, Q16:90)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

[First: Justice (ʿAdl)]{.smallcaps}: Justice means that in whatever we say or
do, the weight must always be on the truth. Whether the matter involves a friend
or an enemy, a senior or a junior, benefit or loss, our own affair or someone
else's, justice and truth must never be abandoned under any circumstance. The
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The just will be with Allāh on
pulpits of light---those who are just in their rulings, with their families, and
in all that they are entrusted with." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

A woman from Banū Makhzūm committed theft, and people sought intercession
through Usāmah ibn Zayd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
said, "Do you intercede regarding one of the punishments prescribed by Allāh?"
Then he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} stood and addressed the people, "Indeed, those before
you were destroyed because when a noble person among them stole, they would
leave him, but when a weak person stole, they would carry out the punishment
upon him."

Then he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "By Allāh, if Fāṭimah bint Muḥammad were to
steal, I would cut off her hand." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

From this, it becomes clear that in matters of justice, no one should be
favored, nor should justice be compromised for anyone.

[Second: Excellence (Iḥsān)]{.smallcaps}: Every act of virtue is called
goodness. In justice, no consideration is given to someone's personal benefit or
loss; everyone is given only what is rightfully due. But in _iḥsān_,
consideration is shown beyond what is obligatory.

There are many forms of _iḥsān_: helping someone financially, rescuing someone
from hardship, forgiving faults, suppressing anger, forgiving debts, and every
correct action through which you bring happiness or comfort to others.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allāh has prescribed
excellence (_iḥsān_) in all things. So when you kill, kill in a good manner; and
when you slaughter, slaughter in a good manner. Let one of you sharpen his blade
and give comfort to his animal." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

If someone shows kindness to you, you should also show kindness in return. Allāh
says:

\AYAH_AR_START
هَلۡ جَزَآءُ ٱلۡإِحۡسَٰنِ إِلَّا ٱلۡإِحۡسَٰنُ ٦٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Is the reward for excellence anything but excellence? (Sūrat al-Raḥmān, Q55:60)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and his Companions were on a journey
when they needed water. They encountered a woman carrying two water-skins. They
asked her about water, and she replied that she had none nearby, but was on her
way to her family. They then brought her to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Pour into our vessels." They took water
from her skins, and then, "he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} tied the mouths of the
water-skins, invoked blessings, and the Companions drank and filled their
containers." Afterwards, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Gather for her
(something)." So they collected food for her.

Then he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "We have not diminished your water in the
least; rather, Allāh has given us drink." She returned to her people and said,
"I have met either the greatest magician or the Prophet of Allāh." Later, her
people accepted Islam. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]


## Fulfilling Covenants and Promises (ʿAhd)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَأَوۡفُواْ بِٱلۡعَهۡدِۖ إِنَّ ٱلۡعَهۡدَ كَانَ مَسۡـُٔولٗا ٣٤

And fulfill the covenant; indeed, the covenant will be questioned about. (Sūrat
al-Isrāʾ, Q17:34)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Fulfilling promises is extremely important. The Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The signs of a hypocrite are three: when he speaks, he
lies; when he makes a promise, he breaks it; and when he is entrusted, he
betrays." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref missing? -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} advised that one should not make promises that
one cannot fulfill.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->
<!-- TODO check translit of name -->

ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī al-Ḥamsā' \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "I made a transaction
with the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} before he received revelation. I owed him
something, so I said, 'I will come back to you.' I forgot. Then I remembered

\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, 'O young man, you have caused me hardship. I have been
here waiting for you for three days.'" \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Whether a promise is made to a Muslim or a non-Muslim, as long as it does not
contradict religion, fulfilling it is obligatory.


## Forgiveness (ʿAfw)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START

\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Adopt forgiveness. (Sūrat al-Aʿrāf, Q7:199)
\AYAH_END


Mistakes are part of human nature, and forgiveness should be practiced. If we
forgive others, Allāh will forgive us.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Charity does not decrease wealth,
no one forgives except that Allāh increases him in honor, and no one humbles
himself for Allāh except that Allāh raises him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

Abū Masʿūd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "I was beating a bondservant of mine

power over you than you have over him.' I turned and saw that it was the
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. I said, 'He is free for the sake of

said, "Do not fight for me. I forgive my blood for the sake of reform." He later
passed away from that wound. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]


## Retaliation with Justice

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَمَنِ ٱعۡتَدَىٰ عَلَيۡكُمۡ فَٱعۡتَدُواْ عَلَيۡهِ بِمِثۡلِ مَا ٱعۡتَدَىٰ عَلَيۡكُمۡۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So whoever transgresses against you, respond with the like of what he
transgressed against you. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:194)
\AYAH_END


Two matters are understood from this _āyah_.

[First]{.smallcaps}: If someone commits injustice against us, we have the right
to seek retribution. Repaying wrongdoing with equivalent wrongdoing is a
permitted principle. Forgiving voluntarily is certainly virtuous, but if one
chooses to take retribution, no one may prevent it nor blame it.

[Second]{.smallcaps}: Retaliation must not exceed the injustice committed. One
may respond only to the extent of the wrong suffered. If excess is committed,
then one becomes sinful before Allāh.


## Gentleness and Soft-Heartedness

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَبِمَا رَحۡمَةٖ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ لِنتَ لَهُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
It was by the mercy of Allāh that you were gentle with them. (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān,
Q3:159)
\AYAH_END


A person should adopt gentleness rather than harshness in his dealings. Whatever
is said should be said gently, and whatever is explained should be explained
with ease.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allāh is gentle and loves
gentleness in all matters." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever is deprived of gentleness is deprived of
goodness." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref missing -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that whoever possesses three qualities, Allāh will
shade him under His shade and admit him into Paradise: showing gentleness to the
weak, kindness to parents, and goodness toward servants.

<!-- TODO ref al-Tirmidhī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever shows mercy, even to a small
child, and honors the elderly, is among us." \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

ʿĀ'ishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} reported:

> A group of Jews came to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and said,
> "al-sāmu ʿalaykum" (death be upon you). I understood it and replied, "And upon
> you be death and the curse of Allāh!"

> The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Be gentle, O ʿĀ'ishah.
> Indeed, Allāh loves gentleness in all matters." I said, "O Messenger of Allāh,

> ʿalaykum (and upon you).'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Thus, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} responded appropriately without excess or
harshness.


## Humility (Tawāḍuʿ)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱخۡفِضۡ جَنَاحَكَ لِلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ ٨٨
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And lower your wing to the believers. (Sūrat al-Ḥijr, Q15:88)
\AYAH_END


This _āyah_ shows that Muslims should deal with one another with kindness and
gentleness. One should not do anything that humiliates another Muslim. This
attitude is called humility and humility of the heart.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "No one humbles himself for the
sake of Allāh except that Allāh raises him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Humility does not mean that a person should degrade himself completely or become
insignificant. Rather, it means that arrogance should not develop and that every
person should respect others.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allāh revealed to me that you
should adopt humility, so that no one transgresses against another, and no one
boasts over another." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Fadail Sahabah -->

Once, Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥanafīyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} asked ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} who was best after the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. He
replied, "Abū Bakr." Then "ʿUmar." When asked about himself, he said, "I am only
one of the Muslims." \[Faḍāʾil al-Ṣaḥābah\]

<!-- TODO ref Kitab al-Zuhd -->

Salmān al-Fārsī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was governor of Madāʾin, yet lived so humbly
that people did not recognize him. Once, a man purchased grass and placed the
load on Salmān's head, making him carry it. As he walked, people told the man
that he was a Companion and a governor. The man apologized and asked him to put
the load down, but Salmān replied, "No, I will carry it to your home and unload
it." \[al-Zuhd\]


## Self-Sacrifice (Īthār)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَيُؤۡثِرُونَ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمۡ وَلَوۡ كَانَ بِهِمۡ خَصَاصَةٞۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And they give preference to others over themselves, even though they are in
need. (Sūrat al-Ḥashr, Q59:9)
\AYAH_END


Giving priority to the needs of others over one's own needs is called
self-sacrifice (_īthār_). It means remaining hungry while feeding others,
enduring hardship oneself while providing comfort to others. When the Muslims of
Makkah left their homes and migrated to Madīnah, the Anṣār welcomed them
wholeheartedly. They gave them their homes, gardens, and fields, and endured all
kinds of hardship themselves so that their Muhājir brothers could live in
comfort.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

Once, a hungry man came to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}. At that
time, there was nothing in the Prophet's house except water. The Prophet
Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Who will host this man tonight so that Allāh
may show him mercy?"

A man from the Anṣār said, "I will, O Messenger of Allāh." He took the guest to
his home and said to his wife, "Do you have anything?" She said, "Nothing except
food for the children." He said, "Keep the children busy, and when our guest
enters, put out the lamp and show him that we are eating." So they sat, and the
guest ate while they remained hungry.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->
<!-- TODO review translation: (and greatly pleased) -->

In the morning, he went to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, who said, "Allāh was
amazed (and greatly pleased) with what you both did for your guest last night."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]


## Moderation (Iʿtidāl)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱبۡتَغِ بَيۡنَ ذَٰلِكَ سَبِيلٗا ١١٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And seek a way between the two extremes. (Sūrat al-Isrāʾ, Q17:110)
\AYAH_END


Islam teaches moderation in all matters---no excess, no neglect. Islam teaches
that one should neither go to extremes nor fall into negligence in any matter,
but should follow a balanced path.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->
<!-- TODO review translation -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Do from deeds what you are able,
for Allāh does not become tired until you become tired." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī;
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The most beloved deeds to Allāh are those
that are consistent, even if they are small." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Nasāʾī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Beware of excess in religion, for those before you
were destroyed by excess in religion." \[Sunan al-Nasāʾī\]

<!-- TODO ref missing? -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "How excellent is moderation in wealth, how
excellent is moderation in poverty, and how excellent is moderation in worship."

Generosity is a very noble quality, but even in generosity, Allāh has commanded
moderation, so that one does not give to others in such a way that he himself
becomes needy and is forced to beg.

There is no greater virtue than worship, yet even in worship, moderation is
required so that it does not become excessive to the point that a person becomes
incapable of fulfilling other duties, nor so little that he forgets Allāh.

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

When ʿUthmān ibn Maẓʿūn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} devoted nights to prayer and days to
fasting, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} stopped him and said, "O ʿUthmān ibn
Maẓʿūn, Allāh has not prescribed monasticism for us. Do you not have an example
in me? By Allāh, I am the most fearful of Allāh among you and the most mindful
of Him. I fast, and I break my fast, I pray, and I sleep, and I marry women.
Whoever turns away from my Sunnah is not from me." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO check if father is companion too. ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said to ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr ibn al-ʿĀṣ
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. "Your body has a right over you, your eyes have a right
over you, your wife has a right over you..." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]


## Courage

## Steadfastness (Istiqāmah)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱسۡتَقِمۡ كَمَآ أُمِرۡتَۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So remain steadfast as you have been commanded. (Sūrat al-Shūrá, Q42:15)
\AYAH_END


The meaning of this _āyah_ is that once something is recognized as truth, one
should remain firmly established upon it. Difficulties may arise, opponents may
appear, persecution may occur, and dangers may surround one, but one must endure
everything and never turn away from the truth.

Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} while he was reclining on his cloak in the shade of
the Kaʿbah. We said, 'Will you not seek help for us? Will you not supplicate for
us?' He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, 'Among those before you, a man would be taken
and a trench dug for him, then he would be placed in it and a saw would be
brought and placed on his head and he would be cut into two, yet that would not

Just as a person must be firm in faith and belief, he must also be firm in

## Proclaiming the Truth (Iẓhār al-Ḥaqq)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَٱصۡدَعۡ بِمَا تُؤۡمَرُ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So proclaim openly what you are commanded. (Sūrat al-Ḥijr, Q15:94)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref Ibn Mājah -->

This _āyah_ shows that the truth must be spoken clearly and openly. It is not
correct to conceal the truth out of fear of abuse, harm, or loss of life or
wealth. The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Do not let the fear of
people prevent any of you from speaking the truth when he knows it." \[Sunan Ibn
Mājah\]

insignificant. The Companions asked, "O Messenger of Allāh, how can one of us
consider himself insignificant?" He replied, "He sees a matter concerning Allāh
in which he should speak, yet he does not speak. So Allāh will say to him on the
Day of Resurrection, 'What prevented you from speaking concerning
such-and-such?' He will say, 'Fear of people.' Allāh will say, 'It is Me whom
you should have feared more.'" \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

Abū Dharr al-Ghifārī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "When I accepted Islam, I
went to the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, and he said to me, 'Return to

in Whose Hand is my soul, I will proclaim it among them.' So I went to the
mosque and announced my Islam. The people of Quraysh stood up and beat me until
I fell unconscious..." Then he returned the next day and did the same. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]


## Consultation (Shūrá)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَشَاوِرۡهُمۡ فِي ٱلۡأَمۡرِۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And consult them in matters. (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, Q3:159)
\AYAH_END


In this _āyah_, Allāh commanded the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to
consult the Muslims in matters of governance and other affairs.

Those matters whose rulings have come explicitly from Allāh do not require
consultation. However, in other matters, consultation is extremely necessary and
highly commendable.

When this _āyah_ was revealed, the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said
that Allāh and His Messenger are not in need of consultation, but Allāh issued
this command out of mercy for the Ummah. Whoever consults will not lose the
truth, and whoever abandons consultation will not be safe from error---meaning
that the risk of mistake is greater.

## Asking the People of Knowledge

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَسۡـَٔلُوٓاْ أَهۡلَ ٱلذِّكۡرِ إِن كُنتُمۡ لَا تَعۡلَمُونَ ٤٣
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So ask the people of knowledge if you do not know. (Sūrat al-Naḥl, Q16:43)
\AYAH_END


No one knows everything. When something is unknown, one must ask those who know,
without embarrassment.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

ʿĀʾishah bint Abī Bakr \hon{\P_TWO}{\BODY} said, "How excellent were the
women of the Anṣār; shyness did not prevent them from gaining understanding of
the religion." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

This _āyah_ also shows that a person should not fall short in acquiring
knowledge, but should make every effort to learn what he does not know.

<!-- TODO ref Ibn Mājah -->

The Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Seeking knowledge is obligatory upon
every Muslim." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Tirmidhī -->

is like my superiority over the least of you." Then he said, "Indeed, Allāh, His
Angels, the inhabitants of the heavens and the earth, even the ant in its hole
and the fish, send blessings upon those who teach people goodness." \[Sunan
al-Tirmidhī\]

<!-- TODO ref Sunan al-Darimi -->

engaged in supplication and worship, and the other engaged in learning and
teaching religion. He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said that both were upon goodness, but one
was superior to the other. He said that those making supplication may or may not
be granted by Allāh, whereas those learning and teaching knowledge benefit
