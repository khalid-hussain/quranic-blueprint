
# Good Deeds

## Truthfulness

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَكُونُواْ مَعَ ٱلصَّٰدِقِينَ ١١٩
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And be with those who are truthful. (Sūrat al-Tawbah, Q9:119)
\AYAH_END


Truthfulness brings peace to the heart. A believer should speak the truth in all
circumstances, whether in hardship or ease, and should act truthfully.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was asked about the distinguishing quality
of the people of Paradise. He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Truthfulness leads to
righteousness, and righteousness leads to Paradise. A man continues to speak the
truth until he is recorded with Allah as a ṣiddīq." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Speaking the truth before a tyrannical ruler is the greatest form of jihād. The
Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The best jihād is a word of truth spoken before a
tyrannical ruler." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

In speaking the truth, one should not favor relatives, close ones, friends, or
even fear enemies.

Once, the Companion Qudāmah ibn Maẓʿūn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} committed an
offense of consuming alcohol. When ʿUmar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} intended to
carry out the legal punishment, Qudāmah's wife testified truthfully against him.
She did not favor her husband over the truth. \[Muwaṭṭaʾ Mālik\] She did not
give preference to her husband over the truth, but upheld honesty even when it
was difficult.

Abandoning truth for a small worldly benefit and earning the displeasure of
Allah is not the conduct of people of intellect. Truthfulness is:

- A mark of īmān.
- A path to righteousness.
- A means to eternal success.

## Generosity and Charity

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ أَنفِقُواْ مِمَّا رَزَقۡنَٰكُم
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O you who believe! Spend from what We have provided for you. (Sūrat al-Baqarah,
Q2:254)
\AYAH_END


Whatever Allah has granted a servant from His blessings, he should give from it
to those who have not received it or have received less. This is what is called
sakhāwah (generosity) and fayyāḍiyyah (magnanimity).

Generosity manifests in many forms, including:

- Forgiving one's rightful dues for others.
- Giving surplus wealth to others.
- Giving despite one's own need.
- Preferring others over oneself (īthār).
- Spending one's physical strength in helping others.
- Using one's intellect for the benefit of others.
- Enduring personal risk to protect others or uphold truth.
- Even sacrificing one's life in the path of justice.

Thus, generosity is not limited to wealth; it encompasses every form of selfless
giving.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Do not tie up (your wealth),
otherwise it will be tied up from you." Meaning: if you withhold from others,
Allah will withhold from you.

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} once asked the Companions, "Which of you loves his
own wealth more than the wealth of his heirs?" They replied, "None of us
considers the wealth of his heirs more beloved than his own."

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Your wealth is what you send ahead, and what you leave
behind belongs to your heirs." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Envy (ghibṭah) is not permissible except in
two cases:

1.  A person whom Allah has given wealth, and he spends it rightly.
2.  A person whom Allah has given knowledge, and he acts upon it and teaches
    it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

A man asked which charity is the greatest. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
replied, "That you give charity while you are healthy, desirous of wealth, and
hopeful of living, and not delay it until the soul reaches the throat, then you
say: 'Give such-and-such to so-and-so,' while it has already become the property
of your heirs." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## A Goodly Loan (Qard Ḥasan)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱلۡمُصَّدِّقِينَ وَٱلۡمُصَّدِّقَٰتِ وَأَقۡرَضُواْ ٱللَّهَ قَرۡضًا حَسَنٗا يُضَٰعَفُ لَهُمۡ وَلَهُمۡ أَجۡرٞ كَرِيمٞ ١٨
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, those men and women who give in charity and lend to Allah a good loan
will have it multiplied for them, and they will have an honourable reward.
(Sūrat al-Ḥadīd, Q57:18)
\AYAH_END


When someone shows kindness to us in this world, we return that kindness.
However, the favors of Allah upon us can never be repaid. Therefore, the way to
"lend to Allah" is to serve His servants---to assist those in need and support
beneficial causes.

Clearly, Allah is not in need of any loan. Rather, giving a "loan to Allah"
means helping His needy servants and spending in paths that are pleasing to Him.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that on the Day of Judgment, Allah
will say, "O son of Ādam! I was ill, and you did not visit Me." The servant will
reply that Allah is the Lord of the worlds. Allah will say that His servant was
ill and had he visited him, he would have found Allah with him.

Allah will then say, "O son of Ādam! I asked you for food, and you did not feed
Me." The servant will reply that Allah is the Lord of the worlds. Allah will say
that His servant asked for food and had he fed him, he would have found his
reward with Allah.

Allah will then say, "O son of Ādam! I asked you for water, and you did not give
Me water." The servant will reply that Allah is the Lord of the worlds. Allah
will say that His servant asked for water and had he given it, he would have
found it with Allah. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Loans and Debt

Allah Says,

<!-- TODO: The complete āyah is not quoted. -->

\AYAH_AR_START
وَإِن كَانَ ذُو عُسۡرَةٖ فَنَظِرَةٌ إِلَىٰ مَيۡسَرَةٖۚ وَأَن تَصَدَّقُواْ خَيۡرٞ لَّكُمۡ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And if the debtor is in difficulty, then grant him time until ease; and if you
remit it as charity, that is better for you. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:280)
\AYAH_END


People take loans from one another in times of need, but a person should strive
to repay their debt as soon as possible. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The
best among you are those who are best in repaying debts." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

When al-Zubayr \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} went out for the Battle of the Camel,
he called his son and said, "I believe I may be wronged and killed today. What
worries me most is my debt. Sell our property and repay my debts first; and if
anything remains, seek Allah's help." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Because of this Quranic command, the Companions would grant respite to debtors.
A man owed Qatādah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} money. Whenever Qatādah came to
collect, the man would hide. One day, he was found and admitted to his hardship.
Qatādah wept and narrated that the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Whoever grants
respite to a debtor in difficulty or forgives him, Allah will shade him under
His Throne on the Day when there is no shade but His." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Companions not only granted respite, but they would also forgive debts. A
man owed money to Abū al-Yasar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and was extremely poor.
Abū al-Yasar said to him, "Go, your debt is forgiven." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Once, a funeral was brought in, for which the deceased owed three dīnārs. The
Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} refused to lead the funeral prayer. Abū
Qatādah al-Anṣārī said, "O Messenger of Allah, I will take responsibility for
his debt." Then the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} led the funeral prayer. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

## Trustworthiness (Amānah)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأۡمُرُكُمۡ أَن تُؤَدُّواْ ٱلۡأَمَٰنَٰتِ إِلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهَا
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, Allah commands you to deliver trusts to their rightful owners. (Sūrat
al-Nisāʾ, Q4:58)
\AYAH_END


Amānah (trust) means that a person conducts his dealings with complete honesty
and integrity, giving everyone their due fully and precisely. This includes:

**Returning Entrusted Property**

If someone entrusts you with wealth or property, it must be returned exactly as
it was given. The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Render the trust to the
one who entrusted you, and do not betray the one who betrays you." \[Sunan Abī
Dāwūd\]

**Safeguarding Secrets**

If someone shares a secret, it is also an amānah and must not be disclosed. The
Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "When a man speaks to another and then turns away,
it is a trust." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\] To expose such a trust is a sign of
shamelessness and betrayal.

**Give Sincere Advice**

If someone seeks advice, sincere advice should be given. The Messenger of Allah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Allow people to barter amongst themselves, but if one of
your brothers seeks advice, then let him offer advice." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

**Leadership Is A Trust**

If a person considers himself unfit for a responsibility, he should withdraw
from it. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said to Abu Dharr al-Ghifari, "O Abū Dharr,
you are weak, and it (leadership) is a trust. On the Day of Resurrection, it
will be a cause of humiliation and regret, except for one who takes it with its
right and fulfills what is due upon him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Such people are called trustworthy. The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was
extremely trustworthy. People generally referred to him as **al-Amīn**, and even
his enemies trusted him. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that betraying a trust
is a sign of deficiency in faith and hypocrisy. He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "There
is no faith for the one who has no trustworthiness, and there is no religion for
the one who does not fulfill his covenant." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} would repay debts with better than what he borrowed
and emphasized justice in all dealings. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## Modesty (Ḥayāʾ)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَيَسۡتَحۡيِۦ مِنكُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
He felt shy before you. (Sūrat al-Aḥzāb, Q33:53)
\AYAH_END


Due to modesty, a person is saved from many evils. The Messenger of Allah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that modesty brings nothing but goodness. A person who
feels no hesitation in committing evil is shameless and immodest. The Prophet
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said: "If you have no modesty, then do whatever you wish."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that faith has over sixty branches, and modesty is
one branch of faith. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was extremely modest. During his
time, there was a man who was excessively shy, to the extent that it caused him
loss. His brother was becoming upset with him. When the Prophet
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} saw this, he said not to be angry with him, because modesty
is part of faith. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

However, one should not be shy in understanding religion, speaking the truth,
enjoining good, or forbidding evil. The Quran states that Allah does not shy
away from stating the truth.

Once, a female Companion came to ask the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} a question
and prefaced it by saying, "O Messenger of Allah, Allah does not shy away from
the truth." 'Ā'ishah said that how excellent were the women of the Anṣār, for
modesty did not prevent them from seeking religious knowledge. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Mercy (Raḥmah)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
رُحَمَآءُ بَيۡنَهُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Merciful among themselves. (Sūrat al-Fatḥ, Q48:29)
\AYAH_END


Showing kindness and good conduct toward one another in relationships is called
mercy.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The one who does not show
mercy will not be shown mercy." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

This means that whoever does not show mercy to others, Allah will not show mercy
to him. It may also mean that those who do not show mercy to others will not
receive mercy from others.

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The Most Merciful shows mercy to those who are
merciful. Show mercy to those on the earth, and the One above the heavens will
show mercy to you." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

A Bedouin once said to the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, "You people kiss your
children, but we do not." The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} replied, "What can I
do if Allah has removed mercy from your heart?" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Special mercy should be shown to the young. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
said, "Whoever does not show mercy to our young is not from us." \[Musnad
Aḥmad\]

Mercy is not limited to Muslims alone, but applies to all of humanity. The
Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that whoever does not show mercy to
people, Allah will not show mercy to him. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Mercy toward animals is also a teaching of Islam. The Prophet
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that if a person shows mercy even to a sacrificial
animal, Allah will show mercy to him on the Day of Judgment.

## Justice and Excellence (ʿAdl & Iḥsān)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ يَأۡمُرُ بِٱلۡعَدۡلِ وَٱلۡإِحۡسَٰنِ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, Allah commands justice and excellence. (Sūrat al-Naḥl, Q16:90)
\AYAH_END


**First: Justice (ʿAdl).**

Justice means that in whatever we say or do, the weight must always be on truth.
Whether the matter involves a friend or an enemy, a senior or a junior, benefit
or loss, our own affair or someone else's, justice and truth must never be
abandoned under any circumstance. The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
said, "The just will be with Allah on pulpits of light --- those who are just in
their rulings, with their families, and in all that they are entrusted with."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

A woman from Banū Makhzūm committed theft, and people sought intercession
through Usama ibn Zayd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
said, "Do you intercede regarding one of the punishments prescribed by Allah?"
Then he \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} stood and addressed the people, "Indeed, those
before you were destroyed because when a noble person among them stole, they
would leave him, but when a weak person stole, they would carry out the
punishment upon him."

Then he \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "By Allah, if Fāṭimah bint Muḥammad were to
steal, I would cut off her hand." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

From this, it becomes clear that in matters of justice, no one should be
favored, nor should justice be compromised for anyone.

**Second: Excellence (Iḥsān).**

Every act of virtue is called goodness. In justice, no consideration is given to
someone's personal benefit or loss; everyone is given only what is rightfully
due. But in iḥsān, consideration is shown beyond what is obligatory.

There are many forms of iḥsān: helping someone financially, rescuing someone
from hardship, forgiving faults, suppressing anger, forgiving debts, and every
correct action through which you bring happiness or comfort to others.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allah has prescribed
excellence (iḥsān) in all things. So when you kill, kill in a good manner; and
when you slaughter, slaughter in a good manner. Let one of you sharpen his blade
and give comfort to his animal." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

If someone shows kindness to you, you should also show kindness in return. Allah
says:

\AYAH_AR_START
هَلۡ جَزَآءُ ٱلۡإِحۡسَٰنِ إِلَّا ٱلۡإِحۡسَٰنُ ٦٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Is the reward for excellence anything but excellence? (Sūrat al-Raḥmān, Q55:60)
\AYAH_END


The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and his Companions were on a journey
when they needed water. They encountered a woman carrying two water-skins. They
asked her about water, and she replied that she had none nearby, but was on her
way to her family. They then brought her to the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}.

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Pour into our vessels." They took water
from her skins, and then: "He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} tied the mouths of the
water-skins, invoked blessings, and the Companions drank and filled their
containers." Afterwards, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Gather for her
(something)." So they collected food for her.

Then he \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "We have not diminished your water in the
least; rather, Allah has given us drink." She returned to her people and said,
"I have met either the greatest magician or the Prophet of Allah." Later, her
people accepted Islam. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## Fulfilling Covenants and Promises (ʿAhd)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَأَوۡفُواْ بِٱلۡعَهۡدِۖ إِنَّ ٱلۡعَهۡدَ كَانَ مَسۡـُٔولٗا ٣٤
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And fulfill the covenant; indeed, the covenant will be questioned about. (Sūrat
al-Isrāʾ, Q17:34)
\AYAH_END


Fulfilling promises is extremely important. The Messenger of Allah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The signs of a hypocrite are three: when he speaks, he
lies; when he makes a promise, he breaks it; and when he is entrusted, he
betrays." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} advised that one should not make promises that one
cannot fulfill.

ʿAbdullāh ibn Abī al-Ḥamsā' \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "I made a
transaction with the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} before he received revelation. I
owed him something, so I said, 'I will come back to you.' I forgot. Then I
remembered after three days and came to him, and found him still in that place.
The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, 'O young man, you have caused me hardship. I
have been here waiting for you for three days." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Whether a promise is made to a Muslim or a non-Muslim, as long as it does not
contradict religion, fulfilling it is obligatory.

## Forgiveness (ʿAfw)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
خُذِ ٱلۡعَفۡوَ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Adopt forgiveness. (Sūrat al-Aʿrāf, Q7:199)
\AYAH_END


Mistakes are part of human nature, and forgiveness should be practiced. If we
forgive others, Allah will forgive us.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Charity does not decrease
wealth, no one forgives except that Allah increases him in honor, and no one
humbles himself for Allah except that Allah raises him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Abū Masʿūd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "I was beating a [غلام]{dir="rtl"} of
mine when I heard a voice behind me saying: 'Know, O Abū Masʿūd, that Allah has
more power over you than you have over him.' I turned and saw that it was the
Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. I said, 'He is free for the sake of
Allah.' The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, 'If you had not done so, the Fire
would have touched you.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

ʿUrwah ibn Masʿūd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was calling people to Islam in Ṭāʾif when
he was shot with an arrow during the adhān. His people rushed to fight, but he
said, "Do not fight for me. I forgive my blood for the sake of reform." He later
passed away from that wound. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

## Retaliation with Justice

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَمَنِ ٱعۡتَدَىٰ عَلَيۡكُمۡ فَٱعۡتَدُواْ عَلَيۡهِ بِمِثۡلِ مَا ٱعۡتَدَىٰ عَلَيۡكُمۡۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So whoever transgresses against you, respond with the like of what he
transgressed against you. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:194)
\AYAH_END


Two matters are understood from this āyah.

First: If someone commits injustice against us, we have the right to seek
retribution. Repaying wrongdoing with equivalent wrongdoing is a permitted
principle. Forgiving voluntarily is certainly virtuous, but if one chooses to
take retribution, no one may prevent it nor blame it.

Second: Retaliation must not exceed the injustice committed. One may respond
only to the extent of the wrong suffered. If excess is committed, then one
becomes sinful before Allah.

## Gentleness and Soft-Heartedness

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَبِمَا رَحۡمَةٖ مِّنَ ٱللَّهِ لِنتَ لَهُمۡۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
It was by the mercy of Allah that you were gentle with them. (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān,
Q3:159)
\AYAH_END


A person should adopt gentleness rather than harshness in his dealings. Whatever
is said should be said gently, and whatever is explained should be explained
with ease.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Allah is gentle and loves
gentleness in all matters." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Whoever is deprived of gentleness is deprived of
goodness." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that whoever possesses three qualities, Allah will
shade him under His shade and admit him into Paradise: showing gentleness to the
weak, kindness to parents, and goodness toward servants.

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Whoever shows mercy, even to a small
child, and honors the elderly, is among us." \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

ʿĀ'ishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} reported: "A group of Jews came to the Messenger of
Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and said, 'al-sāmu ʿalaykum' (death be upon you). I
understood it and said, 'And upon you be death and the curse of Allah!'

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, 'Be gentle, O ʿĀ'ishah. Indeed,
Allah loves gentleness in all matters.' I said, 'O Messenger of Allah, did you
not hear what they said?' He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, 'I said: wa ʿalaykum (and
upon you).'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Thus, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} responded appropriately without excess or
harshness.

## Humility (Tawāḍuʿ)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱخۡفِضۡ جَنَاحَكَ لِلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ ٨٨
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And lower your wing to the believers. (Sūrat al-Ḥijr, Q15:88)
\AYAH_END


This āyah shows that Muslims should deal with one another with kindness and
gentleness. One should not do anything that humiliates another Muslim. This
attitude is called humility and humility of the heart.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "No one humbles himself for the
sake of Allah except that Allah raises him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Humility does not mean that a person should degrade himself completely or become
insignificant. Rather, it means that arrogance should not develop and that every
person should respect others.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Allah revealed to me that you
should adopt humility, so that no one transgresses against another, and no one
boasts over another." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Once, Muḥammad ibn al-Ḥanafiyyah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} asked ʿAlī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} who was best after the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. He
replied: "Abū Bakr." Then "ʿUmar." When asked about himself, he said, "I am only
one of the Muslims." \[Faḍāʾil al-Ṣaḥābah\]

Salmān al-Fārsī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was governor of Madāʾin, yet lived so humbly
that people did not recognize him. Once, a man purchased grass and placed the
load on Salmān's head, making him carry it. As he walked, people told the man
that he was a Companion and a governor. The man apologized and asked him to put
the load down, but Salmān replied, "No, I will carry it to your home and unload
it." \[al-Zuhd\]

## Self-Sacrifice (Īthār)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَيُؤۡثِرُونَ عَلَىٰٓ أَنفُسِهِمۡ وَلَوۡ كَانَ بِهِمۡ خَصَاصَةٞۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And they give preference to others over themselves, even though they are in
need. (Sūrat al-Ḥashr, Q59:9)
\AYAH_END


Giving priority to the needs of others over one's own needs is called
self-sacrifice. It means remaining hungry while feeding others, enduring
hardship oneself while providing comfort to others. When the Muslims of Makkah
left their homes and migrated to Madinah, the Anṣār welcomed them
wholeheartedly. They gave them their homes, gardens, and fields, and endured all
kinds of hardship themselves so that the Muhājir brothers could live in comfort.

Once, a hungry man came to the Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. At that
time, there was nothing in the Prophet's house except water. The Prophet
Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Who will host this man tonight so that Allah
may show him mercy?"

A man from the Anṣār said, "I will, O Messenger of Allah." He took the guest to
his home and said to his wife, "Do you have anything?" She said, "Nothing except
food for the children." He said, "Keep the children busy, and when our guest
enters, put out the lamp and show him that we are eating." So they sat, and the
guest ate while they remained hungry.

In the morning, he went to the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, who said, "Allah was
amazed (and greatly pleased) with what you both did for your guest last night."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Moderation (Iʿtidāl)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱبۡتَغِ بَيۡنَ ذَٰلِكَ سَبِيلٗا ١١٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And seek a way between the two extremes. (Sūrat al-Isrāʾ, Q17:110)
\AYAH_END


Islam teaches moderation in all matters---no excess, no neglect. Islam teaches
that one should neither go to extremes nor fall into negligence in any matter,
but should follow a balanced path.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Do from deeds what you are able,
for Allah does not become tired until you become tired." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī;
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The most beloved deeds to Allah are those
that are consistent, even if they are small." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Beware of excess in religion, for those before you
were destroyed by excess in religion." \[Sunan al-Nasāʾī\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "How excellent is moderation in wealth, how
excellent is moderation in poverty, and how excellent is moderation in worship."

Generosity is a very noble quality, but even in generosity, Allah has commanded
moderation, so that one does not give to others in such a way that he himself
becomes needy and is forced to beg.

There is no greater virtue than worship, yet even in worship, moderation is
required so that it does not become excessive to the point that a person becomes
incapable of fulfilling other duties, nor so little that he forgets Allah.

When ʿUthmān ibn Maẓʿūn \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} devoted nights to prayer and days to
fasting, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} stopped him and said, "O ʿUthmān ibn
Maẓʿūn, Allah has not prescribed monasticism for us. Do you not have an example
in me? By Allah, I am the most fearful of Allah among you and the most mindful
of Him. I fast, and I break my fast, I pray, and I sleep, and I marry women.
Whoever turns away from my Sunnah is not from me." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said to Abdullah ibn Amr ibn al-As
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}. "Your body has a right over you, your eyes have a right
over you, your wife has a right over you..." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Courage

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ إِذَا لَقِيتُمُ ٱلَّذِينَ كَفَرُواْ زَحۡفٗا فَلَا تُوَلُّوهُمُ ٱلۡأَدۡبَارَ ١٥
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! When you meet the disbelievers advancing for battle, do not turn
your backs. (Sūrat al-Anfāl, Q8:15)
\AYAH_END

This commands steadfast courage in the face of the enemy. True courage is
firmness upon truth, not recklessness. Courage includes standing up for justice,
speaking truth before tyrants, and remaining steadfast during trials.

ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Whenever the fighting intensified, and the two
sides met, we would seek protection behind the Prophet Muhammad
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and none of us would be closer to the enemy than him."
\[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Anas ibn Mālik \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
was the best of people, the most generous of people, and the bravest of people.
One night, the people of Madinah were alarmed, so some people went out in the
direction of the sound. The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} met them returning,
having already gone ahead of them. He was on a horse belonging to Abū Ṭalḥah,
with a sword around his neck, and he was saying, 'Do not be afraid, do not be
afraid.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The best jihād is a word of
truth spoken before a tyrannical ruler." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

ʿAlī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Courage is patience at the time of fear."
\[al-Zuhd\]

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} used to supplicate, saying, "O Allah, I
seek refuge in You from cowardice, I seek refuge in You from miserliness..."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The worst traits found in a man are anxious
miserliness and severe cowardice." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The strong believer is better and more beloved to
Allah than the weak believer, while there is good in both..." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Steadfastness (Istiqāmah)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱسۡتَقِمۡ كَمَآ أُمِرۡتَۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So remain steadfast as you have been commanded. (Sūrat al-Shūrā, Q42:15)
\AYAH_END


The meaning of this āyah is that once something is recognized as truth, one
should remain firmly established upon it. Difficulties may arise, opponents may
appear, persecution may occur, and dangers may surround one, but one must endure
everything and never turn away from the truth.

Khabbab ibn al-Aratt \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "We complained to the Prophet
Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} while he was reclining on his cloak in the shade of
the Kaʿbah. We said, 'Will you not seek help for us? Will you not supplicate for
us?' He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, 'Among those before you, a man would be taken
and a trench dug for him, then he would be placed in it and a saw would be
brought and placed on his head and he would be cut into two, yet that would not
turn him away from his religion. His flesh would be combed with iron combs,
separating it from his bones and sinews, yet that would not turn him away from
his religion...'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Bilāl was laid on scorching sand, heavy stones were placed on his chest, and he
was dragged along the ground with a rope around his neck, being told to abandon
Islam. Even then, the only words on his tongue were "Aḥad, Aḥad" (One God, One
God). \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Just as a person must be firm in faith and belief, he must also be firm in
action. Steadfastness means that whatever good deed one adopts should be
practiced consistently in all circumstances, not done sometimes and abandoned at
other times.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The most beloved deeds to Allah
are those that are consistent, even if they are small." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī;
Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Proclaiming the Truth (Iẓhār al-Ḥaqq)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَٱصۡدَعۡ بِمَا تُؤۡمَرُ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So proclaim openly what you are commanded. (Sūrat al-Ḥijr, Q15:94)
\AYAH_END


This āyah shows that the truth must be spoken clearly and openly. It is not
correct to conceal the truth out of fear of abuse, harm, or loss of life or
wealth. The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Do not let the fear of
people prevent any of you from speaking the truth when he knows it." \[Sunan Ibn
Mājah\]

Once, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that no one should consider himself
insignificant. The Companions asked, "O Messenger of Allah, how can one of us
consider himself insignificant?" He replied:

"He sees a matter concerning Allah in which he should speak, yet he does not
speak. So Allah will say to him on the Day of Resurrection: 'What prevented you
from speaking concerning such-and-such?' He will say: 'Fear of people.' Allah
will say: 'It is Me whom you should have feared more.'" \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

Abu Dharr al-Ghifari \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported, "When I accepted Islam,
I went to the Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and he said to me, 'Return to
your people and inform them until my command comes to you.' I said, 'By the One
in Whose Hand is my soul, I will proclaim it among them.' So I went to the
mosque and announced my Islam. The people of Quraysh stood up and beat me until
I fell unconscious..." Then he returned the next day and did the same. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

## Consultation (Shūrā)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَشَاوِرۡهُمۡ فِي ٱلۡأَمۡرِۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And consult them in matters. (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, Q3:159)
\AYAH_END


In this āyah, Allah commanded the Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} to
consult the Muslims in matters of governance and other affairs.

Those matters whose rulings have come explicitly from Allah do not require
consultation. However, in other matters, consultation is extremely necessary and
highly commendable.

When this āyah was revealed, the Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said
that Allah and His Messenger are not in need of consultation, but Allah issued
this command out of mercy for the Ummah. Whoever consults will not lose the
truth, and whoever abandons consultation will not be safe from error---meaning
that the risk of mistake is greater.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} would consult the Companions in
general matters. During the Battle of the Trench, he acted upon the opinion of
Ḥaḍrat Salmān al-Fārsī regarding the digging of the trench. \[Sīrah Ibn Hishām\]

At the Battle of Badr, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} chose a location for
encampment. Hubab ibn al-Mundhir \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "O Messenger of
Allah, is this a place that Allah has shown you, so we cannot advance or retreat
from it, or is it opinion and strategy?" The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
replied, "Rather, it is opinion and strategy."

He said, "This is not a suitable place. Move forward to the nearest water
source..." The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} accepted his opinion and acted upon
it. \[Sīrah Ibn Hishām\]

The Quran praises the believers by saying:

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَأَمۡرُهُمۡ شُورَىٰ بَيۡنَهُمۡ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And their affairs are conducted by mutual consultation. (Sūrat al-Shūrā, Q42:38)
\AYAH_END


The meaning of a hadith of the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} is that the one who
is consulted should give sincere and correct advice.

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that after faith, the greatest wisdom is to deal
with people lovingly. A self-opinionated person cannot manage affairs
successfully, and no one has ever been destroyed due to consultation. If Allah
intends someone's destruction, self-conceit and arrogance are the first things
that ruin him.

## Asking the People of Knowledge

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَسۡـَٔلُوٓاْ أَهۡلَ ٱلذِّكۡرِ إِن كُنتُمۡ لَا تَعۡلَمُونَ ٤٣
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So ask the people of knowledge if you do not know. (Sūrat al-Naḥl, Q16:43)
\AYAH_END


No one knows everything. When something is unknown, one must ask those who know,
without embarrassment.

Aisha bint Abu Bakr \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} said, "How excellent were the
women of the Anṣār; shyness did not prevent them from gaining understanding of
the religion." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

This āyah also shows that a person should not fall short in acquiring knowledge,
but should make every effort to learn what he does not know.

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Seeking knowledge is obligatory upon
every Muslim." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "The superiority of the scholar over the worshipper is
like my superiority over the least of you." Then he said, "Indeed, Allah, His
angels, the inhabitants of the heavens and the earth, even the ant in its hole
and the fish, send blessings upon those who teach people goodness." \[Sunan
al-Tirmidhī\]

Once, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} entered the mosque and saw two groups: one
engaged in supplication and worship, and the other engaged in learning and
teaching religion. He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that both were upon goodness, but one
was superior to the other. He said that those making supplication may or may not
be granted by Allah, whereas those learning and teaching knowledge benefit
others. Saying this, the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} sat with the group engaged in
learning. \[Sunan al-Dārimī\]

## Reconciliation (Ṣulḥ)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّمَا ٱلۡمُؤۡمِنُونَ إِخۡوَةٞ فَأَصۡلِحُواْ بَيۡنَ أَخَوَيۡكُمۡۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, the believers are brothers, so reconcile between your brothers. (Sūrat
al-Ḥujurāt, Q49:10)
\AYAH_END


If a dispute arises among Muslims, it is our duty that all should collectively
restrain the aggressor and compel him toward reconciliation. And when they
incline toward peace, reconciliation must be established with justice.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} frequently brought about
reconciliation among Muslims. Sahl ibn Sa\'d al-Sa\'idi \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
reported, "There was a dispute among the people of Banū ʿAmr ibn ʿAwf, so the
Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was informed about it. He
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} went out with some of his Companions to reconcile between
them." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet Muhammad \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Shall I not inform you of
something more excellent than voluntary fasting, prayer, and charity?" They
said, "Yes, O Messenger of Allah." He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Reconciling
between people. Indeed, corruption and discord between people is the shaver---it
shaves away the religion." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "He is not a liar who reconciles between people,
conveying good or saying what is good." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī; Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]
