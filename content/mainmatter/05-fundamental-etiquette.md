
# Fundamental Etiquettes

## Seeking a Livelihood

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَٱنتَشِرُواْ فِي ٱلۡأَرۡضِ وَٱبۡتَغُواْ مِن فَضۡلِ ٱللَّهِ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Then disperse throughout the land and seek the bounty of Allāh. (Sūrat
al-Jumuʿah, Q62:10)
\AYAH_END


Allāh's bounty refers to lawful provision. Seeking lawful earnings is itself
obedience and worship.

Earning through labor, trade, farming, or employment---by any lawful means---is
necessary to fulfill family obligations and to spend in the path of Allāh.

<!-- TODO ref Shu3ab al-Iman -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "After the obligatory duties, seeking
lawful sustenance is an obligation." \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

<!-- TODO ref Isabah -->

The Companions all worked: Abū Bakr, ʿUmar, and ʿUthmān engaged in trade; Salmān
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} wove mats; one Companion worked with a pickaxe until his
hands became blackened. When the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} saw his hands, he
asked about them. The Companion replied that he labored to provide for his
family. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} kissed his hands. \[al-Iṣābah\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Begging is not permissible for a wealthy or able-bodied person. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "No one has ever eaten better food than that which he
earns with his own hands." In another place he \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "It is
better for one of you to carry a bundle of firewood on his back than to ask
people..." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref missing -->

Some female Companions earned by weaving cloth.

Those who are capable yet remain idle and depend on charity without necessity
should fear Allāh.


## Etiquette of Purity

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَإِن كُنتُمۡ جُنُبٗا فَٱطَّهَّرُواْۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
If you are in a state of major impurity, then purify yourselves. (Sūrat
al-Māʾidah, Q5:6)
\AYAH_END


Maintaining purity and cleanliness is of immense importance. The very foundation
of the religion rests upon purity.

Once, Prophet Muḥammad \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} passed by a grave and said that the person
within it was being punished because he did not protect himself from drops of
urine (i.e. neglected cleanliness after relieving himself).

ʿUthmān \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was so particular about cleanliness that after
accepting Islam, he would generally perform _ghusl_ (ritual bathing) once daily.

### 1. Washing Hands After Sleep {.unnumbered}

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "When one of you awakens from
sleep, he should not put his hand into a vessel until he washes it three times,
for he does not know where his hand spent the night." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

### 2. Cleaning the Teeth (Siwāk) {.unnumbered}

<!-- TODO al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO previously used tooth-stick -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "If it were not that I would
cause hardship for my Ummah, I would have commanded them to use the siwāk at
every prayer." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

### 3. Avoiding Public Harm {.unnumbered}

<!-- TODO review translation -->

It is instructed that one should not relieve oneself on pathways or in the shade
of trees, as it harms others.

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Beware of the two matters that
cause curses: relieving oneself on pathways or in places of shade." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim\]

### 4. Avoid Urinating in Bathing Areas {.unnumbered}

It is prohibited to urinate in the place where one bathes, as it leads to
impurity and harm.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "None of you should urinate in
the place where he bathes..." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

### 5. Istinjā' (Cleaning After Relieving Oneself) {.unnumbered}

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

It should be performed with the left hand. And cleansing should be done using
water and (traditionally) earth or similar material. This is established from
multiple narrations describing the practice of the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}.
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

### 6. Weekly Ghusl and Personal Care {.unnumbered}

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "It is a duty upon every Muslim to perform
_ghusl_ once every seven days, washing his head and body." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Additionally: Wearing clean clothes and applying fragrance are from the
recommended etiquettes of a believer. Some scholars have even considered this
obligatory for Jumuʿah.


## Etiquette of Dress

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
قَدۡ أَنزَلۡنَا عَلَيۡكُمۡ لِبَاسٗا يُوَٰرِي سَوۡءَٰتِكُمۡ وَرِيشٗاۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
We have sent down clothing to cover your nakedness and as adornment. (Sūrat
al-Aʿrāf, Q7:26)
\AYAH_END


Clothing serves two purposes:

1.  To protect the body from heat and cold.
2.  To cover those parts of the body that must not be exposed to others.

<!-- TODO footnote for _ʿawrah_? -->

For men, the area from the navel to the knees is _ʿawrah_.

For women, the body from the head down to the ankles is _ʿawrah_.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

Exposing these parts before others is impermissible, and even in private it is
disliked. A Companion asked the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, "If we
are alone, may we uncover ourselves?" He replied, "Allāh is more deserving that
you feel shy before Him." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Tirmidhī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Never remain naked, for angels are with
you who leave you only at times of relieving oneself and intimacy. Be shy before
them." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

<!-- TODO ref multiple books -->

Further guidelines:

- Men should not wear pure silk without necessity. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

- Men must not imitate women in dress, nor women imitate men. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
  al-Bukhārī\]

- Men should not let garments hang below the ankles; women may lengthen their
  garments. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī and Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

- Clothes should not be so thin that the body is visible. The Prophet
  \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} warned of women who are clothed yet naked. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

- Men should avoid flashy colors, especially solid red; striped red is
  permitted. Saffron-dyed garments are disliked. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

- The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} preferred white clothing for men. \[Sunan Abī
  Dāwūd\]

- When wearing clothes with sleeves, one should first insert the right arm.
  \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

When wearing new clothes, the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would say:

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

\HADITH_AR_START
اَلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ الَّذِي كَسَانِي هَذَا وَرَزَقَنِيهِ مِنْ غَيْرِ حَوْلٍ مِنِّي وَلَا قُوَّةٍ.
\HADITH_AR_END

\HADITH_START
"All praise is for Allāh who clothed me with this and provided it for me without
any power or strength from myself." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]
\HADITH_END


## Etiquette of Speech

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَقُولُواْ لِلنَّاسِ حُسۡنٗا
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And speak to people in a good manner. (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:83)
\AYAH_END


This _āyah_ teaches that when people speak to one another, their speech should
be good, gentle, and well-mannered. One's tone should be soft, and harshness
should not enter into conversation. The status of the person being addressed
should be taken into account: elders should be spoken to respectfully, and
younger people with affection and kindness.

One should not utter words that contain mockery, sarcasm, or humiliation. Allāh
Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَلَا تَلۡمِزُوٓاْ أَنفُسَكُمۡ وَلَا تَنَابَزُواْ بِٱلۡأَلۡقَٰبِۖ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Do not defame one another, nor call each other by offensive nicknames. (Sūrat
al-Ḥujurāt, Q49:11)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A believer does not insult,
curse, speak obscenely, or use foul language." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} also said, "Whoever believes in Allāh and the Last Day
should speak good or remain silent." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Ḥārith ibn Hishām asked the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} to give him one piece of
advice to hold firmly to. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} pointed to his tongue
and said, "Restrain this." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A good word is charity." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Indecent speech should be avoided. Speech should be truthful, just, and calm.
One should not rush when speaking.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would speak slowly and distinctly---so
clearly that if someone wished to count his words, he could do so. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Tirmidhī -->

When addressing a group, one should direct attention to everyone, turning toward
them gradually so that no one feels neglected. \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

Speech should be concise. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "I have been
commanded to be concise in speech, for brevity is better." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

When women speak to non-_maḥram_ men, their tone should not be soft or alluring.
(Sūrat al-Aḥzāb, Q33:32)

Speech should be low and calm; shouting without need is foolishness. Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱغۡضُضۡ مِن صَوۡتِكَۚ إِنَّ أَنكَرَ ٱلۡأَصۡوَٰتِ لَصَوۡتُ ٱلۡحَمِيرِ ١٩
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Lower your voice; indeed, the harshest of voices is the voice of donkeys. (Sūrat
Luqmān, Q31:19)
\AYAH_END


The speech of the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} was gentle and pleasant,
delivered with clarity and calmness.


## Etiquette of Greeting (Salām)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَإِذَا حُيِّيتُم بِتَحِيَّةٖ فَحَيُّواْ بِأَحۡسَنَ مِنۡهَآ أَوۡ رُدُّوهَآۗ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
When you are greeted with a greeting, respond with one better than it or at
least return it equally. (Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, Q4:86)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

When Muslims meet, they should say, "As-salāmu ʿalaykum" (Peace be upon you).
This spreads blessings and increases love between hearts. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "You will not enter Paradise until you believe, and you
will not truly believe until you love one another. Shall I tell you something
which, if you do it, you will love one another? Spread _salām_ among
yourselves." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

_Salām_ should be offered regardless of whether the person is known or unknown,
young or old. However, etiquette dictates that the younger greets the elder, the
one walking greets the one sitting, a smaller group greets a larger group, and
the rider greets the pedestrian.

Upon entering one's home, one should greet one's family with _salām_. When
leaving a gathering, _salām_ should also be given. This ensures that
interactions both begin and end with peace and goodwill.

It is obligatory to reply to _salām_, either equally or with a better greeting.
This means that the wordings with which one is greeted should be increased and
returned in a better manner, thereby increasing in goodness and reward.


## Etiquette of Gatherings (Ādāb al-Majālis)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوٓاْ إِذَا قِيلَ لَكُمۡ تَفَسَّحُواْ فِي ٱلۡمَجَٰلِسِ فَٱفۡسَحُواْ يَفۡسَحِ ٱللَّهُ لَكُمۡۖ وَإِذَا قِيلَ ٱنشُزُواْ فَٱنشُزُواْ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers, when you are told to make space in gatherings, then make space;
Allāh will make space for you. And when you are told to rise, then rise.
(Sūrat al-Mujādilah, Q58:11)
\AYAH_END


This _āyah_ refers to the proper etiquette of sitting in gatherings, as taught
in the Quran. Often, people try to sit specifically in places that carry some
form of distinction or status. In such situations, space becomes constrained,
and others are inconvenienced. If they are asked to make room for others, they
may take offense. Therefore, Allāh has taught proper manners---that one should
sit with openness and make space for others in gatherings.

The practice of the Companions was that they would sit wherever they found space
without formality. They would not push through people to sit in front or occupy
prominent positions.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

One should not make another person stand up to take their place. The Prophet
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "A man should not make another man stand from his place
and then sit in it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

If someone temporarily leaves their seat and then returns, they are more
entitled to it. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "If one of you gets up from
his place and then returns to it, then he has more right to it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

One should not attempt to sit in places of distinction or honor within a
gathering. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} warned against seeking prominence,
"Whoever loves that people stand for him, let him take his seat in the Fire."
\[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

When visiting someone, one should not sit in their designated or honored place
without permission. This falls under the general command of respect and seeking
permission: "Do not enter houses other than your own until you seek
permission..." (Sūrat al-Nūr, Q24:27)


## Etiquette of Entering Homes

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ لَا تَدۡخُلُواْ بُيُوتًا غَيۡرَ بُيُوتِكُمۡ حَتَّىٰ تَسۡتَأۡنِسُواْ وَتُسَلِّمُواْ عَلَىٰٓ أَهۡلِهَاۚ ذَٰلِكُمۡ خَيۡرٞ لَّكُمۡ لَعَلَّكُمۡ تَذَكَّرُونَ ٢٧فَإِن لَّمۡ تَجِدُواْ فِيهَآ أَحَدٗا فَلَا تَدۡخُلُوهَا حَتَّىٰ يُؤۡذَنَ لَكُمۡۖ وَإِن قِيلَ لَكُمُ ٱرۡجِعُواْ فَٱرۡجِعُواْۖ هُوَ أَزۡكَىٰ لَكُمۡۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Do not enter houses other than your own until you have sought
permission and greeted their occupants. That is better for you, so that you may
take heed. And if you find no one therein, do not enter until permission is
given to you. And if it is said to you, "Go back," then go back; that is purer
for you. (Sūrat al-Nūr, Q24:27--28)
\AYAH_END


There is great wisdom in seeking permission before entering someone's home. The
primary reason is that at times a person may be in a condition in which they do
not wish to be seen by others.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

When the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} went to someone's house, he would
stand to the right or left of the door before seeking permission, and would not
stand directly in front, so that his gaze would not fall upon the interior of
the house. It is reported, "Whenever the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} came to a
door, he would not stand facing it, but would stand to its right or left..."
\[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

Once, a man came and stood directly in front of the Prophet's \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
door. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said to him to stand to the right or left
of the door, because permission was prescribed so that the inside of the house
is not looked into. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref Adab Mufrad -->

The correct method of seeking permission is to offer _salām_ and then ask, "May
I enter?" A man asked, "Should I seek permission from my mother?" He
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} replied, "Yes." \[Al-Adab al-Mufrad\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->
<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

If permission is not granted after offering _salām_ three times, one should
return. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] However, if a person has already been invited, then
permission is not required. The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "The messenger
sent to invite a man is his permission." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO review translation of ff para -->

There is no need to seek permission when entering shops or similar public
places. Even when entering one's own house, one should enter with _salām_. Allāh
says, "When you enter houses, greet one another with a greeting from Allāh..."
(Sūrat al-Nūr, Q24:61)

Some people would face hardship if required to seek permission repeatedly, such
as small children or male and female servants, who frequently come and go in the
house. For them, this ruling does not apply generally. However, there are three
times when even they must seek permission:

<!-- TODO footnote for references? -->

1. After the night prayer (ʿIshāʾ)
2. Before the dawn prayer (Fajr)
3. At midday (when people rest)


## Etiquette of Mourning

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِذَآ أَصَٰبَتۡهُم مُّصِيبَةٞ قَالُوٓاْ إِنَّا لِلَّهِ وَإِنَّآ إِلَيۡهِ رَٰجِعُونَ ١٥٦
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And when a calamity befalls them, they say, "Indeed we belong to Allāh, and to
Him we shall return." (Sūrat al-Baqarah, Q2:156)
\AYAH_END


When a calamity strikes a person, or when someone passes away, one should
exercise patience and supplicate to Allāh. If tears flow due to grief, there is
no harm in it. However, wailing loudly, tearing clothes, and similar actions are
against the teachings of Islam.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever slaps his cheeks, tears
his clothes, and cries in the manner of the Days of Ignorance is not from my
Ummah." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} loved Jaʿfar \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} deeply. When
news of his martyrdom reached him, the women of his household began wailing. The
Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} sent word forbidding them, but they did not stop. He
warned them again, and when they persisted, he said, "Throw dust into their
mouths." \[Musnad Aḥmad\] This expression indicates a strong prohibition of
wailing and lamentation.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} himself wept at the death of his son Ibrāhīm and
said, "The eyes shed tears, and the heart grieves, but we do not say except what
pleases our Lord..." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\] This establishes the balance where
natural grief is permitted, and excessive lamentation is prohibited.

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->

When a death occurs in a Muslim household, it is recommended that relatives,
friends, or neighbors send food to the bereaved family. At the time of Jaʿfar's
martyrdom, the Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} sent food to his household
and said, "Prepare food for the family of Jaʿfar, for there has come to them
that which has preoccupied them." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]
