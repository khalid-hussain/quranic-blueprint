
# Relationship With Allah

## God-Consciousness (Taqwā)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ ٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَ وَلۡتَنظُرۡ نَفۡسٞ مَّا قَدَّمَتۡ لِغَدٖۖ وَٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرُۢ بِمَا تَعۡمَلُونَ ١٨
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Be mindful of Allah and let every soul look to what (deeds) it has
sent forth for tomorrow. And fear Allah, (for) certainly Allah is All-Aware of
what you do. (Sūrat al-Ḥashr, Q59:18)
\AYAH_END


\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ ٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَ حَقَّ تُقَاتِهِۦ وَلَا تَمُوتُنَّ إِلَّا وَأَنتُم مُّسۡلِمُونَ ١٠٢
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Be mindful of Allah in the way He deserves, and do not die except
in (a state of full) submission (to Him). (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, Q3:102)
\AYAH_END


Taqwā is that you act in obedience to Allah upon a light from Allah, hoping for
the reward of Allah; and that you abandon disobedience to Allah upon a light
from Allah, fearing the punishment of Allah.

ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was once asked, "What is taqwā?"
He replied, "Have you not walked upon a thorny path?" The man said, "Yes." ʿUmar
said, "What did you do?" He replied, "I gathered my garments tightly and walked
carefully." ʿUmar said, "That is taqwā." \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Taqwā is fearing the Most
Majestic (Allah), acting upon the Revelation, being content with little, and
preparing for the Day of Departure." \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

True honor in the sight of Allah is based on taqwā. The Quran states that the
most honored of people before Allah is the one who possesses the greatest taqwā.

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Taqwā has three levels:

1.  The first is the vigilance of the heart and limbs to avoid sins and
    prohibitions.
2.  The second is avoiding disliked matters.
3.  The third is avoiding what is superfluous and of no concern.

The first gives life to the heart. The second grants it soundness and strength.
The third brings it happiness, joy, and delight." \[al-Fawāʾid\]

Similarly, Imam Ibn Rajab al-Ḥanbalī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Taqwā is that
a servant places a protective barrier between himself and that which he fears
and dreads. Thus, taqwā of Allah is that a person places a barrier between
himself and Allah's punishment, anger, and displeasure---by carrying out what He
has commanded and abstaining from what He has forbidden." \[Jāmiʿ al-ʿUlūm wa
al-Ḥikam\]

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said during the Farewell Pilgrimage, "O
people, indeed your Lord is One, and your father is one. There is no superiority
of an Arab over a non-Arab, nor of a non-Arab over an Arab; nor of a white
person over a black person, nor of a black person over a white person---except
by taqwā." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Correct understanding of the
religion is a light that Allah casts into the heart of His servant, and it
increases through having taqwā of Allah and having a sound intention." \[Iʿlām
al-Muwaqqiʿīn\] Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَمَن يَتَّقِ ٱللَّهَ يَجۡعَل لَّهُۥ مَخۡرَجٗا ٢وَيَرۡزُقۡهُ مِنۡ حَيۡثُ لَا يَحۡتَسِبُۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And whoever fears Allah---He will make for him a way out, and provide for him
from where he could never imagine. (Sūrat al-Ṭalāq, Q65:2--3)
\AYAH_END


ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that this āyah is the greatest
āyah in the Quran regarding relief and deliverance. \[Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr\]

Imam Ibn al-Jawzī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "I was afflicted by a distress that
caused me prolonged anxiety, and I sought relief by every possible means but
found none. Then this āyah was brought to my attention, and I realized that
taqwā is the true cause of deliverance from every hardship. When I resolved to
attain taqwā, I found the way out." \[Ṣayd al-Khāṭir\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Just as the affairs of the one who
fears Allah are made easy, the affairs of the one who abandons taqwā are made
difficult." \[Ad-Dāʾ wa ad-Dawāʾ\]

## Sincerity (Ikhlāṣ)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّآ أَنزَلۡنَآ إِلَيۡكَ ٱلۡكِتَٰبَ بِٱلۡحَقِّ فَٱعۡبُدِ ٱللَّهَ مُخۡلِصٗا لَّهُ ٱلدِّينَ ٢
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, We have sent down the Book to you (O Prophet) in truth, so worship
Allah, devoting the religion purely to Him. (Sūrat al-Zumar, Q39:2)
\AYAH_END


Any righteous deed performed solely to obey Allah and seek His pleasure is
called _ikhlāṣ_ (sincerity). In worship and obedience, nothing should be
associated with Allah---whether idols of stone and clay, created beings, or
inner desires such as fame, reputation, prestige, or worldly status.

A deed lacking sincerity carries no weight with Allah. Abū Umāmah al-Bāhilī \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported: A man came to the Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} and
said, "O Messenger of Allah, what do you say about a man who fights seeking
reward and recognition?" The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "He
receives nothing." He repeated the question three times, and the Prophet
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said each time, "He receives nothing." Then the Prophet
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allah does not accept any deed except that
which is done sincerely for Him and by which His Face is sought." \[Sunan
al-Nasāʾī\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "There is nothing more
beneficial for a servant than sincerity toward his Lord in all his affairs,
along with sincerity of resolve. So let him be sincere toward Allah in both his
intention and his action. Allah says: 'Then if they had been true to Allah, it
would have been better for them' (Sūrat Muḥammad, 21).

His happiness lies in the sincerity of resolve and the sincerity of action.
Sincerity of resolve means having firm determination to act without hesitation.
When resolve is sincere, all that remains is the sincerity of action---doing
one's utmost and striving fully, without allowing anything inward or outward to
hold him back. Sincerity of resolve protects him from weakness of will, and
sincerity of action protects him from laziness and lethargy. Whoever is sincere
toward Allah in all his affairs, Allah grants him more than He grants others.
This sincerity arises from true love of Allah and true reliance upon Him. Thus,
the most sincere of people is the one who loves Allah the most and places the
greatest trust in Him." \[al-Fawāʾid\]

The Companions embodied sincerity in every action. It is authentically reported
in the books of Sīrah that al-Walīd ibn al-Walīd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was
among those captured at the Battle of Badr. He later secured his release by
paying a ransom. After his release, he outwardly returned toward Makkah, but
when he reached Dhū al-Ḥulayfah, he turned back, went to the Messenger of Allah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}, and embraced Islam openly. When his brother Khālid ibn al-Walīd
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} later remarked to him that had he accepted Islam
earlier, he would have avoided paying the ransom, al-Walīd replied with a
statement that reflects his sincerity (ikhlāṣ): He said that he deliberately
accepted Islam only after paying the ransom so that no one could claim that he
embraced Islam merely to escape the payment. \[al-Sīrah al-Nabawiyyah\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Ikhlāṣ (sincerity) is when a
servant's inner actions and outward actions are equal. Riyāʾ (showing off) is
when the outward actions are better than what is within the heart. Truthfulness
in sincerity is when the inner state is better and more refined than the outward
actions." \[Madārij al-Sālikīn\]

## Patience and Steadfastness (Ṣabr)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَٱصۡبِرۡ إِنَّ وَعۡدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقّٞۖ وَلَا يَسۡتَخِفَّنَّكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُوقِنُونَ ٦٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So be patient, for the promise of Allah certainly is true. And do not be
disturbed by those who have no sure faith. (Sūrat al-Rūm, Q30:60)
\AYAH_END


Ṣabr refers to inner strength, moral resolve, and steadfast perseverance. The
Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "...And patience is illumination
(light)..." (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

The Quran conveys this meaning in multiple ways, all pointing to the following
realities:

1.  Endure all hardships while remaining firm upon your purpose.
2.  In times of trial and difficulty, do not panic; accept Allah's decree with
    contentment.
3.  Do not allow obstacles or dangers to weaken your resolve.
4.  Forgive those who wrong you and exercise restraint.
5.  Stand firm with courage when facing an enemy.
6.  Control your lower self.
7.  Fulfill religious obligations consistently throughout life with
    steadfastness.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "No one has been given a gift better
and more expansive than patience." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Allah the Exalted says: 'If I test My servant
by taking away his two beloved ones (his eyes) and he remains patient, I will
compensate him with Paradise.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Know that victory comes with
patience, relief comes with distress, and ease comes with hardship." \[Musnad
Aḥmad\]

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "How amazing is the affair of the
believer! Indeed, all of his affairs are good, and this is not the case for
anyone except the believer. If something good happens to him, he is grateful,
and that is good for him. And if something harmful befalls him, he is patient,
and that is good for him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

## Gratitude (Shukr)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱشۡكُرُواْ لِلَّهِ إِن كُنتُمۡ إِيَّاهُ تَعۡبُدُونَ ١٧٢
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And be grateful to Allah if you (truly) worship Him (alone). (Sūrat al-Baqarah,
Q2:172)
\AYAH_END


Our creation, life, sustenance, rest, movement, growth---everything is from
Allah's Mercy. Gratitude toward such a Beneficent Lord is essential.

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Gratitude (shukr) is to manifest
the effect of Allah's blessing upon the tongue through praise and
acknowledgment; in the heart through witnessing and love; and upon the limbs
through submission and obedience." \[Madarīj al-Sālikīn\]

Prophet Sulaymān \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} supplicated, "My Lord, enable me to be
grateful for Your Favor which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents, and
to do righteousness of which You Approve." \[Sūrat al-Naml, 9\]

Muʿādh ibn Jabal \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported that the Messenger of Allah
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} took his hand and said, "O Muʿādh, by Allah, I love you. So do
not leave saying at the end of every prayer: O Allah, help me to remember You,
to thank You, and to worship You in the best manner." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Whoever is granted a heart that is
grateful has been given the best of this world and the Hereafter." \[Musnad
Aḥmad\]

The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allah is pleased with a servant who
eats food and praises Him for it, or drinks a drink and praises Him for it."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

True gratitude means attentiveness to Allah's commands and appreciation of His
blessings:

- Gratitude of the tongue: speaking only what pleases Allah.
- Gratitude of the feet: walking only toward good.
- Gratitude of the eyes: seeing only what is lawful and beneficial.
- Gratitude of the mind: thinking about what benefits this world and the
  Hereafter.
- Gratitude of the heart: filling it with love and remembrance of Allah.
- Gratitude of wealth, strength, and health: using them to seek Allah's
  pleasure.

In essence, gratitude for every blessing lies in using it for the purpose for
which Allah created it.

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} said: The Prophet \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} would
stand in prayer at night until his feet became swollen. I said, "Why do you do
this when Allah has forgiven your past and future sins?" He \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
replied, "Should I not be a grateful servant?" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Blessings are tied to
gratitude, and gratitude is tied to increase. The increase from Allah will never
cease unless gratitude from the servant ceases." \[Kitāb al-Shukr\]

Imam Ibn Abī al-Dunyā \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} recorded that one of the wise men
wrote to his brother, "To proceed: O my brother, we awaken amidst blessings from
Allah that we cannot enumerate, despite the abundance of our disobedience to
Him. We do not know which of them deserves more gratitude: the beautiful
blessings He has made apparent, or the ugly deeds He has concealed." \[Kitāb
al-Shukr\]

## Reliance upon Allah (Tawakkul)

Allah Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَتَوَكَّلۡ عَلَى ٱللَّهِۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِٱللَّهِ وَكِيلًا ٨١
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And put your trust in Allah, for Allah is sufficient as a Trustee of Affairs.
(Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, Q4:81)
\AYAH_END


Reliance means that whatever task a person undertakes, he does so with full
resolve and determination, while firmly believing that if there is goodness in
it, success will certainly come by the will of Allah.

Everything in the heavens and the earth is dependent (muḥtāj) upon Allah. That
which is itself dependent cannot be relied upon absolutely. Therefore, those who
truly believe in Allah place their trust in Him alone, and their hearts do not
settle upon anyone besides Him.

The Messenger of Allah \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "If you were to rely upon Allah with
true reliance, He would provide for you just as He provides for the birds: they
go out in the morning with empty stomachs and return in the evening with full
stomachs." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Imam Aḥmad \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Tawakkul is to sever hope from the
creation." \[Ṭabaqāt al-Ḥanābilah\]

Tawakkul does not mean abandoning effort. Anas ibn Mālik [رضي الله
عنه]{dir="rtl"} narrated: A man said, "O Messenger of Allah, should I tie my
camel and rely upon Allah, or leave it untied and rely upon Allah?" He
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} replied, "Tie it and then rely upon Allah." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "It is not from tawakkul to
abandon the means, nor to label helplessness as tawakkul. Rather, tawakkul is
not complete except by taking the means that Allah has established." \[Zād
al-Maʿād\]

From this, it becomes clear that a person must take the necessary means (asbāb)
and then entrust the outcome to Allah. He does what is within his capacity and
leaves the result to divine wisdom.

There is a subtle wisdom in true tawakkul. If success comes, one does not become
arrogant, knowing it is from Allah. If failure occurs, one does not fall into
despair, knowing that Allah chose what is best. Allah says, "And whoever relies
upon Allah, then He is sufficient for him." \[Sūrat al-Ṭalāq Q65:3\]
