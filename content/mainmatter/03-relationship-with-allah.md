
# Relationship With Allāh

## God-Consciousness (Taqwá)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ ٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَ وَلۡتَنظُرۡ نَفۡسٞ مَّا قَدَّمَتۡ لِغَدٖۖ وَٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَۚ إِنَّ ٱللَّهَ خَبِيرُۢ بِمَا تَعۡمَلُونَ ١٨
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Be mindful of Allāh and let every soul look to what (deeds) it has
sent forth for tomorrow. And fear Allāh, (for) certainly Allāh is All-Aware of
what you do. (Sūrat al-Ḥashr, Q59:18)
\AYAH_END


\AYAH_AR_START
يَٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ ٱتَّقُواْ ٱللَّهَ حَقَّ تُقَاتِهِۦ وَلَا تَمُوتُنَّ إِلَّا وَأَنتُم مُّسۡلِمُونَ ١٠٢
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
O believers! Be mindful of Allāh in the way He deserves, and do not die except
in (a state of full) submission (to Him). (Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, Q3:102)
\AYAH_END


_Taqwá_ is that you act in obedience to Allāh upon a light from Allāh, hoping
for the reward of Allāh; and that you abandon disobedience to Allāh upon a light
from Allāh, fearing the punishment of Allāh.

<!-- TODO ref Shu3ab al-Iman -->

ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was once asked, "What is _taqwá_?" He
replied, "Have you not walked upon a thorny path?" The man said, "Yes." ʿUmar
said, "What did you do?" He replied, "I gathered my garments tightly and walked
carefully." ʿUmar said, "That is _taqwá_." \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

<!-- TODO ref Shu3ab al-Iman -->

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Taqwá_ is fearing the Most
Majestic (Allāh), acting upon the Revelation, being content with little, and
preparing for the Day of Departure." \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

True honor in the sight of Allāh is based on _taqwá_. The Quran states that the
most honored of people before Allāh is the one who possesses the greatest
_taqwá_.

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Taqwá_ has three levels:

1.  The first is the vigilance of the heart and limbs to avoid sins and
    prohibitions.
2.  The second is avoiding disliked matters.
3.  The third is avoiding what is superfluous and of no concern.

The first gives life to the heart. The second grants it soundness and strength.
The third brings it happiness, joy, and delight." \[al-Fawāʾid\]

<!-- TODO Jami3 al-Ulum wa al-Hikam -->

Similarly, Imam Ibn Rajab al-Ḥanbalī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Taqwá_ is that
a servant places a protective barrier between himself and that which he fears
and dreads. Thus, _taqwá_ of Allāh is that a person places a barrier between
himself and Allāh's punishment, anger, and displeasure---by carrying out what He
has commanded and abstaining from what He has forbidden." \[Jāmiʿ al-ʿUlūm wa
al-Ḥikam\]

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said during the Farewell Pilgrimage,
"O people, indeed your Lord is One, and your father is one. There is no
superiority of an Arab over a non-Arab, nor of a non-Arab over an Arab; nor of a
white person over a black person, nor of a black person over a white
person---except by _taqwá_." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Correct understanding of the
religion is a light that Allāh casts into the heart of His servant, and it
increases through having _taqwá_ of Allāh and having a sound intention." \[Iʿlām
al-Muwaqqiʿīn\] Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَمَن يَتَّقِ ٱللَّهَ يَجۡعَل لَّهُۥ مَخۡرَجٗا ٢وَيَرۡزُقۡهُ مِنۡ حَيۡثُ لَا يَحۡتَسِبُۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And whoever fears Allāh---He will make for him a way out, and provide for him
from where he could never imagine. (Sūrat al-Ṭalāq, Q65:2--3)
\AYAH_END


<!-- TODO ref Ibn Kathīr -->

ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said that this _āyah_ is the greatest
_āyah_ in the Quran regarding relief and deliverance. \[Tafsīr Ibn Kathīr\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Jawzi -->

Imam Ibn al-Jawzī \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "I was afflicted by a distress that
caused me prolonged anxiety, and I sought relief by every possible means but
found none. Then this _āyah_ was brought to my attention, and I realized that
_taqwá_ is the true cause of deliverance from every hardship. When I resolved to
attain _taqwá_, I found the way out." \[Ṣayd al-Khāṭir\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Just as the affairs of the one who
fears Allāh are made easy, the affairs of the one who abandons _taqwá_ are made
difficult." \[Ad-Dāʾ wa ad-Dawāʾ\]


## Sincerity (Ikhlāṣ)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
إِنَّآ أَنزَلۡنَآ إِلَيۡكَ ٱلۡكِتَٰبَ بِٱلۡحَقِّ فَٱعۡبُدِ ٱللَّهَ مُخۡلِصٗا لَّهُ ٱلدِّينَ ٢
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
Indeed, We have sent down the Book to you (O Prophet) in truth, so worship
Allāh, devoting the religion purely to Him. (Sūrat al-Zumar, Q39:2)
\AYAH_END


Any righteous deed performed solely to obey Allāh and seek His pleasure is
called _ikhlāṣ_ (sincerity). In worship and obedience, nothing should be
associated with Allāh---whether idols of stone and clay, created beings, or
inner desires such as fame, reputation, prestige, or worldly status.

A deed lacking sincerity carries no weight with Allāh. Abū Umāmah al-Bāhilī
\hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported,

> A man came to the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} and said, "O Messenger of Allāh,
> what do you say about a man who fights seeking reward and recognition?" The
> Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "He receives nothing." He repeated
> the question three times, and the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said each time,
> "He receives nothing." Then the Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Indeed,
> Allāh does not accept any deed except that which is done sincerely for Him and
> by which His Face is sought." \[Sunan al-Nasāʾī\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said,

> There is nothing more beneficial for a servant than sincerity toward his Lord
> in all his affairs, along with sincerity of resolve. So let him be sincere
> toward Allāh in both his intention and his action. Allāh says, "Then if they
> had been true to Allāh, it would have been better for them." (Sūrat Muḥammad,
> Q47:21)

> His happiness lies in the sincerity of resolve and the sincerity of action.
> Sincerity of resolve means having firm determination to act without
> hesitation. When resolve is sincere, all that remains is the sincerity of
> action---doing one's utmost and striving fully, without allowing anything
> inward or outward to hold him back. Sincerity of resolve protects him from
> weakness of will, and sincerity of action protects him from laziness and
> lethargy. Whoever is sincere toward Allāh in all his affairs, Allāh grants him
> more than He grants others. This sincerity arises from true love of Allāh and
> true reliance upon Him. Thus, the most sincere of people is the one who loves
> Allāh the most and places the greatest trust in Him. \[al-Fawāʾid\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn Hisham? -->
<!-- TODO outwardly returned? -->

The Companions embodied sincerity in every action. It is authentically reported
in the books of Sīrah that al-Walīd ibn al-Walīd \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} was among
those captured at the Battle of Badr. He later secured his release by paying a
ransom. After his release, he outwardly returned toward Makkah, but when he
reached Dhū al-Ḥulayfah, he turned back, went to the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}, and embraced Islam openly. When his brother Khālid ibn
al-Walīd \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} later remarked to him that had he accepted Islam
earlier, he would have avoided paying the ransom, al-Walīd replied with a
statement that reflects his sincerity (_ikhlāṣ_): He said that he deliberately
accepted Islam only after paying the ransom so that no one could claim that he
embraced Islam merely to escape the payment. \[al-Sīrah al-Nabawiyyah\]

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->
<!-- TODO check translit of Riya -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Ikhlāṣ_ (sincerity) is when a
servant's inner actions and outward actions are equal. _Riyāʾ_ (showing off) is
when the outward actions are better than what is within the heart. Truthfulness
in sincerity is when the inner state is better and more refined than the outward
actions." \[Madārij al-Sālikīn\]


## Patience and Steadfastness (Ṣabr)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
فَٱصۡبِرۡ إِنَّ وَعۡدَ ٱللَّهِ حَقّٞۖ وَلَا يَسۡتَخِفَّنَّكَ ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يُوقِنُونَ ٦٠
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
So be patient, for the promise of Allāh certainly is true. And do not be
disturbed by those who have no sure faith. (Sūrat al-Rūm, Q30:60)
\AYAH_END

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

_Ṣabr_ refers to inner strength, moral resolve, and steadfast perseverance. The
Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "...And patience is illumination
(light)..." (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim)

The Quran conveys this meaning in multiple ways, all pointing to the following
realities:

1.  Endure all hardships while remaining firm upon your purpose.
2.  In times of trial and difficulty, do not panic; accept Allāh's decree with
    contentment.
3.  Do not allow obstacles or dangers to weaken your resolve.
4.  Forgive those who wrong you and exercise restraint.
5.  Stand firm with courage when facing an enemy.
6.  Control your lower self.
7.  Fulfill religious obligations consistently throughout life with
    steadfastness.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "No one has been given a gift
better and more expansive than patience." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Allāh the Exalted says, 'If I test My
servant by taking away his two beloved ones (his eyes) and he remains patient, I
will compensate him with Paradise.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Know that victory comes with
patience, relief comes with distress, and ease comes with hardship." \[Musnad
Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "How amazing is the affair of the
believer! Indeed, all of his affairs are good, and this is not the case for
anyone except the believer. If something good happens to him, he is grateful,
and that is good for him. And if something harmful befalls him, he is patient,
and that is good for him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]


## Gratitude (Shukr)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَٱشۡكُرُواْ لِلَّهِ إِن كُنتُمۡ إِيَّاهُ تَعۡبُدُونَ ١٧٢
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And be grateful to Allāh if you (truly) worship Him (alone). (Sūrat al-Baqarah,
Q2:172)
\AYAH_END


Our creation, life, sustenance, rest, movement, growth---everything is from
Allāh's Mercy. Gratitude toward such a Beneficent Lord is essential.

<!-- TODO ref Ibn al-Qayyim -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Shukr_ (gratitude) is to manifest
the effect of Allāh's blessing upon the tongue through praise and
acknowledgment; in the heart through witnessing and love; and upon the limbs
through submission and obedience." \[Madarīj al-Sālikīn\]

<!-- TODO add ayah? -->

Prophet Sulaymān \hon{\S_HIM}{\BODY} supplicated, "My Lord, enable me to be
grateful for Your Favor which You have bestowed upon me and upon my parents, and
to do righteousness of which You Approve." \[Sūrat al-Naml, Q27:9\]

<!-- TODO ref Abū Dāwūd -->
<!-- TODO add Arabic? -->

Muʿādh ibn Jabal \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} reported that the Messenger of Allāh
\hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} took his hand and said, "O Muʿādh, by Allāh, I love you. So
do not leave saying at the end of every prayer, 'O Allāh, help me to remember
You, to thank You, and to worship You in the best manner.'" \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Whoever is granted a heart that
is grateful has been given the best of this world and the Hereafter." \[Musnad
Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref Muslim -->

The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "Indeed, Allāh is pleased with a servant
who eats food and praises Him for it, or drinks a drink and praises Him for it."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

True gratitude means attentiveness to Allāh's commands and appreciation of His
blessings:

- Gratitude of the tongue: speaking only what pleases Allāh.
- Gratitude of the feet: walking only toward good.
- Gratitude of the eyes: seeing only what is lawful and beneficial.
- Gratitude of the mind: thinking about what benefits this world and the
  Hereafter.
- Gratitude of the heart: filling it with love and remembrance of Allāh.
- Gratitude of wealth, strength, and health: using them to seek Allāh's
  pleasure.

In essence, gratitude for every blessing lies in using it for the purpose for
which Allāh created it.

<!-- TODO ref al-Bukhārī -->

ʿĀʾishah \hon{\P_HER}{\BODY} said, "The Prophet \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} would stand
in prayer at night until his feet became swollen. I said, 'Why do you do this
when Allāh has forgiven your past and future sins?' He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
replied, 'Should I not be a grateful servant?'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

<!-- TODO ref Kitab al-Shukr -->

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY} said, "Blessings are tied to gratitude,
and gratitude is tied to increase. The increase from Allāh will never cease
unless gratitude from the servant ceases." \[Kitāb al-Shukr\]

<!-- TODO ref Kitab al-Shukr -->

Imam Ibn Abī al-Dunyā \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} recorded that one of the wise men
wrote to his brother, "To proceed: O my brother, we awaken amidst blessings from
Allāh that we cannot enumerate, despite the abundance of our disobedience to
Him. We do not know which of them deserves more gratitude: the beautiful
blessings He has made apparent, or the ugly deeds He has concealed." \[Kitāb
al-Shukr\]


## Reliance upon Allāh (Tawakkul)

Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَتَوَكَّلۡ عَلَى ٱللَّهِۚ وَكَفَىٰ بِٱللَّهِ وَكِيلًا ٨١
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And put your trust in Allāh, for Allāh is sufficient as a Trustee of Affairs.
(Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, Q4:81)
\AYAH_END


Reliance means that whatever task a person undertakes, he does so with full
resolve and determination, while firmly believing that if there is goodness in
it, success will certainly come by the will of Allāh.

Everything in the heavens and the earth is dependent (_muḥtāj_) on Allāh. That
which is itself dependent cannot be relied upon absolutely. Therefore, those who
truly believe in Allāh place their trust in Him alone, and their hearts do not
settle on anyone besides Him.

<!-- TODO ref Aḥmad; check if this is in the Sahihayn? -->

The Messenger of Allāh \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY} said, "If you were to rely upon Allāh
with true reliance, He would provide for you just as He provides for the birds:
they go out in the morning with empty stomachs and return in the evening with
full stomachs." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

<!-- TODO ref Tabaqat Hanabilah -->

Imam Aḥmad \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "_Tawakkul_ is to sever hope from the
creation." \[Ṭabaqāt al-Ḥanābilah\]

<!-- TODO ref al-Tirmidhī -->

_Tawakkul_ does not mean abandoning effort. Anas ibn Mālik \hon{\P_HIM}{\BODY}
narrated that a man said, "O Messenger of Allāh, should I tie my camel and rely
upon Allāh, or leave it untied and rely upon Allāh?" He \hon{\PBUH}{\BODY}
replied, "Tie it and then rely upon Allāh." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

<!-- TODO ref Zaad Ma3aad -->

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim \hon{\M_HIM}{\BODY} said, "It is not from _tawakkul_ to
abandon the means, nor to label helplessness as _tawakkul_. Rather, _tawakkul_
is not complete except by taking the means that Allāh has established." \[Zād
al-Maʿād\]

From this, it becomes clear that a person must take the necessary means (_asbāb_)
and then entrust the outcome to Allāh. He does what is within his capacity and
leaves the result to divine wisdom.

<!-- TODO add ayah? -->

There is a subtle wisdom in true _tawakkul_. If success comes, one does not
become arrogant, knowing it is from Allāh. If failure occurs, one does not fall
into despair, knowing that Allāh chose what is best. Allāh Says,

\AYAH_AR_START
وَمَن يَتَوَكَّلۡ عَلَى ٱللَّهِ فَهُوَ حَسۡبُهُۥٓۚ
\AYAH_AR_END

\AYAH_START
And whoever relies upon Allāh, then He is sufficient for him. (Sūrat al-Ṭalāq,
Q65:3)
\AYAH_END
