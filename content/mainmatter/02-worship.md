
# Worship (ʿIbādāt)

Allah Says,

[يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱعْبُدُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُمْ وَٱلَّذِينَ مِن
قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ]{dir="rtl"}

*O humanity! Worship your Lord, Who created you and those before you, so that
you may become mindful (of Him).* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah, 21\]

Expressing one's humility before Allah and carrying out His Commands is worship.
A person may perform deeds that appear very good, but if his intention is not to
manifest servitude to Allah and to seek His Pleasure, then that deed is not
worship.

From this, it becomes clear that in order for a good deed to be counted as
worship, it is necessary that it be accompanied by a pure and sincere intention.
If you give someone millions of rupees in order to gain fame for yourself, that
is not worship. But if you give even a few coins to someone seeking the pleasure
of Allah, then that is a great act of worship.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Indeed, actions are only judged by
intentions." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Just as having a good intention is necessary for worship, it is also necessary
that the act be performed according to the method taught by Allah and His
Messenger. Allah Says in the Qurʾān:

[فَاذْكُرُوا اللَّهَ كَمَا عَلَّمَكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*So remember Allah as He has taught you.* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah, 239\]

[قُلْ إِن كُنتُمْ تُحِبُّونَ ٱللَّهَ فَٱتَّبِعُونِى يُحْبِبْكُمُ ٱللَّهُ
وَيَغْفِرْ لَكُمْ ذُنُوبَكُمْ وَٱللَّهُ غَفُورٌۭ رَّحِيم]{dir="rtl"}ٌۭ

*Say, (O Prophet), "If you (sincerely) love Allah, then follow me; Allah will
Love you and Forgive your sins. For Allah is All-Forgiving, Most Merciful."*
\[Surah Aal Imran, 31\]

[قُلْ أَطِيعُوا۟ ٱللَّهَ وَأَطِيعُوا۟ ٱلرَّسُولَ فَإِن تَوَلَّوْا۟ فَإِنَّمَا
عَلَيْهِ مَا حُمِّلَ وَعَلَيْكُم مَّا حُمِّلْتُمْ وَإِن تُطِيعُوهُ تَهْتَدُوا۟
وَمَا عَلَى ٱلرَّسُولِ إِلَّا ٱلْبَلَـٰغُ ٱلْمُبِين]{dir="rtl"}ُ

*Say, "Obey Allah and obey the Messenger. But if you turn away, then he is only
responsible for his duty, and you are responsible for yours. And if you obey
him, you will be (rightly) guided. The Messenger's duty is only to deliver (the
Message) clearly."* \[Surah An-Nur, 54\]

If a person performs even the best of deeds, with the best of intentions, but it
is done in a manner different from that taught by Allah and His Messenger, then
it is not worship.

Among the Companions ([رضي الله عنهم]{dir="rtl"}), some people wished to perform
very strict and severe forms of worship. The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
stopped them and said that he had not been sent with such a form of Sharīʿah.

Anas ibn Mālik [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported: Three men came to the houses
of the wives of the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, asking about the worship of the
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}. When they were informed, it was as though they
considered it little. They said, "Where are we compared to the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}? His past and future sins have been forgiven."

One of them said, "As for me, I will pray all night forever." Another said, "I
will fast continuously and never break my fast." Another said, "I will keep away
from women and never marry."

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} came and said, "Are you the ones who said
such and such? By Allah, I am the one who fears Allah the most among you and am
the most mindful of Him. Yet I fast and I break my fast, I pray, and I sleep,
and I marry women. Whoever turns away from my Sunnah is not from me." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, \"It is important to
know that the pleasure and love of Allāh must not be sought by inflicting
torture/harm on oneself and striving for difficulties. Some ignorant people
believe that the more difficult the deed is, the better it is, and that the
magnitude of the reward always depends on the degree of difficulty. No! This is
an absolutely wrong belief! The magnitude of the reward for the deed depends on
the benefit that it brings.\" \[Majmū al-Fatāwa\]

## Prayer (Ṣalāh)

Allah Says,

[وَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَءَاتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَٱرْكَعُوا۟ مَعَ
ٱلرَّٰكِعِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*Establish the prayer, pay zakāh, and bow down with those who bow down.* \[Sūrat
al-Baqarah, 43\]

Ṣalāh linguistically means dua (supplication). Technically, it means to worship
Allah with statements and actions that are known to start with a takbeer and end
with a tasleem. The 5 daily prayers were legislated on the Night of Isra wal
Mi\'raj 1.5 years before Hijrah. Angel Jibreel [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"} was sent
the next morning, when the sun passed its zenith, to teach the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} the respective times of the prayers.

Among the acts of worship in Islam, ṣalāh holds the highest rank. As long as a
Muslim's senses and awareness are intact, he cannot abandon prayer. The Qurʾān
has placed strong emphasis on it. Laziness in prayer has been described as a
sign of hypocrisy, and abandoning it has been described by the Messenger of
Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} as a sign of disbelief. The Qurʾān warns those who are
heedless in their prayers. When the disbelievers will be asked why they are in
Hell, they will reply that they were among those who did not pray.

Prayer deters a person from shamelessness and immorality. Allah Says,

[ٱتْلُ مَآ أُوحِىَ إِلَيْكَ مِنَ ٱلْكِتَـٰبِ وَأَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ ۖ إِنَّ
ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ تَنْهَىٰ عَنِ ٱلْفَحْشَآءِ وَٱلْمُنكَرِ ۗ وَلَذِكْرُ ٱللَّهِ
أَكْبَرُ ۗ وَٱللَّهُ يَعْلَمُ مَا تَصْنَعُونَ]{dir="rtl"}

*Recite (O Prophet) what is revealed to you of the Book, and establish Salāh.
Surely (genuine) Salāh deters one from indecency and evil acts. Indeed,
remembrance of Allah is (an) even greater (deterrant). And Allah (fully) knows
what you (all) do.* \[Sūrat al-ʿAnkabūt, 45\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The coolness of my eyes has been
placed in prayer." \[Musnad Aḥmad\] He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "Prayer is the
pillar of the religion. Whoever establishes it has established the religion, and
whoever abandons it has demolished the religion." \[Shuʿab al-Īmān\]

When ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was attacked with a dagger
and lay wounded, he was awakened for the Fajr prayer that morning. He said,
"There is no share in Islam for the one who abandons the prayer," and he
performed the prayer in that condition. \[al-Muwaṭṭa'\]

Ḥusayn ibn ʿAlī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was on the battlefield of Karbala. The
bodies of companions and loved ones lay before him, and enemies surrounded him
from all sides. When the time for Ẓuhr prayer arrived, he asked the enemy to
grant him time so that he could perform the prayer.

Abū Ṭalḥah al-Anṣārī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was praying in his orchard, which
was heavy with fruit. His gaze fell upon it, and his heart became momentarily
distracted by its beauty. As soon as he realized this during prayer, he felt
ashamed that worldly wealth had diverted his attention. He went to ʿUthmān ibn
ʿAffān [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}, who was the Caliph at the time, and said that
the orchard which distracted him would be given away in the path of Allah.
\[al-Sunan al-Kubrā\]

Muslims are commanded to perform the prayer themselves and to instruct their
wives and children to pray. Five daily prayers are obligatory during the day and
night: Fajr (dawn), Ẓuhr (midday), ʿAṣr (afternoon), Maghrib (sunset), and
ʿIshāʾ (night).

Rather than praying alone or at home, men should perform prayer in the mosque
with the congregation (jamāʿah). Abū Hurayrah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported
that the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "By the One in Whose Hand is my
soul, I considered ordering firewood to be gathered, then ordering the prayer
and the call to prayer to be made, then appointing a man to lead the people in
prayer, and then going to men and burning their houses over them." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

For women, praying at home is better. Umm Ḥumayd, the wife of Abū Ḥumayd
al-Sāʿidī [رضي الله عنهما]{dir="rtl"}, reported: She came to the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} and said, "O Messenger of Allah, I love to pray with you."

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "I know that you love to pray with me. But your prayer
in your house is better for you than your prayer in your room, and your prayer
in your room is better for you than your prayer in your courtyard, and your
prayer in your courtyard is better for you than your prayer in the mosque of
your people, and your prayer in the mosque of your people is better for you than
your prayer in my mosque (Masjid al-Nabwi)."

She said: So she ordered that a place of prayer be prepared for her in the
furthest and darkest part of her house, and she continued to pray there until
she met Allah. \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} instructed that children should be
commanded to pray from the age of seven, and if they do not, then they must be
instructed firmly by the age of ten. \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\] Good and bad habits
begin forming in this period, so this greatest habit of righteousness should
also be instilled at that time so that it becomes firmly established in
adulthood.

Care must be taken to pray with calmness and tranquility, without haste. The
Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} once said that the greatest thief is the one
who steals from his prayer. The Companions asked, "O Messenger of Allah, how
does one steal from prayer?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} replied, "By not performing the
bowing and prostration properly and by lacking khushūʿ (humble attentiveness)."
\[Musnad Aḥmad\]

One should also not look around during the prayer. Abū Dharr [رضي الله
عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported that the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah
continues to face the servant during his prayer as long as he does not turn
away. But when he turns away, Allah turns away from him." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

## Obligatory Charity (Zakāh)

Allah Says,

[وَأَقِيمُوا۟ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَءَاتُوا۟ ٱلزَّكَوٰةَ وَٱرْكَعُوا۟ مَعَ
ٱلرَّٰكِعِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*Establish the prayer, pay zakāh, and bow down with those who bow down.* \[Sūrat
al-Baqarah, 43\]

It is reported that Zakāh was legislated in 2 AH, approximately eighteen months
after the arrival of the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} in Madīnah.

Zakāh refers to spending a fixed, prescribed portion, once every lunar year,
from wealth that exceeds one's basic needs, on eligible recipients as determined
by Allah. Allah has specified Zakāh recipients or beneficiaries to 8 specific
categories. These are:

1.  The Poor

2.  The Needy

3.  Collectors or Administrators of Zakat

4.  Softening the Hearts

5.  Slaves

6.  Those in Debt

7.  In The Path of Allah

8.  The Stranded Traveller

Zakāh becomes obligatory only when a person's wealth reaches a minimum threshold
known as niṣāb, and a full lunar year (ḥawl) has passed over that wealth.
ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar [رضي الله عنهما]{dir="rtl"} narrated, "Whoever acquires
wealth is not liable to pay Zakāh on it until a year has passed while he remains
in possession of it." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

This threshold of niṣāb was stipulated by the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}.
The niṣāb is calculated based on twenty mithqāl of gold, which is approximately
85--87 grams of gold, or two hundred dirhams of silver, equivalent to
approximately 595 grams of silver.

In practice, the value of the niṣāb is commonly determined by the market value
of approximately 85 grams of gold. Muslims whose wealth or net assets reach this
threshold and remain in their possession for a full lunar year are obligated to
pay Zakāh, which is 2.5% of that qualifying wealth.

Zakāh should not be viewed as a tax, fine, or penalty. Giving Zakāh does not
diminish wealth; rather, it purifies it and brings blessing. The Messenger of
Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that charity purifies wealth, cleansing it of impurity
and increasing its goodness. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also warned that a person who does not
give Zakāh on gold and silver will, on the Day of Resurrection, have plates
fashioned from that wealth, which will be heated and used to brand his forehead
and sides. His wealth will be transformed into a snake that will continue to
bite him. (Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim) Likewise, those who withhold Zakāh on livestock will
find that their animals will trample and gore them on the Day of Resurrection.
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

Beyond Zakāh, a Muslim is encouraged to give charity (ṣadaqah) whenever the
opportunity arises. Technically speaking, Zakāh refers to obligatory charity,
whereas ṣadaqah refers to voluntary charity. Even non-monetary acts, such as
smiling or removing something harmful from the road, are considered ṣadaqah. In
essence, all Zakāh is ṣadaqah, but not all ṣadaqah is Zakāh.

Abū Masʿūd al-Anṣārī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} used to perform manual labor in
the marketplace, and whatever he earned, he would give in charity for the sake
of Allah. Salmān al-Fārsī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was the governor of
al-Madāʾin. He received a stipend of five thousand dinars; whenever he received
it, he would give it away in charity, and he would weave mats with his own hands
to provide for his food and drink.

## Fasting (Ṣiyām)

Allah Says,

[يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ الصِّيَامُ]{dir="rtl"}

*O believers, fasting has been prescribed for you.* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah, 183\]

It is reported that fasting was legislated in 2 AH in the month of Sha'ban. The
Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} fasted 9 complete Ramaḍāns in his lifetime as
per the consensus of the scholars.

In the course of the year, the month of Ramaḍān arrives once. Allah has
Commanded Muslims to observe fasting during this month. Linguistically, Ṣiyām
means to abstain from something, such as abstaining from speaking. Technically,
it means worshipping Allah by abstaining from food, drink, and other actions
which invalidate a person's fast, from true dawn to sunset.

Allah has stated that fasting produces purity. The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Fasting is a shield against the Fire \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\].
The Companions [رضي الله عنهم]{dir="rtl"} were so eager to fast that when they
came to know there was little food in the house, they would say, "I am fasting
today."

ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} reported: The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} came to me one day and said, "Do you have anything (to eat)?" We
said, "No." He said, "Then I am fasting." Then he came to us another day, and we
said, "O Messenger of Allah, some ḥays (a dish of dates, ghee, and cheese) was
gifted to us." He said, "Show it to me, for I began the day fasting," and then
he ate. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The female companions were also so devoted to fasting that it caused difficulty
for their husbands. Eventually, the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
instructed, "A woman should not fast while her husband is present except with
his permission." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "When any one of you is fasting, he
should not engage in obscene speech or ignorant behavior. If someone fights him
or abuses him, let him say: 'I am fasting.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The odor from the mouth of a
fasting person is more pleasing to Allah than the fragrance of musk." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

## Pilgrimage (Ḥajj)

Allah Says,

[وَلِلَّهِ عَلَى ٱلنَّاسِ حِجُّ ٱلْبَيْتِ مَنِ ٱسْتَطَاعَ إِلَيْهِ سَبِيلًۭا
ۚ]{dir="rtl"}

*And Ḥajj (pilgrimage) to this House is an obligation by Allah upon whoever is
able among the people* \[Sūrat Āl ʿImrān, 97\]

In the land of Arabia, there is a city called Makkah. This is the very city in
which our Messenger, Muḥammad [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, was born. In this city stands the
Kaʿbah, which is called the House of Allah. There are many other blessed places
in Makkah as well. Muslims travel there during a designated time and fulfill
certain Commands of Allah---this act is called Ḥajj.

It is reported that Ḥajj was legislated in 9 AH or 10 AH after the Conquest of
Makkah, when idolatry was wiped out, and Islam flourished. The Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} performed one Hajj in 10 AH.

ʿAlī ibn Abī Ṭālib [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported that the Messenger of
Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever possesses provisions and a mount that can
convey him to the House of Allah, yet does not perform Ḥajj, then it makes no
difference whether he dies as a Jew or a Christian." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Perform Ḥajj and ʿUmrah
consecutively, for they remove poverty and sins just as the furnace removes
impurities from iron, gold, and silver." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

Abū Hurayrah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported that the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} was asked: "Which deed is best?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Belief
in Allah and His Messenger." He was asked, "Then what?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
"Jihād in the path of Allah." He was asked, "Then what?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,
"An accepted Ḥajj." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} said: "I said: 'O Messenger of Allah, we see
jihād as the best of deeds. Should we not take part in it?' He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
replied, 'The best jihād for you (women) is an accepted Ḥajj.'" \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever performs Ḥajj and does not
engage in obscenity or commit sin will return (free of sins) as on the day his
mother gave birth to him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

A person upon whom Ḥajj becomes obligatory is one who possesses sufficient
wealth to cover the travel expenses to and from Makkah, along with the necessary
household expenses until his return, and whose route is safe.

Ḥajj, as taught to us by the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, is of three types:

1.  **Ḥajj al-Tamattuʿ:** The word tamattuʿ means to take benefit or advantage.
    A person intending this form of Ḥajj is known as al-Mutamattiʿ. In this form
    of Ḥajj, a pilgrim performs ʿUmrah first, exits iḥrām, and then enters iḥrām
    again for Ḥajj during the same journey. This is the form of Ḥajj that the
    Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} advised his Companions to perform.

2.  **Ḥajj al-Qirān:** The word qirān means to combine. A person intending this
    form of Ḥajj is known as a Qārin. In this form of Ḥajj, a pilgrim performs
    ʿUmrah and Ḥajj together in one iḥrām, without exiting iḥrām until the Day
    of Sacrifice. This is the form of Ḥajj that the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
    himself performed.

3.  **Ḥajj al-Ifrād**: The word ifrād means to do something alone or
    independently. A person intending this form of Ḥajj is known as a Mufrid. In
    this form of Ḥajj, a pilgrim performs only Ḥajj, without performing ʿUmrah
    during the journey. The iḥrām is worn only for Ḥajj.

## Jihād

Allah Says,

[وَجَـٰهِدُوا۟ فِى ٱللَّهِ حَقَّ جِهَادِهِۦ ۚ]{dir="rtl"}

*Strive for (the cause of) Allah in the way He deserves.* \[Sūrat al-Ḥajj, 78\]

The word jihād means striving, exertion, and effort. Any struggle or effort made
in the path of Allah is called jihād. Every person can perform jihād according
to their capacity. This can be understood through the following examples:

- When we restrain our hearts and fulfill Allah's Commands such as establishing
  prayer, fasting, striving to learn the religion of Allah, traveling for
  knowledge, and staying awake at night. This is the jihād of the body.

- When we spend wealth to seek the Pleasure of Allah---caring for orphans,
  widows, the poor, and the needy, and contributing to communal and religious
  causes. This is the jihād of wealth.

- When we act upon Allah's Commands ourselves and also encourage others to do
  so, give sincere advice, command good, and forbid evil through speech and
  writing. This is the jihād of knowledge.

- When the situation demands it, and a person sacrifices his life to raise the
  word of Allah and to defend the religion. This is the jihād of life, also
  known as qitāl (armed combat).

All of these are forms of jihād, and every Muslim should participate in them to
the best of his ability and capacity.

ʿAbdullāh ibn Masʿūd [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} reported: A man came to the
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} (all the way from Yemen) and sought permission to
participate in jihād. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} asked, "Are your parents
alive?" He replied, "Yes." The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Then strive in
their service." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The one who strives to support a widow and a
poor person is like one who performs jihād in the path of Allah --- or like one
who stands in prayer at night and fasts during the day." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The best form of jihād is a word of
truth spoken before an oppressive ruler." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The true mujāhid is the one who
strives against his own soul in obedience to Allah." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Striving against
one's own soul is the foundation of striving against the disbelievers, for
whoever does not strive against his own soul first will not be able to strive
against his external enemy." \[Majmūʿ al-Fatāwā\]

Al-Junayd [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever does not strive against his own
soul in obedience to Allah will never succeed in striving against his enemy."
\[Ḥilyat al-Awliyāʾ\]

Imam Ibn al-Qayyim [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Striving against the soul has
four stages:

1.  Striving to learn guidance,

2.  Striving to act upon it after knowing it,

3.  Striving to call others to it and teach those who are ignorant of it,

4.  Striving to remain patient upon the hardships of calling to Allah and the
    harm caused by people, enduring that for Allah's sake." \[Zād al-Maʿād\]

## Jihād of Combat

Allah Says,

[يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ قَـٰتِلُوا۟ ٱلَّذِينَ يَلُونَكُم مِّنَ
ٱلْكُفَّارِ وَلْيَجِدُوا۟ فِيكُمْ غِلْظَةًۭ ۚ وَٱعْلَمُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ مَعَ
ٱلْمُتَّقِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*O believers! Fight the disbelievers around you and let them find firmness in
you. And know that Allah is with those mindful (of Him)* \[Sūrat al-Tawbah,
9:123\]

In this āyah, Muslims are commanded to fight against disbelievers. This command
applies specifically to those disbelievers who initiate warfare against Muslims,
prevent them from practicing their religion, expel them from their homes, and
with whom Muslims have no treaty or covenant. This form of struggle is called
jihād of combat.

Muslims should possess sufficient strength so that they are not overawed by
others, nor appear weak before them.

One who fights in support of Allah's religion and places his life in danger for
its sake holds a very high rank with Allah. Once, a Companion wished to abandon
the world and seclude himself in worship. When the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} learned of this, he said, "A morning journey in the path of
Allah, or an evening journey, is better than the world and everything in it."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "No servant whose feet become dusty
in the path of Allah will be touched by the Fire." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Standing guard for a day and a
night in the path of Allah is better than fasting a month and standing in prayer
during it. And if he dies, his deeds continue to be recorded for him, his
provision continues, and he is protected from the trial of the grave." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim}

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "In Paradise there are one hundred
levels which Allah has prepared for those who strive in His path. The distance
between every two levels is like the distance between the heaven and the earth."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

**The Qurʾānic Stages of Legislation Regarding Fighting**

After the Hijrah of the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} to Madīnah, the legislation
concerning fighting in the path of Allah was revealed gradually, in distinct
stages.

**First Stage: Permission to Fight**

Imam al-Zuhrī [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} stated that the first Qurʾānic āyah
revealed regarding fighting after the Hijrah was:

[أُذِنَ لِلَّذِينَ يُقَاتَلُونَ بِأَنَّهُمْ ظُلِمُوا ۚ وَإِنَّ اللَّهَ عَلَىٰ
نَصْرِهِمْ لَقَدِيرٌ]{dir="rtl"}

*"Permission (to fight back) is granted to those who are being fought, because
they have been wronged. And indeed, Allah is Most Capable of granting them
victory."* \[Sūrat al-Ḥajj, 39\]

Imam Aḥmad [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} recorded from Ibn ʿAbbās [رضي الله
عنه]{dir="rtl"} that he said, "This was the first āyah revealed concerning
fighting."

This stage established permission, not obligation, as a response to oppression.

**Second Stage: Command to Fight Those Who Initiate Hostility**

This was followed by a command restricting fighting only to aggressors, with a
strict prohibition against transgression. Allah Says:

[وَقَاتِلُوا فِي سَبِيلِ اللَّهِ الَّذِينَ يُقَاتِلُونَكُمْ وَلَا تَعْتَدُوا ۚ
إِنَّ اللَّهَ لَا يُحِبُّ الْمُعْتَدِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*Fight in the cause of Allah those who fight you, but do not transgress. Indeed,
Allah does not love the transgressors.* \[Sūrat al-Baqarah, 190\]

This āyah established ethical limits and moral restraint in warfare. Allah\'s
Messenger [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} used to fight only those who fought him and avoid
non-combatants.

**Third Stage: General Command in the Context of Collective Hostility**

Later, a more general command was revealed addressing organized aggression
against the Muslim community:

[وَقَاتِلُوا الْمُشْرِكِينَ كَافَّةً كَمَا يُقَاتِلُونَكُمْ كَافَّةً ۚ
وَاعْلَمُوا أَنَّ اللَّهَ مَعَ الْمُتَّقِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*Fight the polytheists collectively as they fight you collectively, and know
that Allah is with those who are mindful of Him.* \[Sūrat at-Tawbah, 36\]

This āyah clarified the collective nature of defense when the Muslim community
is confronted as a whole.

All military expeditions of the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} occurred after
the Hijrah, within a span of ten years. Zayd ibn Arqam [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"}
narrated, "The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} fought nineteen battles and performed only
one ḥajj after migrating to Madinah, which was Ḥajj al-Wadāʿ." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

Buraydah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} narrated, "The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} conducted nineteen military expeditions, and he personally fought
in eight of them." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever sincerely asks Allah for martyrdom,
Allah will cause him to reach the ranks of the martyrs, even if he dies on his
bed." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

**Dispelling A Misconception**

A few āyahs are frequently misquoted to suggest that Islam commands the killing
of all disbelievers, "Kill them wherever you find them" (Sūrat al-Baqarah, 191)
and "When the sacred months have passed, then fight the polytheists wherever you
find them..." (Sūrat al-Tawbah, 5)

This leads some to falsely assume that Islam permits the indiscriminate killing
of disbelievers. This understanding is incorrect and ignores the Qur'anic
context, prophetic practice, and scholarly consensus.

**1. The Āyah Is Context-Specific**

This āyah was revealed in the context of armed conflict with the Quraysh, who
initiated hostilities, persecuted Muslims, expelled them from Makkah, and
violated treaties. The āyah is not a general command, but a wartime directive
addressing an active battlefield situation.

**2. Restricted by the Very Next Āyat**

The same passages in both instances immediately state:

"Fight in the way of Allah those who fight you, but do not transgress" (Sūrat
al-Baqarah, 190) and "If they cease, then there is no aggression except against
the oppressors" (Sūrat al-Baqarah, 193). This clearly limits fighting to
combatants, prohibits transgression, and mandates cessation when hostility ends.

And likewise, "And if any one of the polytheists seeks your protection, then
grant him protection so that he may hear the words of Allah. Then escort him to
a place of safety..." (Sūrat al-Tawbah, 6) This establishes that even enemy
combatants were not to be harmed if they sought protection.

**3. Confirmed by the Sunnah**

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} never killed non-combatants, explicitly forbade
killing women, children, monks, and civilians, and lived peacefully with
non-Muslims under treaties and covenants. If the āyah meant unrestricted
killing, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} would have implemented it that way, which he
categorically did not.

Any reading that strips these āyahs of their context is textually false, legally
invalid, and theologically dangerous.
