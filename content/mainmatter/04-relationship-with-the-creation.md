
# Relationship With The Creation

## Enjoining Good and Forbidding Evil

Allah Says,

[أَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ وَأْمُرْ بِٱلْمَعْرُوفِ وَٱنْهَ عَنِ ٱلْمُنكَرِ وَٱصْبِرْ
عَلَىٰ مَآ أَصَابَكَ ۖ إِنَّ ذَٰلِكَ مِنْ عَزْمِ ٱلْأُمُورِ]{dir="rtl"}

*Establish prayer, encourage what is good and forbid what is evil, and endure
patiently whatever befalls you. Surely this is a resolve to aspire to.* \[Sūrat
Luqmān, 17\]

Whatever Allah has commanded is good, and whatever He has prohibited is evil.
Therefore, it is upon all of us to encourage what is good and prevent what is
wrong. If, through us, people begin to do good or refrain from evil, then we
will receive a reward equal to theirs. Enjoining good and forbidding evil is
itself a great act of righteousness.

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "The one who enjoins
good and forbids evil requires three qualities: knowledge before it, gentleness
during it, and patience after it." \[al-Amr bil-Maʿrūf wa-n-Nahy ʿan al-Munkar\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Your enjoining what is right and forbidding
what is wrong is charity for you." \[Jāmiʿ al-Tirmidhī\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever among you sees an evil, let
him change it with his hand. If he cannot, then with his tongue. If he cannot,
then with his heart---and that is the weakest level of faith." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "When a sin is committed on earth,
the one who witnesses it and hates it is like one who was absent, and the one
who was absent but approves of it is like one who witnessed it." \[Sunan Abī
Dāwūd\]

If people fail to enjoin good and forbid evil, they will not be spared from
collective punishment. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} illustrated this with an
example:

"The example of those who uphold the limits of Allah and those who violate them
is like people who draw lots for seats on a ship. Some take the upper deck and
others the lower deck. When those below need water, they pass by those above, so
they say: 'If we make a hole in our portion, we will not harm those above us.'
If they are left to do what they intend, all will perish. But if they are
restrained, all will be saved." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} warned that those who refrain from forbidding evil
out of fear of powerful or wealthy people will be questioned by Allah, for true
fear should be directed toward Him alone. The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
said, "Let not the fear of people prevent any one of you from speaking the truth
when he knows it." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

If people abandon the duty of commanding good and forbidding evil, then
widespread corruption leads to collective consequences. The Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "By the One in Whose Hand is my soul, you must enjoin good
and forbid evil, or Allah will soon send a punishment upon you from Him. Then
you will supplicate to Him, but He will not respond to you." \[Jāmiʿ
al-Tirmidhī\]

Allah Says,

[وَٱتَّقُوا۟ فِتْنَةًۭ لَّا تُصِيبَنَّ ٱلَّذِينَ ظَلَمُوا۟ مِنكُمْ خَآصَّةًۭ ۖ
وَٱعْلَمُوٓا۟ أَنَّ ٱللَّهَ شَدِيدُ ٱلْعِقَابِ]{dir="rtl"}

Beware of a trial that will not only affect the wrongdoers among you. And know
that Allah is severe in punishment. \[Sūrat al-Anfāl, 25\]

This means that when wrongdoing spreads, and the righteous remain silent,
punishment may encompass both the wrongdoers and those who failed to oppose
them.

Shaykh al-Islām Ibn Taymiyyah [رحمه الله]{dir="rtl"} said, "Rather, the religion
is built upon enjoining what is good and forbidding what is evil, and the two
are inseparable. Evil must not be forbidden except alongside calling to a good
that replaces it, just as Allah, Exalted is He, commanded that He be worshipped
and forbade the worship of anything besides Him. The foundation of all of this
is the testimony that none has the right to be worshipped except Allah.

The human soul was created to act, not merely to abstain. Abstention is only
sought for the sake of what it leads to. If a person does not occupy himself
with righteous action, he will not truly abandon evil deeds---except in a
deficient manner. Indeed, many prohibited matters are only forbidden because
they corrupt and undermine righteous deeds, so they are prohibited in order to
safeguard the soundness of righteous actions." \[Iqtiḍāʾ aṣ-Ṣirāṭ al-Mustaqīm\]

## The Method of Daʿwah (Calling to Allah)

Allah Says,

[ٱدْعُ إِلَىٰ سَبِيلِ رَبِّكَ بِٱلْحِكْمَةِ وَٱلْمَوْعِظَةِ ٱلْحَسَنَةِ
وَجَٰدِلْهُم بِٱلَّتِى هِىَ أَحْسَنُ]{dir="rtl"}

*"Call to the way of your Lord with wisdom and good instruction, and argue with
them in the best manner."* \[Sūrat al-Naḥl, 125\]

This āyah teaches Muslims that people should be invited to the religion of Allah
in three ways:

1.  Wisdom (Hikmah)

2.  Good counsel (mawʿiẓah ḥasanah)

3.  Reasoned debate in the best manner

When presenting a new idea, people generally adopt one of three approaches:

1.  They present convincing, thoughtful arguments.

2.  They offer sincere advice that touches the heart.

3.  They refute opposing arguments in a dignified way.

The first is wisdom, the second is good counsel, and the third is refined
debate.

Islam teaches that there is no compulsion in religion. Faith is a matter of
conviction, and conviction cannot be forced into anyone's heart. Reflect upon
this: who among people possessed stronger faith and Islam than the Companions?
Not a single Companion was forced into Islam. All of them accepted faith after
witnessing the noble character of the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} and
hearing the truth with their own ears.

Even today, you can invite people to Allah's religion in the same way.

When the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} sent Muʿādh ibn Jabal and Abū Mūsā
al-Ashʿarī [رضي الله عنهما]{dir="rtl"} to Yemen to call people to Islam, he
advised them at the time of departure: "Make things easy; do not make them
difficult. Give glad tidings; do not drive people away." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## Practice What You Preach

Allah Says,

[لِمَ تَقُولُونَ مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ - كَبُرَ مَقْتًا عِندَ ٱللَّهِ أَن
تَقُولُوا۟ مَا لَا تَفْعَلُونَ]{dir="rtl"}

*Why do you say what you do not do? It is greatly hateful to Allah that you say
what you do not do.* \[Sūrat al-Ṣaff, 2-3\]

If you do not act upon what you say, your words will have no effect on others.
Allah further says: Do you command people to righteousness while forgetting your
own selves?

This means that whatever a person says with his tongue, he must also act upon it
himself. Otherwise, his words will have no effect on people.

Whatever the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} instructed others to do, he
practiced it himself first. He encouraged people to help the poor and needy, and
he himself set the example by helping them. He advised forgiveness, and he
forgave even those who poisoned him, those who shot arrows at him, and those who
attacked him with swords---yet he never raised his hand against them.

In short, when you advise others, do not forget yourself.

## After Conveying the Truth, Another's Misguidance Will Not Harm You

Allah Says,

[يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ عَلَيْكُمْ أَنفُسَكُمْ لَا يَضُرُّكُم مَّن
ضَلَّ إِذَا ٱهْتَدَيْتُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*O believers, take care of your own souls. If you are rightly guided, those who
go astray will not harm you.* \[Sūrat al-Māʾidah, 105\]

The meaning of this āyah is that a person's responsibility is to convey the
truth. Forcing others to accept it is not his duty. Once a person has fulfilled
the obligation of delivering the truth sincerely, the responsibility is lifted
from him.

Abū Bakr al-Ṣiddīq [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} once recited this āyah and said to
the people, "O people, do not misunderstand this āyah by its outward meaning. I
heard the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} say that when people see an
oppressor committing oppression and do not restrain him, it is possible that
Allah will seize all of them with punishment." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Abū Thaʿlabah al-Khashanī [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} asked the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} about the meaning of this āyah. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
replied, "Command one another to do good and forbid one another from evil. But
when you see greed being obeyed, miserliness being followed, worldly life being
preferred over religion, and every person admiring his own opinion, then take
care of yourself and leave the common people. After you will come a time in
which holding firmly to the religion will be like holding a burning coal in
one's hand." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

## Conduct Towards Parents 

Allah Says,

[وَبِٱلْوَٰلِدَيْنِ إِحْسَانًا]{dir="rtl"}

*And show kindness to parents.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 36\]

Allah has commanded that parents be treated with honor, service, and obedience.
It is necessary to obey them in everything except that which involves
disobedience to Allah.

Among the parents, the mother has the greatest right.

A man once asked the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"},

"Who is most deserving of my good companionship?" He replied, "Your mother."

The man asked, "Then who?" He replied, "Your mother."

He asked again, "Then who?" He replied, "Your mother."

When he asked the fourth time, he replied, "Your father." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Allah says in the Qur'an that you must treat your parents with goodness. If one
or both of them reach old age in your presence, do not even say 'uff' to them,
do not scold them, and speak to them with respect. Obey them, and say, "My Lord,
have mercy upon them, just as they raised me when I was small." \[Sūrat
al-Isrāʾ, 23--24\]

A man asked the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, "What right do parents have
over their children?" He replied, "They are your Paradise and your Hellfire."
\[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

Meaning: obedience to them leads to Paradise, and disobedience leads to Hell.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "May he be humiliated! May he be
humiliated! May he be humiliated!" It was said, "Who, O Messenger of Allah?" He
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The one who finds his parents in old age, one or both of
them, and does not enter Paradise (by serving them)." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

If parents are firm upon the religion, they should be followed. If they are
mistaken, they should be guided politely and respectfully.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} presented the true religion to his uncle,
and Prophet Ibrāhīm [عليه السلام]{dir="rtl"} presented the truth to his father.
One should always continue supplicating for the forgiveness of one's parents.

## Conduct Towards Children 

Allah Says,

[يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ قُوا۟ أَنفُسَكُمْ وَأَهْلِيكُمْ
نَارًا]{dir="rtl"}

*O believers, protect yourselves and your families from a Fire.* \[Sūrat
al-Taḥrīm, 6\]

Just as parents have rights over their children, children also have rights over
their parents.

In this āyah, Allah commands that you protect your households from the
Fire---meaning that children must be prevented from actions that ruin their
religion and worldly life.

Proper education and righteous upbringing are essential. True love is not found
in wealth, food, clothing, jewelry, or large houses. True love lies in
protecting one's family from Allah's anger and saving them from becoming fuel
for Hellfire.

Allah says in the Qur'an that righteous servants not only supplicate for their
parents, but also for their children:

[وَأَصْلِحْ لِي فِي ذُرِّيَّتِي]{dir="rtl"}

*And grant righteousness to my offspring.* \[Sūrat al-Aḥqāf, 15\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "There is no gift a father gives his
child greater than good manners and proper discipline." \[Sunan al-Tirmidhī\]

## Conduct of a Wife Toward Her Husband

Allah Says,

[فَٱلصَّـٰلِحَـٰتُ قَـٰنِتَـٰتٌ حَـٰفِظَـٰتٌ لِّلْغَيْبِ]{dir="rtl"}

*Righteous women are obedient and guard what Allah has entrusted them with in
the absence of their husbands.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 34\]

This āyah teaches us that righteous wives are obedient to their husbands and
protect their honor, dignity, and property in their absence.

Just as women have rights over men, men also have rights over women.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said that after taqwā, there is no
blessing greater for a man than a righteous wife---one who obeys him when he
commands, pleases him when he looks at her, fulfills an oath when he places one
upon her, and safeguards herself and his wealth in his absence. \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A woman is a guardian over her husband's house
and she will be questioned about her responsibility." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "If I were to command anyone to prostrate to
someone other than Allah, I would have commanded a wife to prostrate to her
husband." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

The female Companions took great care in serving their husbands, honoring them,
and safeguarding their property. ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} used to
wash the Prophet's [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} clothes with her own hands, prepare his
tooth-stick, and apply fragrance to him. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Once, a poor trader asked Asmāʾ bint Abī Bakr [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} for
permission to sell goods in the shade of her wall. She wished to permit him but
did not want to do so without her husband al-Zubayr's consent. She told the man
to return when al-Zubayr was present. When he came again and sought permission,
al-Zubayr objected at first, then relented. Once Asmāʾ realized her husband's
approval, she granted permission. \[al-Adab al-Mufrad\]

On another occasion, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} noticed silver rings in
ʿĀʾishah's hand and asked what they were. She replied, "I made them to beautify
myself for you." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

## Conduct of a Husband Toward His Wife

Allah Says,

[وَعَاشِرُوهُنَّ بِالْمَعْرُوفِ]{dir="rtl"}

*Live with them in kindness.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 19\]

Allah has commanded that women be treated with kindness. Kind treatment means
that no form of oppression or injustice should be committed against them.

Women are an entrustment from Allah. Their religious education and moral
upbringing must be ensured. They should be taught how to become righteous
mothers, good wives, and devoted servants of Allah. They must not be allowed to
follow paths that destroy their worldly life and ruin their Hereafter.

Allah has granted women specific rights in marriage, divorce, inheritance, and
daily life---none of these rights may be reduced or denied.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The best among you are those who
are best to their wives." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A man is a guardian over his household, and he will be
questioned about it." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "Do not hate your wife if you notice a fault in
her---perhaps you will find another quality in her that pleases you." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim\]

Whatever a man eats, he should feed his wife the same. Whatever he wears, he
should clothe her likewise. Women should be regarded as one's own selves.
Whatever one likes for oneself should be liked for them as well. \[Sunan Abī
Dāwūd\]

Women are generally tender-hearted, and for this reason, the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} showed them special care. Once, during a journey, the wives of
the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} were riding camels that were moving quickly. The
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said to Anjashah, the Abyssinian servant, "Be gentle---do
not break the glass vessels." (meaning the women) \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Companions showed deep affection toward their wives. Once, Ḥasan ibn ʿAlī
[رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} divorced his wife for a specific reason and sent her
the full dowry (mahr). Upon seeing it, she wept and said, "This is insignificant
compensation for separation from a beloved companion." When Ḥasan heard this, he
too wept uncontrollably. \[al-Iṣābah\]

## Conduct with Relatives

Allah Says,

[وَبِذِي الْقُرْبَىٰ]{dir="rtl"}

*And show kindness to relatives.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 36\]

After parents, relatives have the greatest right. Every possible kindness should
be shown to them.

A man once asked the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} to tell him something
that would lead him to Paradise. He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Worship Allah without
associating partners with Him, establish prayer, give zakāh, and uphold ties of
kinship." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever wishes for expansion in provision and increase
in lifespan should maintain family ties." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "The one who truly upholds kinship is not the one
who reciprocates kindness, but the one who maintains ties even when relatives
cut them off." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Companions were exemplary in their conduct toward relatives.

## Conduct with Orphans

Allah Says,

[وَالْيَتَامَىٰ]{dir="rtl"}

*And show kindness to orphans.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 36\]

An orphan is a child whose father has passed away. Allah commands that orphans
be treated with kindness and not oppressed or subdued in a manner that stunts
their growth and well-being.

Allah Says:

[فَأَمَّا الْيَتِيمَ فَلَا تَقْهَرْ]{dir="rtl"}

*So do not oppress the orphan.* \[Sūrat al-Ḍuḥā, 9\]

Those who mistreat orphans should reflect: if they were to die, leaving behind
young children, how would they feel if others treated those children the same
way?

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The best Muslim home is one in
which an orphan is treated kindly, and the worst Muslim home is one in which an
orphan is mistreated." \[Sunan Ibn Mājah\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "I and the one who sponsors an orphan will be in
Paradise like this," and he joined his index and middle fingers. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} would never eat unless an orphan
was present with him. \[al-Adab al-Mufrad\]

ʿĀʾishah [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} took orphan girls from her family and the
Anṣār into her home and raised them with great care. \[al-Adab al-Mufrad\]

Orphans' wealth must not be wasted or misused. It may be spent only where it
benefits them. ʿĀʾishah used to invest orphans' wealth in trade so it would
grow.

Until orphans reach maturity and sound judgment, their wealth should not be
handed over to them. Once they are able to distinguish benefit from harm, their
wealth should be returned to them.

## Conduct Toward the Poor and Needy

Allah Says,

[وَالْمَسَاكِينِ]{dir="rtl"}

*And show kindness to the needy.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 36\]

Showing kindness to the poor and fulfilling their needs is a right upon Muslims.

One should not become arrogant due to wealth nor behave negligently toward those
in need, assuming that one will never require help.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever fulfills the need of his
brother, Allah will fulfill his need." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

"Whoever relieves a Muslim of a hardship, Allah will relieve him of a hardship
on the Day of Resurrection." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Companions actively sought out the needy. When al-Zubayr [رضي الله
عنه]{dir="rtl"} passed away, he left behind a massive debt. His son ʿAbdullāh
worried about repayment. Ḥakīm ibn Ḥizām offered help if needed. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿUmar would rarely eat without a poor person present. \[al-Adab
al-Mufrad\]

When Ḥārithah lost his eyesight, he tied a rope from his basket to the door.
When a poor person arrived, he would take dates from the basket and follow the
rope to give them.

If someone asks for help---whether physical, financial, or intellectual---the
request should not be harshly rejected. If assistance is possible, it should be
given; if not, one should still respond gently.

Allah Says:

[وَأَمَّا السَّائِلَ فَلَا تَنْهَرْ]{dir="rtl"}

*As for the one who asks, do not repel him.* \[Sūrat al-Ḍuḥā, 10\]

Helping can also be done by recommending someone else. Allah says that whoever
intercedes for a good cause will share in its reward, and whoever intercedes for
an evil cause will share in its sin.

When someone came to the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} seeking help, he would say to
the Companions: "Intercede, and you will be rewarded; and Allah decrees upon the
tongue of His Prophet whatever He wills." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## Conduct with Neighbors

Allah Says,

[وَالْجَارِ ذِي الْقُرْبَىٰ وَالْجَارِ الْجُنُبِ وَالصَّاحِبِ
بِالْجَنبِ]{dir="rtl"}

*And show kindness to the near neighbor, the distant neighbor, and the companion
at your side.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 36\]

The rights of neighbors are immense in Islam. A neighbor is anyone who lives
nearby. Islam places strong emphasis on good treatment of neighbors.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever believes in Allah and the
Last Day should honor his neighbor." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said,"By Allah, he is not a believer! By Allah, he is
not a believer! By Allah, he is not a believer!"

It was said, "Who, O Messenger of Allah?" He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The one whose
neighbor is not safe from his harm." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "He is not a believer who sleeps full while his
neighbor is hungry beside him." \[al-Adab al-Mufrad\]

This āyah mentions two types of neighbors: the near neighbor and the distant
neighbor. Some scholars explain that the near neighbor refers to a relative who
is also a neighbor, and the distant neighbor refers to one who is not a
relative. Others say the near neighbor is one who shares the same faith, and the
distant neighbor is one of another faith.

Even neighbors of other religions must be treated with kindness. Once, ʿAbdullāh
ibn ʿUmar [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} slaughtered a goat and asked his household
whether they had sent some to his Jewish neighbor. He said that he heard the
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} say, "Jibrīl kept advising me regarding the neighbor
until I thought he would make him an heir." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

In addition to these, there is another category that is not commonly called a
neighbor but is treated as such due to constant association. The Qurʾān refers
to them as "the companion at one's side," such as a travel companion, a
colleague, or a business partner. All such people must also be treated with
kindness.

## Conduct with Travelers and Guests (Ādāb al-Ḍuyūf wa Ibn al-Sabīl)

Allah Says,

[وَابْنِ السَّبِيلِ]{dir="rtl"}

*And show kindness to the traveler.* \[Sūrat al-Nisāʾ, 36\]

Travelers are those who leave behind their families, relatives, friends, and
wealth to journey. At times, they may have no food to eat, no water to drink, no
bed to sleep on, no covering to keep warm, and no money to continue their
travel. Showing kindness to them and caring for their comfort is a duty upon
Muslims.

Whether a traveler is a guest or not, rich or poor, kindness must be shown. Umm
Sharīk [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} was a wealthy Companion who turned her home
into a guesthouse. Many visitors who came to the Messenger of Allah
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} would stay at her home.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever believes in Allah and the Last Day
should honor his guest." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

In a ḥadīth, it is reported that the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} visited
ʿAbdullāh ibn ʿAmr [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} and said, "Have I not been informed
that you pray all night and fast every day?" He replied, "Yes."

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Do not do that. Pray and sleep, fast and break
your fast. Your body has a right over you, your eyes have a right over you, your
guest has a right over you, and your wife has a right over you." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "Hospitality is for one day and one night, and
hosting a guest is for three days. Anything beyond that is charity." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

## Conduct with Widows (Ādāb al-Arāmil)

Allah Says,

[وَأَنكِحُوا الْأَيَامَىٰ مِنكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*And marry off the unmarried among you.* \[Sūrat al-Nūr, 32\]

Widows are women whose husbands have passed away. They are often left without
support for food, clothing, shelter, and protection. Islam commands that they be
treated with kindness.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The one who strives on behalf of
widows and the poor is like one who strives in the path of Allah." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "He is like one who fasts by day and stands in prayer by
night." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Some ignorant people consider widows unlucky---this is contrary to Islamic
teachings.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "On the Day of Resurrection, I will be the
first to open the gate of Paradise, and I will see a woman rushing ahead of me."

When asked who she was, he said, "She is a widow who endured patiently while
caring for her orphaned children." \[Musnad Aḥmad\]

Proper conduct toward widows includes arranging suitable marriages for them
after their waiting period (ʿiddah).

A key duty toward widows is to arrange their remarriage after their waiting
period, with a suitable man. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} married Khadījah [رضي
الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} when he was twenty-five, and she was a forty-year-old
widow. Later, he married several widows.

## Conduct with Daughters

Allah Says,

[وَإِذَا بُشِّرَ أَحَدُهُم بِالْأُنثَىٰ ظَلَّ وَجْهُهُ مُسْوَدًّا وَهُوَ
كَظِيمٌ]{dir="rtl"}

*When one of them is given the news of a female child, his face darkens and he
suppresses his anger.* \[Sūrat al-Naḥl, 58\]

It is commonly observed that when a girl is born instead of a boy, people do not
celebrate as much and often show sorrow. This was the condition of the Arabs
before Islam, who would even bury their daughters alive.

A Companion once narrated to the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} that before Islam, he
had a daughter. When he would call her, she would run joyfully toward him. One
day, he led her to a well and threw her into it while she cried out, "Father!
Father!"

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} wept so intensely upon hearing this that
his beard became wet with tears. He then said, "Go. Islam has erased what came
before it."

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Allah has forbidden disobedience to mothers
and the burying of daughters alive." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} did not merely forbid killing daughters, he elevated their
status.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Whoever raises two daughters until they reach
maturity will come on the Day of Resurrection with me like this," and he joined
his fingers. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Companions competed with one another in caring for daughters. When the
Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} intended to leave Makkah, Ḥamzah's orphaned daughter ran
toward him crying, "Uncle! Uncle!" ʿAlī lifted her up and gave her to Fāṭimah.
Jaʿfar and Zayd also laid claim to her guardianship.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} ruled, "The maternal aunt is like the mother," and
placed the child in her care. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

## Conduct with the Sick

Allah Says,

[وَلَا عَلَى الْمَرِيضِ حَرَجٌ]{dir="rtl"}

*And there is no hardship upon the sick.* \[Sūrat al-Nūr, 61\]

Allah has granted great concessions to the sick. If water may cause harm,
ablution is waived. Jihād is not obligatory for them. Fasting may be postponed.
If unable to pray standing, they may pray sitting, and if unable even to sit,
they may pray lying down.

Just as Allah has shown leniency to the sick in His commands, He has ordered His
servants to show kindness to them.

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A Muslim has five rights over
another Muslim---one of them is that he visits him when he is sick." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "When a Muslim visits his sick brother in the
morning, seventy thousand angels send blessings upon him until evening; and if
he visits him in the evening, seventy thousand angels send blessings upon him
until morning." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also said, "When someone visits a sick person, he continues
harvesting the fruits of Paradise until he returns." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

In a sacred narration, Allah will say on the Day of Judgment, "O son of Adam, I
was sick and you did not visit Me." The servant will reply, "O Lord, how could I
visit You when You are the Lord of the worlds?"

Allah will say, "Did you not know that My servant was sick and you did not visit
him? Had you visited him, you would have found Me with him." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

When Saʿd ibn Muʿādh [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was wounded, the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} ordered a tent erected in the mosque so he could frequently visit
him. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} also visited sick Jews and hypocrites, showing that
visiting the sick is permitted even for non-Muslims.

## Conduct Toward Muslims in General

Allah Says,

[إِنَّمَا الْمُؤْمِنُونَ إِخْوَةٌ]{dir="rtl"}

*Indeed, the believers are brothers.* \[Sūrat al-Ḥujurāt, 10\]

Every Muslim is bound to another Muslim by a great bond---the bond of faith. No
matter how severe an enemy a person may have been, once he believes in Allah and
His Messenger, he becomes a religious brother.

Allah says:

[فَإِن تَابُوا وَأَقَامُوا الصَّلَاةَ وَآتَوُا الزَّكَاةَ فَإِخْوَانُكُمْ فِي
الدِّينِ]{dir="rtl"}

*If they repent, establish prayer, and give zakāh, then they are your brothers
in faith.* \[Sūrat al-Tawbah, 11\]

The Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "The believers, in their mutual
love, mercy, and compassion, are like a single body: if one part suffers, the
whole body responds with sleeplessness and fever." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "A Muslim is the brother of another Muslim. He does not
oppress him, abandon him, or humiliate him. It is sufficient evil for a person
to look down upon his Muslim brother. The blood, wealth, and honor of a Muslim
are sacred to another Muslim." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "It is not permissible for a Muslim to boycott his
brother for more than three days. When they meet, one turns away and the other
turns away. The better of the two is the one who initiates the greeting of
peace." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Every Muslim has five rights over another Muslim:
responding to the greeting, visiting the sick, accepting invitations, saying
'may Allah have mercy on you' when he sneezes, and accompanying the funeral."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} warned: "Whoever swears falsely to seize the right of a
Muslim, Allah will make Hellfire obligatory for him and Paradise forbidden."
\[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

The summary of all this is that just as a person safeguards his own life,
wealth, honor, and benefit, he must do the same for his fellow Muslim.

## Conduct Toward Animals

Allah Says,

[وَمَا مِن دَابَّةٍ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَلَا طَائِرٍ يَطِيرُ بِجَنَاحَيْهِ إِلَّا
أُمَمٌ أَمْثَالُكُمْ]{dir="rtl"}

*There is no creature on the earth, nor any bird that flies with its wings,
except that they are communities like you.* \[Sūrat al-Anʿām, 38\]

Just as we do not wish harm for one another and care for each other's comfort,
we must also treat animals with gentleness.

Once some people came to ʿUbaydullāh and ʿAbdullāh and asked whether they had
heard anything from the Messenger of Allah [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} regarding a man who
rides a horse and beats it excessively. They replied that they had not. Their
elder sister then said from inside the house: Allah Himself says:

"There is no creature on the earth nor bird that flies with its wings except
that they are communities like you." \[Sūrat al-Anʿām, 38\]

A Companion asked the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}, "O Messenger of Allah, I have made
water troughs for my animals. If stray camels drink from them, will I receive
reward?"

He replied, "There is reward in showing kindness to every living being." \[Ṣaḥīḥ
al-Bukhārī\]

Once during a journey, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} saw that someone had lit a
fire near an ant nest. He asked who had done it. He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} then said,
"It is not befitting that anyone should punish with fire except the Lord of the
Fire." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\] When the man replied, the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}
said, "Extinguish it, extinguish it," so that the ants would not be harmed.

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said regarding a woman, "She was punished because she
imprisoned a cat---neither feeding it nor allowing it to eat from the earth,
until it died." \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} warned that killing animals without need is a grave sin, and
that Allah will question a person who kills an animal unjustly. The Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "No person kills a bird or anything larger without a just
cause except that Allah will question him about it." \[Sunan al-Nasā'ī\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} commanded excellence even in slaughter, saying, "Allah has
prescribed excellence in all things. If you kill, kill well; and if you
slaughter, slaughter well. Let one of you sharpen his blade and give comfort to
the animal." \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} forbade animal fighting, striking animals on the face, and
branding them. \[Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim\]

He [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Fear Allah regarding these mute creatures. Ride them
when they are fit, and eat them when they are fit." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

## Conduct Toward Non-Muslims

Allah Says,

[قُل لِّلَّذِينَ آمَنُوا يَغْفِرُوا لِلَّذِينَ لَا يَرْجُونَ أَيَّامَ
اللَّهِ]{dir="rtl"}

*Say to those who believe that they should forgive those who do not hope for the
Days of Allah.* \[Sūrat al-Jāthiyah, 14\]

Despite inward religious distinction, Muslims are commanded to deal with
non-Muslims with forgiveness, justice, and good character in worldly and moral
dealings.

Even in warfare, Allah Commands:

[وَإِنْ أَحَدٌ مِّنَ الْمُشْرِكِينَ اسْتَجَارَكَ فَأَجِرْهُ]{dir="rtl"}

*If any of the polytheists seeks your protection, then grant him protection.*
\[Sūrat al-Tawbah, 6\]

The Qurʾān also commands good conduct toward parents even if they are
polytheists.

Fulfilling treaties with non-Muslims is described as a sign of piety:

[فَأَتِمُّوا إِلَيْهِمْ عَهْدَهُمْ إِلَىٰ مُدَّتِهِمْ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُحِبُّ
الْمُتَّقِينَ]{dir="rtl"}

*So fulfill their treaty until its term. Indeed, Allah loves the God-fearing.*
\[Sūrat al-Tawbah, 4\]

The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} said, "Show mercy to those on earth, and the One
above the heavens will show mercy to you." \[Sunan Abī Dāwūd\]

Kindness is not restricted to Muslims alone.

Ṣafiyyah [رضي الله عنها]{dir="rtl"} gave charity worth one thousand to her
Jewish relatives.

Abū Maysarah, ʿAmr ibn Maymūn, and ʿAmr ibn Sharjīl gave ṣadaqat al-fiṭr to
Christian monks.

ʿUmar [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} sent a gift to his polytheist brother during the
Prophet's [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} lifetime. \[Ṣaḥīḥ al-Bukhārī\]

Abū Baṣrah al-Ghifārī narrated that when he was still a disbeliever, he stayed
as a guest with the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} and drank the milk of all the goats
of the household. The Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} did not rebuke him, and the
household remained hungry that night.

The mother of Abū Hurayrah [رضي الله عنه]{dir="rtl"} was a disbeliever and used
to insult the Prophet [ﷺ]{dir="rtl"}. When Abū Hurayrah complained, the Prophet
[ﷺ]{dir="rtl"} raised his hands in supplication instead of anger. \[Ṣaḥīḥ
Muslim\]
